## Hunt Overview

**Theme:** Escape from Zyzzlvaria. Teams received a board game at the fictional "ZyzzlCon 3009 convention" and were challenged to locate the Covertly Operational Inversion Node (COIN). The winning team, Beginner's Luck, activated the COIN at 3:03 AM Monday.

## ROUND 1: Planet Pluto ($1.00 each)

### Downsized Plutonians
- **Authors:** Ian Tullis and Dan Katz
- **Answer:** EMIGRES
- **Type:** Wordplay/character names
- **Mechanic:** 21 character names each contain two embedded Snow White dwarf names. All possible dwarf-pair combinations are used. Each group of names sharing a dwarf has one letter in common; the letters per dwarf spell EMIGRES. Additional constraint: the two extracted letters always appear in the order they appear in EMIGRES.

### Charity Work
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** AMERICARES
- **Type:** Sequential instruction/paperclip manipulation puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Nine phases of Plutonian repositioning and colored paperclip movement. Intermediate answers: REAPER, CLUE, WHAT, GARTER, ANDY DICK, CHEESE, TREE, LAST. Final step: overlapping six character names (Calli, Lina, Name, Meri, Erica, Ares) spells AMERICARES.

### Plutonic Solids
- **Authors:** Zack Butler, Brie Frame, and Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** IN POSITION
- **Type:** Physical construction / cryptographic geometry
- **Mechanic:** Five pages, each foldable into a Platonic solid. Each shape uses a distinct cipher to reference MIT buildings. Position each shape so labeled faces point toward their campus locations; blank face indicates what it points at. Circled letters from each solid, read in increasing-faces order, spell IN POSITION.

### The Plutonian Transport Agency
- **Author:** Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** JEEPSTER
- **Type:** Logic puzzle / word acrostic
- **Mechanic:** 20 cars parked in Cambridge street cleaning zones. Identify each car model from images, determine Cambridge cleaning schedule for August 2008, arrange cars by towing date, take first letters to spell "CAR THAT IS A T REX SINGLE" = JEEPSTER.

### The Thin Red Line
- **Authors:** Greg Lohman and Graham Wright
- **Answer:** HISTORIAN
- **Type:** Location-based cipher / Boston landmarks + MBTA
- **Mechanic:** Photos in Freedom Trail order (SW to NE). Each Freedom Trail site maps to an MBTA Red Line stop. Extract trigram from Freedom Trail name; note letter following that trigram in the Red Line stop name. In sequence spells "LIVY OR HERODOTUS, E.G." → HISTORIAN.

### pluHarmony
- **Authors:** Dan Katz, Mike Selinker, and Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** REUNITES
- **Type:** Multi-card overlay / brand name puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Each card contains a printer's devilry of a word breakable around an ampersand (Abercrombie & Fitch, Arm & Hammer, Ben & Jerry's, Black & Decker, Crate & Barrel, Shake & Bake, Good & Plenty, Spic & Span). Match halves across cards, overlay to reveal single letters at identical positions, map to street intersections (2nd–9th streets), read left-to-right → REUNITES.

### Yoda Sings Schubert!
- **Authors:** Graham Wright and Greg Lohman, with Dan Katz as Yoda
- **Answer:** LOVE-INS
- **Type:** Audio / music identification
- **Mechanic:** Yoda performs seven songs from Schubert's 24-part Winterreise cycle. The alphanumeric equivalents of the song numbers (12, 15, 22, 5, 9, 14, 19) → L, O, V, E, I, N, S = LOVE-INS.

### And Now, a Word from Our Intergalactic Corporate Overlords
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker and Evan Davis
- **Answer:** RAYTHEON
- **Type:** Corporate acquisition / letter-substitution
- **Mechanic:** 26 corporate logos each correspond to a business field listed alphabetically. Each company was acquired by another. Extract the letter from the same position in the acquiring company's name. 26 letters spell "MISSILE COMPANY THAT OWNED AMANA" = RAYTHEON.

### Planet Pluto Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker, Dan Katz, and Meta Group A
- **Answer:** SLINGSHOTS
- **Mechanic:** Each of the eight round answers contains a planet name plus one extra consonant (e.g., EMIGRES = MARS + G, JEEPSTER = JUPITER + S). The eight extra consonants spell SLINGSHOTS.

## ROUND 2: The ATM ($2.00 each)

### The Banks of the River Charles
- **Author:** Foggy Brume (photography by Mike King)
- **Answer:** STRAIT
- **Type:** Location photography / Boston geography
- **Mechanic:** Series of Boston photos with ATM/bank locations. Arrange geographically W to E along Charles River. Index letters spell "WATERWAY OR GEORGE OF C&W" → STRAIT.

### Mary Ka-Ching's
- **Author:** Ian Tullis
- **Answer:** THE RAM
- **Type:** Chinese astrology / word puzzle
- **Mechanic:** 22 animals each named with a Chinese element + Chinese zodiac animal. Arrange by sexagenary cycle (starting from Wood Rat year 2524, the restaurant's opening). First letter of each animal's menu category spells "LEO IS THE LION AND ARIES IS..." → THE RAM (Aries = the Ram).

### Invasion of the Micronauts
- **Authors:** Ian Tullis and Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** TROOP
- **Type:** Microscopic hidden image / physics
- **Mechanic:** Hidden microscopic image in an apparently blank PDF. At 6400% magnification, words appear combined with the six quark types (STRANGE, BOTTOM, CHARM, DOWN, TOP, UP) arranged in a grid. Shaded letters spell TROOP.

### 99 Cents a Clue
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker and Dan Katz
- **Answer:** MEDS
- **Type:** Cryptic crossword with song-based clue substitutions
- **Mechanic:** Standard cryptic crossword where part of each clue is replaced by a song title in brackets. Shaded squares spell "PLACEBO LP FIVE" = MEDS (the fifth Placebo album).

### Teller's Window
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker and Mark Gottlieb
- **Answer:** REX
- **Type:** Homophone chain puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Grid where crossing word pairs contain two synonyms. Each pair's homophones are also synonyms. Intersecting letters spell "MY NAME CAN BE SAID AS WRECKS" — REX sounds like WRECKS.

### The ATM Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Foggy Brume, Ian Tullis, and Meta Group B
- **Answer:** RADIO
- **Mechanic:** Each round answer contains an atmospheric layer name (minus "-osphere") plus one extra letter. Arranged from lowest to highest altitude: TROP(osphere)+O, STRAT(osphere)+I, MES(osphere)+D, THERM(osphere)+A, EX(osphere)+R → R, A, D, I, O = RADIO.

## ROUND 3: Hiigara ($2.00 each)

### Cheat Codes
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** INDIVIDUAL LABOR LAW
- **Type:** Controller button cipher / video game
- **Mechanic:** PlayStation controller buttons correspond to letters. Decode key sequences from seven videos → villain names (Gorgons, El Diablo, Wendigo, Vulture, Lord Voldemort, Boris Badenov, Gremlins). Video 8 decodes to final answer.

### Interstellar Basic Algebra
- **Authors:** Ken Stern and Ian Tullis
- **Answer:** SOBER UP
- **Type:** Linear algebra / cocktail recipes
- **Mechanic:** 13 images of mixed drinks with IBA recipes. Recipes form a 13×13 matrix M. Solve Mx = y → x = [1, 14, 19, 23, 5, 18, 19, 15, 2, 5, 18, 21, 16] → letters = SOBER UP.

### Console-Nation Prizes
- **Author:** Kaitlyn Chantry
- **Answer:** LAVENDER
- **Type:** Xbox Achievement lookup / meta-puzzle
- **Mechanic:** 31 gamertags on Xbox.com; identify specific Achievement icons and unlock dates in May 2008. Arrange games chronologically (May 1–31). First letters of clue answers spell "PARTICULAR HUE OF POKEMON SHION TOWN" → LAVENDER (Lavender Town = Shion Town in Japanese).

### Space Invader
- **Authors:** Ian Tullis and Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** CHINESE LANTERN
- **Type:** Diagramless crossword with hidden row insertion
- **Mechanic:** Diagramless crossword where a blank row is inserted at center. Each of 14 Down entries crossing the blank row takes a unique letter to form new valid words (e.g., MIRO → MICRO, WAS OFF → WASH OFF). The 14 inserted letters read sequentially → CHINESE LANTERN.

### The Comedy Planet, Featuring Sid!
- **Author:** Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** AL FRANKEN
- **Type:** Audio / Caesar cipher
- **Mechanic:** 24 jokes where key words are "audibly Caesar-shifted." "Sid" is from planet Cynarg (rot-13 for "planet"). Shift amounts convert to letters spelling "DEMOCRAT COMIC FOR US SENATE" → AL FRANKEN.

### The Hiigara Dome
- **Authors:** Dan Katz (puzzle), Stephen Peters (video editing)
- **Answer:** HARBOR SONG
- **Type:** Music identification / Rock Band footage
- **Mechanic:** Gameplay footage from Rock Band 1 and 2 (no sound/lyrics). Match 28 songs to footage via cryptic dedications. In correct order, initial letters spell "VEGA TRACK AFTER IF I WERE A WEAPON." On Suzanne Vega's Songs in Red and Gray, the track after "If I Were a Weapon" is HARBOR SONG.

### Feelie Fanatics
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** THE MANCHURIAN CANDIDATE
- **Type:** Cryptic wordplay / movie titles / indexed character selection
- **Mechanic:** Three sets of movie-related items (feelies from Infocom games). Match items to movies, use numbers from second set as indices into movie titles, extract consecutive letters per third set's counts. Extracted sequences (QUEEN OF DIAMONDS, SHAW FOR VICE PRESIDENT BUTTON, LIST OF REDS) all reference the same classic film.

### Hiigara Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Foggy Brume, Mike Selinker, and Meta Group B
- **Answer:** METEORS
- **Mechanic:** Each of seven clues contains a sci-fi character name inside the answer. Each character's homeworld provides one letter (first letter of planet name). In order: MELMAC (M), ETERNIA (E), TATOOINE (T), EARTH (E), ORK (O), ROMULUS (R), SAGITTARON (S) = METEORS.

## ROUND 4: Castor & Pollux ($3.00 each)

### The Apples in Stereo
- **Authors:** Ian Tullis and Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** GINGER GOLD
- **Type:** Interactive applet / card game / Apple error codes
- **Mechanic:** Apples to Apples card-matching game where certain apple varieties trigger vintage Apple computer error codes (#4099, #4100, #4101). Error code numbers cross-reference with PLU codes stickered on produce. PLU code 4097 (triggered at 10 points) identifies the apple variety GINGER GOLD.

### Dual Singularities
- **Authors:** Dan Katz and Francis Heaney
- **Answer:** FRIDA KAHLO
- **Type:** Siamese Twins crossword variant
- **Mechanic:** Modified Siamese Twins puzzle where circled squares cause across and down entries to switch grids. Odd-numbered squares (1, 9, 25, 49, 81) yield FRIDA from grid 1 and KAHLO from grid 2 = FRIDA KAHLO.

### Hyperextensions
- **Author:** Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** QUATTRO PRO
- **Type:** File extension cipher
- **Mechanic:** 22 files with non-standard extensions. Data viewed through open-source software yields English phrases, paired into clues for 7-letter words. Each word uses 6 letters from two chained file extensions plus one extra letter. The 11 extra letters spell "APP USING QPW" = QUATTRO PRO.

### Cloaked Battleships
- **Authors:** Greg Lohman and Zack Butler
- **Answer:** BLITHESOME
- **Type:** Battleships variant
- **Mechanic:** Standard Battleships with alphabetic sequences filling the grid. Single-square ships spell B-L-I-T-H (Castorian fleet) and E-S-O-M-E (Polluxian fleet) = BLITHESOME.

### Good Will, Mystery Hunting
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker and Dan Katz
- **Answer:** GUS VAN SANT
- **Type:** Rebus / film trivia
- **Mechanic:** Each of 14 rewritten Good Will Hunting scenes contains an allusion to Matt Damon/Ben Affleck's other films. First letters of the 14 clues spell "MAN WHO YELLS CUT" = GUS VAN SANT (director of Good Will Hunting).

### Castor & Pollux Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Dan Katz, Zack Butler, and Meta Group A
- **Answer:** GUITAR SOLO
- **Mechanic:** Five round answers (FRIDAKAHLO, BLITHESOME, GUSVANSANT, GINGERGOLD, QUATTROPRO). One letter is repeated between exactly two answers at each position. The repeated letters spell GUITAR SOLO.

## ROUND 5: Kronos ($3.00 each)

### Timecop
- **Authors:** Stephen Peters (puzzle), with Mike Selinker as the Timecop
- **Answer:** ALLIANCES
- **Type:** Cryptarithmetic / geography / Amazing Race parody
- **Mechanic:** Nine fictional/real-world character couples eliminated in different world time zones. Each couple's location maps to an MIT building number. Building number + UTC offset = a letter value (1=A, etc.). Sequence: 1, 12, 12, 9, 1, 14, 3, 5, 19 = ALLIANCES.

### Pimp Your Starcrafts
- **Authors:** Ken Stern and Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** REMEMBER
- **Type:** Nested palindromes
- **Mechanic:** 16 "starcrafts" are nested palindromes (e.g., KR(ONO)ARK). Each clue is a famous person with a palindromic first name missing one letter. The missing letter's position in the surname, applied to the palindromic word, yields letters. "Put REBER and MEM together in the fashion of the starcrafts" = RE(MEM)BER = REMEMBER.

### Your So-Called Second Life
- **Author:** Jennifer Braun
- **Answer:** DISASTER
- **Type:** Online scavenger hunt / Second Life
- **Mechanic:** Screenshots at 12 designated Second Life locations yield word fragments spelling "THE RED WORD ON THE SOUTHWEST CORNER OF CLUB RUIN'S ISLAND." That sign displays DISASTER.

### The Amazing Juggling Troupe of Duckkon Undrum V
- **Author:** Dan Katz
- **Answer:** VIRTUOSO
- **Type:** Juggling sequence tracking / complex logic
- **Mechanic:** Six juggling routines across five jugglers managing objects over five hand positions. Track objects through precise positional rules and time steps. Letters extracted per routine via object properties and juggler positions, with final apocalypse sequence, spell VIRTUOSO.

### A Message in Space
- **Authors:** Foggy Brume and Sean Trowbridge
- **Answer:** RENAISSANCE MAN
- **Type:** Dancing Men cipher + Da Vinci mirror writing
- **Mechanic:** Leonardo Da Vinci's Vitruvian Man figures positioned as "dancing men" from the Sherlock Holmes story "The Adventure of the Dancing Men." Letters are mirror-written (referencing Da Vinci). Flags separate words. Decoding spells RENAISSANCE MAN.

### Picture Time
- **Authors:** Ian Tullis, Mike Selinker, and Zack Butler
- **Answer:** ALBATROSS
- **Type:** Visual / rebus / animal idioms
- **Mechanic:** 15 clickable picture tiles (A through R). 19 pictures represent common expressions featuring animals. Phrases collectively spell "BEING AROUND YOUR NECK" → an albatross (from Rime of the Ancient Mariner) = ALBATROSS.

### Now Is the Time on Kronos When We Dance
- **Authors:** Kaitlyn Chantry and Dan Katz
- **Answer:** LEGACY
- **Type:** Film identification / Fred Astaire & Ginger Rogers
- **Mechanic:** Clips from 10 Fred Astaire/Ginger Rogers films. Identify each film, enter character first names with Ginger's backwards (referencing Frank & Ernest cartoon). Blue squares yield "SCOTT FILM HAS JACK AND LANA" → TJ Scott film with David Hasselhoff as Jack and Donita Rose as Lana = LEGACY.

### Character Test
- **Authors:** Tony Delgado, Mike Selinker, and Greg Lohman
- **Answer:** NECESSARY EVIL
- **Type:** RPG character sheet / SoloStat extraction
- **Mechanic:** Seven character sheets from different RPG systems. SoloStat number (1–7) indexes into each character's attribute list and orders the final message. Letters spell titles of a "Savage Worlds Super Villain Setting Guide Book" → NECESSARY EVIL.

### Split Second Decisions
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** WHISPERS
- **Type:** Split Decisions (George Bredehorn's design)
- **Mechanic:** Grid squares filled using either top/bottom or left/right letter sets. From WHISKERS/WHIMPERS, select appropriate letters → WHISPERS.

### Kronos Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Tony Delgado, Dan Katz, and Meta Group A
- **Answer:** RED SHIRTS
- **Mechanic:** Each round answer is a Star Trek episode. Find stardates from Wikipedia/Memory Alpha, order chronologically, use the digit after decimal point as index into the episode title. Letters: R(ALBATROSS), E(LEGACY), D(DISASTER), S(NECESSARY EVIL), H(WHISPERS), I(ALLIANCES), R(REMEMBER), T(VIRTUOSO), S(RENAISSANCE MAN) = REDSHIRTS.

## ROUND 6: Harvoid Constellation / Galaxocaching ($5.00)

Seven geocaching-style virtual caches in the Ursa Major constellation area. Each requires a password derived from solving the cache puzzle.

### Friends in Noble England (Cache GXCALKD)
- **Author:** Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** PICCADILLY CIRCUS
- **Type:** London Underground Tube map / visual overlay
- **Mechanic:** Artwork from Animals on the Underground website. Identify which station's circle is the right eye of exactly three animals (bat, hound, wallaby) and also part of the pig's line elements = PICCADILLY CIRCUS.

### Aliotherapy (Cache GXCALTH)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** PSALM BOOK
- **Type:** Rebus / visual imagery
- **Mechanic:** Two sets of images with common links. Extracted letter pairs (PALM + S + BO + OK) inserted into REBUS framework = PSALM BOOK.

### Brodie Electric (Cache GXCDUBH)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** DRAMATIS PERSONAE
- **Type:** Artificial script / phonetic decoding
- **Mechanic:** Chromaphonoglyphics script by Richard Brodie. Decode glyphs to reveal six partial movie titles with phonetic segments removed. Segments in splice order: DRUM-ATT-ISS-PUR-SO-NEIGH = DRAMATIS PERSONAE.

### Megrez Expedition (Cache GXCMGRZ)
- **Author:** Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** TUCSON, ARIZONA
- **Type:** US city name fragments / directional arrows
- **Mechanic:** Red/white/blue arrows contain fragments of U.S. city names spanning city and state. Arrows read forward or backward per direction. First letters of identified cities spell "PLACE THAT'S BACKWARDS RANOS." City containing SONAR (RANOS backward) = TUCSON, ARIZONA.

### Growth Involves Reconstructing Legos (Cache GXCMIZR)
- **Author:** Thomas Snyder
- **Answer:** TRAUMA CENTER
- **Type:** Visual skyscraper puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Four visual skyscraper grids with photographs of actual city buildings as constraints. 12 colored tetrominos match building locations. Buildings marked with a hospital symbol (H); heights index into city names. Letters in grid order by position = TRAUMA CENTER.

### Keep It Stylin', Stupid (Cache GXCMRAK)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** INDETERMINATE EQUATION
- **Type:** Costume/math puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Event puzzle. Teams earning correct number of costumes receive a slip of paper. Mathematical example (X+Y+Z=20) demonstrates a concept where multiple variable combinations satisfy the equation — cannot be solved for unique values = INDETERMINATE EQUATION.

### Out Huntin' (Cache GXCPHCD)
- **Author:** Tony Delgado (photography by Jonaya Kemper)
- **Answer:** LEPER COLONY
- **Type:** Geocoin cipher
- **Mechanic:** Locate specific geocoin on geocaching.com, align runes from coin with letters in cache log messages using compass bearings. Each 32-character log message maps to positions around coin circumference. Seventh position at north per cache gives extra letter, ordered by coin symbols = LEPER COLONY.

### Harvoid Constellation Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker, Foggy Brume, Ian Tullis, Meta Group B (with cache locations by Stephen Peters, Zack Butler, Greg Lohman, Graham Wright)
- **Answer:** EPSILON ERIDANI
- **Mechanic:** Seven cache passwords map to pairs of constellations via standard astronomical abbreviations. Cache names arranged in OBAFGKM (stellar classification) order, reading left halves then right halves, spell EPSILON ERIDANI.

## ROUND 7: Virtual Sectors ($5.00)

Teams used a "Wheel of Death" with 28 spaces. Each puzzle answer contains all five vowels plus one repeated vowel. 14 of 28 spaces are puzzle answers.

### Sorry, Wrong Number (Sector A)
- **Authors:** Dan Katz, Nikki Akraboff, Lorinne Lampert, and Stephen Peters (performed by Zyzzlvaria Chorus)
- **Answer:** PORE JUD IS DAID
- **Type:** Musical crossover / Broadway lyrics
- **Mechanic:** 11 sets of Broadway musical clips. One word per set is the target (TRA), others are LA (alphabetized). Reorder LA clips to form actual lyrics from one song, identify which different melody is being used, determine the target word when sung to that melody. Words in order spell "BUT NOW THEY KNOW THEIR FRIEND IS GONE FOR GOOD SONG" = PORE JUD IS DAID (Oklahoma!).

### Mos Eisley Spaceport (Sector C)
- **Author:** Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** MILLENNIUM FALCON
- **Type:** Grid-based path reading / Star Wars
- **Mechanic:** 50 Star Wars ships on a grid. Follow numbered spaces sequentially, read letters along path → "ONLY SHIP TO INTERCEPT BOTH LYCOMING AND DEVASTATOR" = MILLENNIUM FALCON.

### Nurse Charnel's Reports from the Sickbay of the U.S.S. Parkerstone (Sector E)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** SIMULTANEOUS
- **Type:** South Park episode extraction
- **Mechanic:** Each passage describes a Kenny McCormick death from South Park (seasons 1–5). Extract nth letter from each episode title (n = ensign number). Letters spell "CHEF AID TRACK SEVEN" → track 7 on South Park: Chef Aid soundtrack = SIMULTANEOUS.

### Fantastic Voyage (Sector F)
- **Authors:** Mary Linton Peters and Stephen Peters
- **Answer:** LIKE A SURGEON
- **Type:** Anatomical geography / exploration log
- **Mechanic:** Teams explore a body mapped as landscape features. All-caps phrases clue anatomical structures. Base camp numbers index into body part names. Time-codes sequence letters to spell "SONG FEATURING KIDNEYS AND MALPRACTICE" = LIKE A SURGEON ("Weird Al" Yankovic).

### Zyzzlvaria, We Love You! (Sector H)
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker and Dan Katz
- **Answer:** AUDIOPHILE
- **Type:** Audio composition / event puzzle
- **Mechanic:** Teams submitted audio files as homophonic answers (national anthems for Zyzzlvaria). AUDIOPHILE sounds like it could phonetically represent the answer. 28+ teams created musical submissions.

### Well-Coordinated (Sector I)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** TOMATO JUICE
- **Type:** Crossword / coordinate extraction
- **Mechanic:** 50 clues produce spreadsheet coordinates filling a crossword grid. 30 coordinate clues extract specific letters: "CLUE THIRTEEN'S PRIMARY INGREDIENT." Clue 13 yielded V-8 → TOMATO JUICE.

### Malthusian Catastrophes (Sector K)
- **Author:** Greg Lohman
- **Answer:** YELLOW SUBMARINE
- **Type:** Mathematical / exponential population growth
- **Mechanic:** 14 sequential stages; calculate when populations reach capacity of megastructures (Stanford torus, Bernal sphere, Halo ring, Ringworld, Alderson disk, etc.). Day-of-month values across all 14 stages in alphanumerics spell "CRAFT OF FAB FOUR" → YELLOW SUBMARINE (Beatles).

### Soylent Partners (Sector L)
- **Authors:** Foggy Brume and Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** RAINBOW CACTUSES
- **Type:** Word combination / color indexing
- **Mechanic:** Each Soylent bar combines two names containing letters that spell a color; leftover letters = unwelcome byproduct. 15 byproducts assigned to inspectors. Index byproducts using color positions, ordered by inspector number → RAINBOW CACTUSES.

### Discworld (Sector N)
- **Author:** Mike Selinker
- **Answer:** UNAUTHORIZED
- **Type:** Directional word puzzle / Discworld
- **Mechanic:** Four clues per set, answers written in compass directions (rimward/hubward/turnwise/widdershins). Change one letter in each entry to match a common letter; create two real-world country names at intersections. 12 intersection letters = UNAUTHORIZED.

### Plan 4 From Outer Space (Sector O)
- **Author:** Dan Katz
- **Answer:** FOURIER TRANSFORM
- **Type:** Cryptarithmetic (6 independent sets + final)
- **Mechanic:** All digits except 4 are encrypted using substitution ciphers. Solve 6 independent equation sets to establish cipher, determine Greek letter values, substitute into 7th set. Result: 4IERTRANS4M → FOURIER TRANSFORM.

### Crop Squares (Sector Q)
- **Author:** Dan Katz (photography by Jennifer Braun)
- **Answer:** BACKGROUND NOISE
- **Type:** Visual extraction / Sol LeWitt art installation
- **Mechanic:** 15 boxes in U-shape, each with 16-letter phrase cluing a letter. Puzzle also uses crops from Sol LeWitt's "Bars of Color Within Squares" terrazzo floor at MIT. Match crops to source boxes, extract letters at corresponding positions → BACKGROUND NOISE.

### Showcases (Sector T)
- **Author:** Ian Tullis
- **Answer:** QUANTITY SURVEYOR
- **Type:** Cryptarithmetic / Deal or No Deal
- **Mechanic:** 26 complaints, each with a case number and model name from Deal or No Deal. Each complaint references a real legal settlement payout. Amounts correspond to the 26 standard values from the show's board. Convert settlement amounts to letters using $0.01=A, $1=B, etc., arranged by case number → QUANTITY SURVEYOR.

### Let's Play Spaceball! (Sector V)
- **Author:** Foggy Brume
- **Answer:** MINOR LEAGUE
- **Type:** Word construction grid (Spaceball variant)
- **Mechanic:** Each grid: pitcher's mound clue + baseline word – one letter = answer (e.g., MAR + DIALOG – A = MARIGOLD). 18 grids arranged on scoreboard reveal "LIKE THE BATAVIA MUCKDOGS, MOBILE BAY BEARS, ALBUQUERQUE ISOTOPES, AND HONOLULU SHARKS" = MINOR LEAGUE.

### Supercollider (Sector W)
- **Authors:** Mike Selinker, Greg Lohman, and Graham Wright
- **Answer:** ULTRAVIOLET RAYS
- **Type:** MIT Virtual Tour video / indexed extraction
- **Mechanic:** 23 objects in MIT Infinite Corridor virtual tour video; each clue corresponds to a specific frame number. Index into each answer at the frame position → 23 letters spell "THEY CAUSE PINBALL PROTONS" = ULTRAVIOLET RAYS.

### Virtual Sectors Metapuzzle
- **Authors:** Foggy Brume, Ken Stern, and Meta Group B
- **Answer:** ESKIMO NEBULA
- **Mechanic:** Wheel of Death with 28 spaces, 14 being puzzle answers (each containing all 5 vowels + one repeated). Extra vowel's next letter per answer. Letters (sans vowels) spell "SUPERMAN'S ADVERSARIAL IMP" (Mr. Mxyzptlk). Teams could buy a vowel (250 play-dollarbucks) to receive large O to overlay on Wheel of Fortune wheel. Colors index into puzzle answers → Harold's location = ESKIMO NEBULA.

Ninth Doctor puzzle provides instruction: "COMMENCING AT THE OORT CLOUD OPERATE HIS TARDIS ON INTERIOR BOARD." Starting at OO in row two, perform checkers-style king jumps → spell location of character Ernie = TIMELINE'S END.

## Notable Puzzle Design Patterns

### Ian Tullis Signature Characteristics (from this hunt)
- Wordplay built on specialized knowledge systems: SI units, ICD-10 codes, Chinese characters, phylogenetics
- Audio-based puzzles with multiple decoding steps (Calling You from My Cell, Crossed Wires)
- Self-referential puzzle structures (Resolutions uses previous Hunt metas as solving framework)
- Clean mechanic exposition: the answer is always derivable from a single core insight
- Pairs frequently with Selinker (Invasion of the Micronauts, Space Invader, Star-Crossed, Picture Time, Warning)

### Mike Selinker Signature Characteristics (from this hunt)
- Broad pop culture references: Star Wars, NASCAR, corporations, LOLcats, Doctor Who, MST3K
- Corporate/brand puzzles with real-world data (And Now a Word, Megrez Expedition, Our Favorite Martian)
- Comedy-inflected puzzle premises (Comedy Planet's Caesar/Sid Caesar pun, I Can Haz Zyzzlburger, CAPTCHA puzzle)
- Served as overall puzzle coordinator — "assigned and developed the puzzles" for the entire hunt
- Prolific co-author: appears on ~35+ puzzles out of hunt total of ~110
- Metapuzzle architect: credited on 5+ metapuzzles including Reverse Dimension meta and Astro Jail meta

### Mark Gottlieb Signature Characteristics (from this hunt)
- Technical/mechanical puzzles: 3D assembly (Bad Eggs), chemical element chaining (Fifth Element), card-back dot-connecting (I'm Your Magic Man)
- Hunt editor: responsible for editing all puzzles (quality control role)
- Puzzle count lower (6) but includes complex, visually precise designs

### Dan Katz Signature Characteristics (from this hunt)
- Hunt architect: "invented Zyzzlvaria; coordinated hunt structure"
- Complex logic and tracking puzzles: juggling sequences, cryptarithmetic, kakuro variants
- Music and film puzzles requiring deep catalog knowledge
- Star Trek themed metapuzzle (Kronos Meta) — demonstrates comfort with franchise trivia systems
- Frequently appears as co-author on metas (credited on 8+ metapuzzles)
