## Hunt-by-Hunt Master Record

### PH I: The Microsoft Games (Aug 13–15, 1999)
- **Theme**: Olympics parody with Microsoft product tie-ins
- **Host**: TLA — Bruce Leban, Roy Leban, Dan Westreich, Gordon Dow, Jason Woolever, Matthew Kotler, Sam Sherry, Sarita James, Scott Ruthfield, Shannon Talbott
- **Teams**: ~35 teams, ~275 solvers
- **Winner**: Killer Bees
- **Structure**: Bronze, Silver, and Gold rounds
- **Established**: Winning team hosts next year

---

### PH II: Age of Puzzles (Apr 1–2, 2000)
- **Theme**: Age of Empires
- **Host**: Killer Bees — **Eric LeVine, Kenny Young, Dana Young**, Steve Bush, Christine Chang, Jeff Reynar, Todd Roshak
- **Teams**: 27 teams, 305 players (1 team finished)
- **Winner**: Cracking Good Toast
- **Firsts**: Computer scoring, point system
- **Note**: Kenny and Dana Young's first hosting role — as winners from PH I

---

### PH III: You Don't Know Puzzles (Nov 4–5, 2000)
- **Theme**: Game show parody (Baffle, Concentration, Crosswits, Inquizition)
- **Host**: Cracking Good Toast — Jack Bridges, Jennifer Gosnell, Mark Lambert, Alex Mogilevsky, Tom Powers, Brian Sanderson, **Peter Sarrett**, Ken Wong, Mark Engelberg
- **Teams**: 28 teams, 344 players (1 team finished)
- **Winner**: Killer Bees
- **Firsts**: 12-person team limit, live events, repeat winner

---

### PH IV: Clue (Nov 3–4, 2001)
- **Theme**: Clue board game and film
- **Host**: The Usual Suspects — Jennifer Cockrill, Giovanni Della-Libera, Meredith McClurg, Brooke Nelson, Andrew Ryder, Peter Sagerson, Kenny Wolf
- **Teams**: 38 teams, 416 players (4 teams finished)
- **Winner**: SCRuBBers
- **Firsts**: Hard 12-member limit (no substitutions), multiple finishing teams

---

### PH V: Mission: Impuzzible (Sep 21–22, 2002)
- **Theme**: Mission: Impossible spy thriller
- **Host**: SCRuBBers — Troy Barnes, Josh Benaloh, Steve Dupree, Matt Lyons, Becca Olsen, Deborah Pierce, Jon Pincus, Dan Simon, Terence Spies, Dave Thaler, Jay Thaler, Jeff Wallace
- **Teams**: 45 teams, 469 players (2 teams finished)
- **Winner**: Cracking Good Toast (1st and 2nd separated by 11 minutes)

---

### PH VI: TimeCorps (May 17–18, 2003)
- **Theme**: Time travel / sci-fi
- **Host**: Cracking Good Toast — Andrew Becker, Rolf Buchner, Thomas Coon, Greg Hermann, Victor Kozyrev, Mike Marcelais, Don Munsil, **Peter Sarrett**, Roger Wolff
- **Teams**: 40 teams, 439 players (4 teams finished)
- **Winner**: TLA Lovers Anonymous (8-hour margin — largest in hunt history)
- **★ NOTABLE PUZZLE**: **Chicago Fire** — crossword printed on red construction paper. Solvers had to pour water on it. Only the Scotch-Guarded squares remained legible, revealing the answer. Legendary tactile puzzle design. **Attributed to Peter Sarrett.**

---

### PH 7: Alice in Puzzlehunt (Mar 20–21, 2004)
- **Theme**: Lewis Carroll's Alice
- **Host**: TLA Loves Alice — Brett Roark, **Bruce Leban**, Chip Brown, Darren Rigby, Elan Lee, Gordon Dow, Ken Jordan, Kiran Kedlaya, Mike Koss, Paul Grimes, **Roy Leban**, Scott Ruthfield, Bruce Kaskel
- **Teams**: 51 teams, 558 players (1 team finished)
- **Winner**: Staggering Geniuses
- **★ Notable**: Coordinated with Will Shortz to include the actual Sunday NYT crossword puzzle within the hunt
- **★ Notable**: Washington State House Resolution No. 4717 officially proclaimed March 20–21, 2004 as "Alice in Puzzlehunt Weekend"
- **Interactive**: Croquet game on Building 50 lawn

---

### PH 8: The Hard Way (Feb 19–20, 2005)
- **Theme**: Las Vegas glamour
- **Host**: Staggering Geniuses — Amanda O'Connor, **Mark L. Gottlieb**, Sean Trowbridge, **Mike Selinker**, Matt Ruhlen, Greg Lewis, Jason Alcock, Matt Dixon, Ron Giesen, Matt Jones, Chris McBride, Chris Pearo
- **Teams**: 57 teams, 643 players (6 teams finished)
- **Winner**: Cracking Good Toast (third-time winner)
- **★ Notable**: Las Vegas travel brochure with 7 embedded puzzles + metapuzzle; building cutouts cast phone number shadows when illuminated
- **First**: Three-time winner; first team to solve all puzzles (non-winning)
- **Published**: Featured in November 2005 GAMES World of Puzzles
- **Note**: Gottlieb and Selinker both on host team

---

### PH 9: Doomsday (Nov 5–6, 2005)
- **Theme**: American comic books / superhero (originally "Countdown")
- **Host**: Everyday Heroes — Karen Babcock, Stephen Beeman, Nick Gedge, Dave Heberer, Sharry Heberer/Claypool, Jesse McGatha, Brett Roark, Richard Rowan
- **Teams**: 62 teams, 708 players (6 teams finished)
- **Winner**: Cracking Good Toast (fourth-time winner)
- **★ Notable**: 8 total metas including **final rebus puzzle meta**
- **Structure**: Opening newspaper format, Captain Micropolis / The Puzzler villain
- **First**: Unlocking scheme without bonuses

### PH A: Atlantis (Feb 10–11, 2007)
- **Theme**: Mythical lost city / underwater
- **Host**: Buzz Lime Pi and Friends — Chris Battey, Mike Burk, Jeremy Elson, Carrie Emmerich, Nick Emmerich, Jon Howell, Jay Lorch, Evan McLain, Sora Werner, Chase Stephens, Robert Stewart, Michelle Teague, **Dana Young, Kenny Young**
- **Teams**: ~75 teams, ~864 participants (12 teams finished)
- **Duration**: ~30 hours
- **Winner**: Death of Dr. Zero
- **★ Notable puzzles**:
  - Interactive Virtual Earth map to locate campus items; organizers flew airplane with high-resolution camera (3cm/pixel detail) — basically bespoke aerial photography as puzzle infrastructure
  - Quinoa-powered analog computer producing phone number
  - Ring diving at Microsoft Pro Club pool
  - Laser-cut foam piece assemblies filtering quinoa into readable patterns
- **Awards**: Laser-etched acrylic "Access Tokens" themed to Atlantis
- **Firsts**: Automated web answer submission; end-of-hunt spectacle at closing ceremonies

---

### PH 11.0: Caught in the Net (Oct 6–7, 2007)
- **Theme**: Tron / digital networks
- **Numbering**: "11.0" as software versioning nod
- **Host**: SCRuBBers — Troy Barnes, Josh Benaloh, Nat Dupree, Steve Dupree, Jessica Lambert, Matt Lyons, Dan Simon, Dave Thaler, Jay Thaler, Kaylene Thaler, **Ian Tullis**, Jeff Wallace, Roger Wolfson
- **Teams**: 71 teams, 830 players (14 teams finished)
- **Winner**: The Usual Suspects
- **★ Notable**: Illuminated flying disks and puzzle spheres; MCP vs. internet rebel forces theme
- **Built in**: Microsoft Silverlight
- **Firsts**: First hunt where all finishing teams wrote programs to solve meta; unlocking system tracking top 3 fastest solves per puzzle
- **Note**: Ian Tullis hosted this hunt as part of SCRuBBers

---

### PH 123: Jeopardy!/Puzzlehaunt! (Feb 28–Mar 1, 2009)
- **Theme**: Jeopardy! morphing into haunted puzzle hunt
- **Host**: The Usual Suspects (Rich Bragg, Giovanni Della-Libera, Gordon Dow, Caroll Ferry, Douglas Ferry, Dave Fisher, Brent Lang, David Miller, Brooke Nelson, Andrew Ryder, Peter Sagerson) + Cracking Good Toast (Andrew Becker, Mike Marcelais, **Peter Sarrett**) + **Dana Young + Kenny Young**
- **Teams**: Redmond: 84 teams, 975 players (1 finished); Bay Area: 16 teams, 178 players (1 finished)
- **Total**: ~100 teams, ~1,100 participants
- **Winners**: SCRuBBers (Redmond); Demonic Robot Tyrannosaurs (Bay Area)
- **★ SIGNATURE KENNY YOUNG META**:
  - 30 paper rectangles → **rhombic triacontahedron**
  - 20 nonagons → **icosahedron**
  - 12 pentagons → **dodecahedron**
  - Combined → **rhombicosidodecahedron** with chess puzzle on its surface
- **"Daily Double"**: Timed interactive team puzzles
- **Divisions**: Competitive (no hints) + Recreational (graduated hints)
- **Firsts**: First simulcast hunt; first competitive/recreational divisions; record participation
- **Origin**: Teams planning hunts 12 and 13 merged

---

### PH 14: Travel the Number 14 (Sep 10–11, 2011)
- **Theme**: Trains / Carmen Sandiego — recover stolen Space Needle
- **Host**: Liboncatipu — Ben Andrews, Jen Chalfan, Jamie Eckman, Jeff Ford, Jonobie Ford, Drew Hoskins, Mike Janney, Jason Lucas, Chris Moore, Andy Rich, Rehana Rodrigues, Deanna Rubin, Adam Ruprecht
- **Teams**: 81 teams
- **Winners**: Death of Dr. Zero (Redmond); Friday the 13th Part VI (Bay Area)
- **★ Notable mechanics**:
  - Hole-punched train tickets as puzzle elements
  - Kinect motion controls for body positions
  - Geometric models built with coffee stirrers and yarn
  - Facebook-based logic grids
  - Braille decoding from pentomino assemblies
  - Magic: The Gathering rethemed card games
  - Physical penny maze representing nickel
  - Word squares; Ricochet Robots adaptations
  - "Quintetrahedronicon" complex physical structure (noted for ambiguous instructions)
- **Awards**: Personalized wooden train whistles (top 3 teams)
- **4 whistle stops** designed for conference room projection
- **First**: Explicit rules for hints and data confirmation

### PH 15: The Motion Picture Post-Apocalyptic (Jun 21–22, 2014)
- **Theme**: Dystopian cinema (300, The Matrix Reloaded, The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift, etc.)
- **Host**: Modular teams (first use of modular format)
- **Puzzles**: ~60
- **Winner**: Will It Blend?
- **★ Innovation**: Introduced modular format — multiple independent authoring teams, no coordination, authors can compete as solvers
- **Impact**: Became standard format for all subsequent hunts

### PH 23: Puzzle University (May 6–7, 2023)
- **Theme**: Academic / fictional university for enigmatological education
- **Host**: Modular — single-team authorship (exception to modular format) — hosted entirely by Dana Young, Kenny Young, Derek Young, and team
- **Puzzles**: ~100 (118 total across 11 rounds)
- **Technology**: First to use gph-site platform
- **Difficulty**: Between Puzzle Potluck and Teammate Hunt
- **Expected completion**: Top teams 12–24 hours
- **Winner**: Unknown (first fully open worldwide hunt, no MS affiliation required)
- **Awards (3rd Bravo Awards)**:
  - Extremely Large Hunt of the Year (tied with MIT MH 2023)
  - Best Twist on a Classic Style finalist (Placement Test round — Dana Young + Derek Young)
  - Best Visual Design finalist (Dana Young + Matt Du)
  - Best Non-Puzzle Contribution finalist (PuzzleJS — Kenny Young)
