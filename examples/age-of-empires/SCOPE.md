## Content Library

For this test scenario, the "encyclopedia" is AoE game knowledge. The solver's reference is their own AoE experience + the game's tech tree and civilization bonuses. No external encyclopedia directory — the solver's memory IS the library.

Key content domains:
1. **Civilizations** — unique bonuses, unique units, team bonuses, wonder names
2. **Units** — stats, counters, upgrade paths
3. **Technologies** — research costs, effects, age requirements
4. **Maps** — standard map types, resource layouts, terrain
5. **Strategy** — build orders, age advancement, rush timings

---

## Narrative

The Monk (narrator) has achieved Heresy and Theocracy research. He speaks in short, declarative sentences. He never uses exclamation marks. His catchphrase — "Wololo" — appears exactly once, at the end of the meta.

Voice rules (adapted from toolkit):
- No exclamation marks
- Short sentences
- Present tense
- No meta-commentary about the puzzles
- The solver is "you"
- Occasionally references game mechanics as if they're real
