## Puzzle 1: Scicabulary — Answer: SHAMROCK

**Source:** MIT Mystery Hunt 2023 (Batch 1)

### Lens Firing — DK

- **DK-5 (Does every puzzle justify its slot?)** 24-entry matching puzzle with a multi-step mechanic (portmanteau deconstruction → two-word phrase matching → indexing). The slot is earned. Not a placeholder.
- **DK-7 (Are the mechanisms varied enough? — contextual to hunt)** As a standalone: the three-layer mechanism (parse jargon term, solve definition, then index) is internally varied. No monotony within the puzzle.
- **DK-10 (Is the thematic coherence structural, not decorative?)** "Scicabulary" as fake scientific vocabulary is the wrapper and the content simultaneously — the jargon terms ARE the mechanism, not labels on it. Structural coherence passes.
- **DK-12 (Would he want to solve this?)** The fake portmanteau terminology is the kind of conceptual joke he appreciates. The matching step defers satisfaction intentionally. Likely yes.

### Lens Firing — TS

- **TS-1 (Is the entry point constructed or discovered?)** The entry point is the instruction: parse the jargon term into its two halves. The constructor placed this explicitly. The puzzle does not hide its entry — it explains it clearly. Constructed.
- **TS-3 (Is each element load-bearing?)** 24 entries. Each must be uniquely resolvable to one definition — the matching constraint does real work. The index numbers are each specific. Load-bearing throughout.
- **TS-4 (Does the theme shape the structure?)** The fake scientific register shapes every entry. The theme is not a title on a generic grid; it's the material the puzzle is made of.
- **TS-5 (Is the difficulty technique-based or noise-based?)** Some entries require genuine portmanteau knowledge (e.g., "Tulismin" for an index-15 phrase — a long phrase is required). The challenge is genuine knowledge, not obfuscation.
- **TS-6 (Can the solver confirm each step independently?)** Yes. Once you identify the matched pair, the index number gives an immediate letter. Intermediate checking works.
- **TS-11 (Is there a puzzle here, or a procedure?)** The matching step is the genuine deduction — you must find which two-word phrase pairs with which jargon term. This is not mechanical.

### Lens Firing — DY

- **DY-3 (Where does the theme dissolve?)** The theme is "scientific jargon" — it holds through all 24 terms and all 24 definitions. Even the extraction (indexing into the matched phrase) carries the theme obliquely (measurement and precision are scientific behaviors). Theme holds.
- **DY-7 (Does the flavor text explain what the layout should have communicated?)** The instruction ("Is there a way to match these up?") is a nudge, not a crutch. The layout communicates the table structure. Flavor text earns its presence.
- **DY-8 (Is the extraction earning its step?)** The index-into-phrase extraction adds one layer over the matching. That layer is thin but not costly. The real solve is the matching; extraction is earned.
- **DY-9 (Is the difficulty curve honest?)** Entry is accessible (definitions are whimsical and parseable). Mid-puzzle difficulty rises with longer phrases and higher indices. No ambush.
- **DY-10 (Is the answer arbitrary or inevitable?)** SHAMROCK is not thematically inevitable — it does not reframe "scientific jargon" in a satisfying way. The extraction reaches it through letter-by-letter accumulation, not through meaning. Mild defect: answer is arbitrary relative to the theme.

### Scores

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Clarity | 4 | Instructions clear; 24-entry table is visually clean |
| Solvability | 4 | Genuinely solvable; some entries require cultural knowledge |
| Elegance | 4 | Tri-layer mechanic is elegant; minor: SHAMROCK is thematically inert |
| Reading Reward | 4 | Scientific jargon register is enjoyable throughout |
| Fun | 4 | The joke-terminology keeps energy up across 24 entries |
| Confirmation | 4 | SHAMROCK arrives letter by letter; no single aha confirms all |

**Total: 24/30 — PASS**

**C5-Unique Defect:** DY-10 flags that SHAMROCK does not land with thematic inevitability. The accumulated-letter extraction produces an answer that feels like the machine stopped at that word. Without biography or philosophy context (which would invoke "the answer is the last move in the logic"), this defect surfaces more sharply here than in conditions with fuller profiles.

## Puzzle 2: People Watching — Answer: HINDWING

- **TS-5 (Is the difficulty technique-based or noise-based?)** Difficulty lies in bird call knowledge and field marks. That is genuine ornithological technique. But 14 entries with varied knowledge domains (calls, plumage, geography, behavior) creates an uneven difficulty distribution rather than a constructed gradient. Some entries are much harder than others for non-specialists.
- **TS-6 (Can the solver confirm each step independently?)** The IBP code extraction produces 4-letter strings per bird; letters extracted from specific positions spell HINDWING. Intermediate confirmation is possible if the solver knows the IBP codes.
- **TS-9 (Does the visual design communicate structure?)** As text-represented here: the social-media formatting (tweet-style vignettes with like counts) communicates the "observation" context well. Structure legible.

- **DY-2 (Is each image chosen for what it communicates?)** The "like count" for each tweet is likely ornithologically significant (e.g., 17 likes → a specific bird count or code position). If so, this is precise communicative work. If decorative, it is visual noise.
- **DY-6 (Does it work without the context?)** The puzzle is solvable by a non-specialist but only with a bird call guide and field mark lookup. Accessible with resources; locked without them.
- **DY-9 (Is the difficulty curve honest?)** Some vignettes have multiple overlapping clues (call + visual field mark + habitat); others may over-rely on single obscure signals. Difficulty is uneven, not graduated.
- **DY-10 (Is the answer arbitrary or inevitable?)** HINDWING is a butterfly/insect term, not a bird term. The answer does not reframe "bird watching" — it lands orthogonally to the theme. This is a significant defect under the "inevitable vs. arbitrary" test.

| Reading Reward | 4 | Ornithology enthusiasts rewarded richly; others face a wall |

| Confirmation | 3 | IBP code verification requires external reference |

**C5-Unique Defect:** DY-10 fires hard here. HINDWING is an insect wing term, not a bird term. The entire puzzle is built around bird identification, yet the answer names a structure found on moths and butterflies. This thematic mismatch — the answer does not name the experience you had while solving — is the kind of defect that surfaces most clearly when the reviewer is armed only with the operational question "is the answer arbitrary or inevitable?" without being moderated by biographical context about the puzzle's source hunt.
