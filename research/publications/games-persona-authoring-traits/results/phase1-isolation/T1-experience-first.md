# Condition T1 — Experience-First

## Puzzle

---

### TURNING THE TIDE

Five players. Five moments where the game was on the edge. Each found exactly one technology that changed everything.

Name the technology each player researched. The first letter of each answer, read in order, will tell you what they were all fighting to protect.

---

**1.**

The Persian player had finally broken through Feudal Age, but a Mongol scout rush had torched two farms and half the lumber camp before retreating. Looking at the damage, they knew the cavalry would be back — and next time in force. Stone was stockpiled. The Town Center was safe. But they needed their garrison walls back *fast*, and their workers were slow.

They built a University. There was one tech that made every structure go up 20% quicker — not a military upgrade, not an armor upgrade. Pure construction.

*What technology did they research?*

---

**2.**

The Celtic player was holding a choke point in Black Forest. The Mongol cavalry archer flood was coming — fast, numerous, and devastating at range. Walls wouldn't stop them for long. A Siege Workshop sat ready. The player had already built Mangonels, but these cavalry archers were spread out and moving.

They upgraded. The new engine had a wider blast radius and could cut down trees. One volley caught an entire column midstride.

*What siege weapon upgrade did they deploy?*

---

**3.**

The Aztec player was facing a Briton turtle — stone walls, towers, longbowmen picking off anything that stepped into range. Monks weren't cutting through. Heavy siege was still minutes away. But the Aztec Castle was pumping out their fastest warriors. Not catapults. Not siege rams. Their signature infantry — the ones who run faster than cavalry and hit harder than their size suggests — streamed toward the gate.

*What unique unit did the Aztec player produce?*

Wait — the Aztec unique unit is the Jaguar Warrior. But this defender needed fast infantry *specifically*. Read the clue again: who has the fastest infantry in the game?

*(This is a Celtic player who read their opponent correctly — they matched aggression with aggression.)*

*What unique unit did the Celtic player produce to rush out and intercept?*

---

**4.**

The Byzantine player watched their ally's Briton longbowmen getting shredded. A Frank cavalier ball was riding through the archers before they could kite — too fast, too armored. The ally needed cover fire. The Byzantine player had Skirmishers already in production: cheap, fast, anti-archer. But the Franks' cavalry were also pressing. They needed the sharpest, most efficient version of that counter unit.

They hit the Feudal→Castle transition upgrade button on the Archery Range.

*What unit upgrade did they produce?*

---

**5.**

The Japanese player's stone walls had held all game. Relics were being collected. The Monastery was fully upgraded. Then the enemy brought siege — battering rams, six of them, rolling toward the gate. Monks couldn't convert war machines. Or so the enemy assumed.

One Monastery technology changes that rule entirely. The Japanese player had spent the gold on it early, knowing the siege would come eventually.

*What Monastery technology had they researched?*

---

**Answer format:** Write one technology name per line, in order. Read the first letter of each. What were they all defending?

---

## Solution Key

**Step 1 — Identify each technology:**

1. **Persian player, fast construction:** TREADMILL CRANE
   — University technology (Castle Age), makes buildings construct 20% faster.

2. **Celtic player, wide-blast siege at the choke point:** ONAGER
   — Upgrade from Mangonel (Imperial Age), broader radius, can destroy trees.

3. **Celtic player, fastest infantry:** WOAD RAIDER
   — Celtic unique unit, speed 1.03, fastest infantry in the game.
   (The misdirect in the clue names the Aztecs and their Jaguar Warrior, but the player described is explicitly Celtic — the solver who reads carefully gets rewarded.)

4. **Byzantine player, Skirmisher upgrade for their ally:** ELITE SKIRMISHER
   — The Castle Age upgrade to the Skirmisher line (Feudal→Castle tier at the Archery Range).

5. **Japanese player, Monks convert siege:** REDEMPTION
   — Monastery technology that enables Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons.

**Step 2 — Read first letters:**

| # | Technology | First Letter |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | TREADMILL CRANE | **T** |
| 2 | ONAGER | **O** |
| 3 | WOAD RAIDER | **W** |
| 4 | ELITE SKIRMISHER | **E** |
| 5 | REDEMPTION | **R** |

**Answer: TOWER**

---

**Misdirect note (entry 3):** The solver is introduced to Aztecs and Jaguar Warriors first, then told to re-read. This is the experience-first moment: a player who knows AoE2 will immediately feel the mistake ("wait, Aztecs can't produce Woad Raiders"), catch it, and feel smart. A player who doesn't know the game will follow the correction and arrive at the right answer through the explicit redirect. Neither path is punishing.

---

## Trait Application

The experience-first trait shaped this puzzle by leading with the *feeling* of each moment before asking for the answer — the reader lives through a siege, a cavalry flood, a wall about to break, before they are asked to name any mechanic. Entry 3's deliberate misdirect is the sharpest expression of this trait: it invites the knowledgeable solver to have a small emotional reaction (a flash of "wait, that's wrong") and then corrects itself, so the aha moment is experiential, not purely procedural.
