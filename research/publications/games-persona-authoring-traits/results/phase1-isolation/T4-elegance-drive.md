# Condition T4 — Elegance Drive

## Puzzle

**FORTIFICATIONS**

Five entries. Each answer begins with one letter. Read those letters top to bottom.

```
1.  Imperial-Age siege weapon that must pack and unpack to fire.

    [T] _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _


2.  Castle-Age upgrade to the Mangonel; can fell trees.

    [O] _ _ _ _ _


3.  The Celts' unique unit — fastest infantry in the game.

    [W] _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _


4.  Mesoamerican infantry unit; countered by the Champion line.

    [E] _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _ _


5.  Monastery technology enabling Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons.

    [R] _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
```

## Solution Key

Each clue has a unique answer. The bracketed letter is the first letter of that answer.

| # | Answer | First Letter |
|---|--------|--------------|
| 1 | TREBUCHET | **T** |
| 2 | ONAGER | **O** |
| 3 | WOAD RAIDER | **W** |
| 4 | EAGLE WARRIOR | **E** |
| 5 | REDEMPTION | **R** |

Reading the bracketed initials top to bottom: **T – O – W – E – R**

Answer: **TOWER**

Verification that each answer is unambiguous:

- TREBUCHET: the only Imperial-Age siege unit with a pack/unpack mechanic; no other unit matches.
- ONAGER: the Mangonel upgrades to Onager (Castle → Imperial); Siege Onager is the further Imperial upgrade, not described as "can fell trees" in casual shorthand, but Onager is the canonical tree-cutter. The clue is tight to this tier.
- WOAD RAIDER: the Celts' unique unit; speed 1.03 is the highest of any infantry in the game. No other unit satisfies both constraints.
- EAGLE WARRIOR: Mesoamerican unique infantry (Aztecs, Mayans, Incas in DE); explicitly countered by the Militia / Champion line per the counter cheat sheet. The two-word answer's first letter is E.
- REDEMPTION: Monastery tech, cost 475G, effect is conversion of buildings and siege weapons. No other tech matches both constraints.

## Trait Application

Every element that does not carry information was removed: there is no flavor text, no title beyond a single thematic word ("FORTIFICATIONS"), no instruction beyond one sentence that states only what to do. The five clues are the minimum surface needed to pin five unique answers, and the extraction mechanism announces itself through the bracketed letters without requiring a stated rule.
