# Condition L-T1 — Artist minus Experience-First

## Puzzle

**FIVE THINGS THAT TOUCH A TOWER**

---

```
TREBUCHET
ONAGER
WATCH TOWER
ELITE SKIRMISHER
REDEMPTION
```

Each of these has a specific relationship to a tower in Age of Empires II.
What word do they spell?

---

## Solution Key

Each answer begins with a letter. Reading the first letter of each item top to bottom spells the answer.

1. **TREBUCHET** — The dedicated destroyer of towers, firing from beyond arrow range. → **T**
2. **ONAGER** — Suppresses arrow fire from towers; can also clear the forest cover shielding them. → **O**
3. **WATCH TOWER** — The structure itself, first tier of the upgrade line. → **W**
4. **ELITE SKIRMISHER** — Garrison this unit inside any tower to add one arrow to its volley. → **E**
5. **REDEMPTION** — The Monastery technology that allows Monks to convert enemy towers. → **R**

**T — O — W — E — R**

The five items each touch a tower from a different angle: destruction, suppression, existence, empowerment, capture. Their first letters name the structure they orbit.

## Omitted Trait Effect

Without the experience-first orientation, the puzzle gives the solver the five answers directly and asks "what word do they spell?" — skipping the clue layer entirely, so there is no journey of identification, no moment of discovery where an AoE2 term resolves from a description, and no calibrated sequence of easier-to-harder deductions that would let a solver build momentum before the final extraction. The visual concept (five relationships to a tower) is intact, but the path from seeing the grid to knowing what to do with it is missing its scaffolding: a solver unfamiliar with puzzle conventions may not know whether to look at first letters, last letters, letter counts, or something else, and the puzzle offers no entry point that guides them toward the mechanism.
