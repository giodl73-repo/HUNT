# Condition L-T2 — Artist minus Surprise Instinct

## Puzzle

**RECORD OF EXTREMES**

Each entry below describes an Age of Empires II unit or object by the single statistic in which it holds the record for its class. Identify each one. Take its first letter. Read down.

---

1. The siege weapon with the longest range of any unit in the game. Stationary when firing. Countered by fast cavalry.

2. The siege weapon that can destroy trees. Upgraded from the Mangonel. Area damage that hits friendly units.

3. The unit with the highest HP of any in the game. Slow. Generates trample damage. Countered by Halberdiers.

4. The unit that hard-counters archers while costing no gold. An upgrade of the Skirmisher.

5. The object that generates gold passively when garrisoned in a Monastery. Thirty gold per minute.

---

## Solution Key

The five entries identify AoE2 units and objects by their record-holding statistic. Taking the first letter of each answer in sequence spells the answer word.

1. "Longest range of any unit — stationary when firing" — **Trebuchet** → **T**
2. "Can destroy trees — upgraded from the Mangonel" — **Onager** (Siege Onager variant) → **O**
3. "Highest HP of any unit — slow — trample — countered by Halberdiers" — **War Elephant** (600 HP elite) → **W**
4. "Hard-counters archers while costing no gold — upgrade of the Skirmisher" — **Elite Skirmisher** → **E**
5. "Generates gold passively when garrisoned in a Monastery" — **Relic** (30 gold/minute) → **R**

**T-O-W-E-R**

## Omitted Trait Effect

Without the surprise orientation, the mechanism is declared in the setup line before the solver reads a single entry — "identify each one, take its first letter, read down" — so the acrostic structure is visible immediately and there is no gap between the puzzle's apparent subject and its extraction method. The clues are written as direct specifications rather than images or riddles, which eliminates the misdirection that would otherwise make a solver feel they had discovered something; instead the puzzle asks for competent identification, and the satisfaction is in completing a known task cleanly rather than in an unexpected reframe.
