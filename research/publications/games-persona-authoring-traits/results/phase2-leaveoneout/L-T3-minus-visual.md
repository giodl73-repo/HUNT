# Condition L-T3 — Artist minus Visual Thinking

## Puzzle

**FIVE REPORTS**

Your scout has sent back five dispatches. Each one names something. Identify the thing named in each report. Then, in the answer you write for each, find the second letter. Read those five letters in order.

---

1. "The quarry workers are exhausted. They've been hauling cart after cart to the foundation site. Whatever they're raising, it needs a lot of this."

2. "Four robed figures left the monastery at dawn. They're not fighting — they're chanting. Two of ours crossed over before noon."

3. "The swordsmen we've been facing just changed. They're still on foot, still in the militia line — but they've graduated past what we knew. One more rank stands between them and the Champion."

4. "The University sent a researcher out to their tower last night. It wasn't a Guard Tower when he left. It isn't anymore."

5. "They've assembled the counterweight frame in the rear. It's aimed at our walls. We've seen this machine before — there's nothing slower, and nothing more devastating to stone."

---

*What word do those five second letters spell?*

---

## Solution Key

Each report describes a specific AoE2 entity. The second letter of each answer is the extraction.

1. "A lot of this" to raise a foundation — **STONE** → S-**T**-O-N-E → **T**
2. Robed figures converting units, leaving the monastery — **MONKS** → M-**O**-N-K-S → **O**
3. The infantry rank between Long Swordsman and Champion — **TWO-HANDED SWORDSMAN** → T-**W**-O... → **W**
4. A Guard Tower upgraded by a University researcher — **KEEP** → K-**E**-E-P → **E**
5. A counterweighted siege machine that targets buildings — **TREBUCHET** → T-**R**-E-B-U-C-H-E-T → **R**

**T — O — W — E — R**

The five reports describe building material, a conversion unit, an infantry upgrade, a tower's final form, and the machine designed to destroy towers. The word hidden in their second letters is the structure that connects all five: the thing built from the first, threatened by the second when it falls to conversion, defended by the third, upgraded into the fourth, and destroyed by the fifth.

## Omitted Trait Effect

Without the visual orientation, there is no spatial cue — no circled letter positions, no column alignment, no diagram showing which character to extract — so the extraction rule must be declared in plain language before the solver begins, which eliminates the discovery moment where a solver realizes on their own that something is hidden in a specific position. The puzzle still earns its surprise through narrative misdirection (five reports that seem to describe a battle in progress rather than a word puzzle) but the mechanism is given rather than found, which reduces the second layer of satisfaction that comes when a solver independently notices the structural trick.
