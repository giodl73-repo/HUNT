# Condition L-T6 — Artist minus Systematic Thinking

## Puzzle

**STANDING OVER**

Five things from Age of Empires II. Each one towers above everything else in its class — but only in a single, specific, surprising way.

---

The siege weapon that flings stone farther than any other engine of war can reach.

The machine that can erase its own landscape — cutting a path through solid forest — with the same blow it uses to kill.

The unit that carries more flesh, more bone, more sheer animal mass than any other thing that has ever walked a battlefield.

The lowly trash unit — no gold in its cost, no prestige in its name — that reliably defeats the most expensive archer in the game.

The object that earns gold while doing nothing. No villager assigned. No mine. Just silence, and income.

---

Take the first letter of what each one is.

Read down.

## Solution Key

Each statement identifies a specific AoE2 unit, weapon, or object by describing the one dimension in which it is the undisputed maximum of its class:

1. "Flings stone farther than any other engine of war" — **Trebuchet** (longest range of any siege weapon) → **T**
2. "Can erase its own landscape / cutting through solid forest" — **Onager** (Siege Onager can destroy trees) → **O**
3. "More mass than any other thing that has ever walked a battlefield" — **War Elephant** (600 HP elite, highest HP of any unit in the game) → **W**
4. "Lowly trash unit... that reliably defeats the most expensive archer" — **Elite Skirmisher** (hard-counters Arbalester despite costing no gold) → **E**
5. "Earns gold while doing nothing" — **Relic** (garrisoned in a Monastery, generates 30 gold/minute passively) → **R**

Reading the initial letters T-O-W-E-R spells **TOWER**.

## Omitted Trait Effect

Without systematic thinking, the extraction mechanic (take first letters, read down) is presented as a felt gesture rather than an explicit instruction — the solver is told to "read down" without being given a labeled column or grid to anchor to, which leaves the interface slightly underspecified. The five entries are chosen for their emotional punch (the Onager erasing its own forest is a genuinely strange image; the Relic earning gold in silence is counterintuitive) rather than for how cleanly they triangulate to unique answers, so a solver could plausibly misidentify entry 4 as "Skirmisher" vs. "Elite Skirmisher" or entry 5 as "Monastery" — the puzzle does not force a single reading through structural constraint.
