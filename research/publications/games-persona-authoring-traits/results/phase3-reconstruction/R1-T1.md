## Puzzle

**FIELD DISPATCHES**
*Five reports from the front. Each names a unit or technology from Age of Empires II.
Identify what is being described, then take the first letter of the second word of each answer.
Read those five letters in order.*

---

1. Researched at the University during the Castle Age, this upgrade transforms a Watch Tower into a sturdier defensive emplacement with greater attack and hit points.

2. The Imperial Age evolution of the Mangonel — so powerful it can level entire forest lines and shred any army foolish enough to mass in formation.

3. The Aztecs field this unique Castle-trained unit painted for battle, inflicting devastating bonus damage against enemy infantry.

4. The Persians send in their signature cavalry: the heaviest unit in the game, with 450 hit points, slow as a glacier, and trailing a wake of trample damage.

5. Painted in the dye of their ancestors and faster than any other infantry on the field, this Celtic unique unit closes ground before the enemy can react.

**Answer: TOWER**

---

**Why each clue points to its answer:**

1. *Guard Tower* — University tech (Castle Age), upgrades Watch Tower. From techs.md: "Guard Tower | Castle | 100F 250S | Upgrades Watch Tower → Guard Tower."

2. *Siege Onager* — Imperial Age upgrade of the Mangonel, noted in units.md as the counter to "Siege Onager, Huskarls" for Arbalesters; described as capable of cutting through trees.

3. *Jaguar Warrior* — Aztecs' unique unit. From units.md: "Jaguar Warrior | Aztecs | Infantry | 60F 30G | +11 bonus vs. infantry (elite); anti-infantry infantry."

4. *War Elephant* — Persians' unique unit. From units.md: "War Elephant | Persians | Cavalry | 200F 75G | 450 HP (elite: 600); trample; extremely slow."

5. *Woad Raider* — Celts' unique unit. From units.md: "Woad Raider | Celts | Infantry | 65F 25G | Speed 1.03 — fastest infantry in the game." Woad is the blue dye associated with ancient Celtic warriors.

---

**Solver experience notes:**

The mechanism is a single consistent rule — take the first letter of the second word — applied to all five answers. An AoE2 player will identify each entry quickly; the extraction is then immediate. The answer (TOWER) is thematically resonant: towers are a fundamental defensive structure in the game, and the puzzle's "field dispatch" framing evokes military reporting. The solver encounters infantry, cavalry, siege, and buildings — touching every major unit category — before the answer clicks into place.
