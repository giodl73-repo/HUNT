# Reconstruction R3 — T1 + T4 + T5 (Experience-First + Elegance + Rigor)
## Active traits: T1, T4, T5

## Puzzle

**CALIBER**

*For each clue, name the Age of Empires II entity described. A number in the clue tells you which letter of the answer to take (ignore spaces). Read those five letters in order.*

```
1. After a group conversion, only 1 Monk in the group needs to rest
   before converting again.

   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
   (1)


2. The 2nd upgrade in the Blacksmith's ranged attack chain, raising
   both attack and range by one step.

   _ _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _
     (2)


3. The Britons' unique archer; its elite form reaches range 7 —
   the longest of any archer in the game.

   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
               (7)


4. Their team bonus gives the Knight line +2 attack against archers.

   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _
     (2)


5. Their team bonus gives Knights +2 line of sight.

   _ _ _ _ _ _
     (2)
```

### Verification against world data

**THEOCRACY** — Monastery technology. Data: "Only one Monk in a group needs to rest after conversion." The clue lifts this mechanic directly. The number 1 is the mechanic: one Monk, not all. THEOCRACY: T-H-E-O-C-R-A-C-Y. Letter 1 = T. Source: techs.md (monastery technologies).

**BODKIN ARROW** — Blacksmith ranged attack upgrade, Castle Age. Data: the chain runs Fletching (Feudal) → Bodkin Arrow (Castle) → Bracer (Imperial), making Bodkin Arrow the second tier. Each tier gives "+1 atk, +1 range." The clue describes both facts. BODKINARROW (ignoring space): B-O-D-K-I-N-A-R-R-O-W. Letter 2 = O. Source: techs.md (blacksmith upgrades, ranged attack chain).

**LONGBOWMAN** — Britons unique unit. Data: "Longest range of any archer (6 base, 7 elite)." The clue cites the elite value, 7, as the number. LONGBOWMAN: L-O-N-G-B-O-W-M-A-N. Letter 7 = W. Source: civs.md (unique unit quick reference), units.md (notable unique units).

**PERSIANS** — Civilization. Data team bonus: "Knight line +2 attack vs. archers." The clue quotes this directly; 2 is the number. PERSIANS: P-E-R-S-I-A-N-S. Letter 2 = E. Source: civs.md (civilization table).

**FRANKS** — Civilization. Data team bonus: "Knights +2 line of sight." The clue quotes this directly; 2 is the number. FRANKS: F-R-A-N-K-S. Letter 2 = R. Source: civs.md (civilization table).

### Design notes

The extraction rule is stated: the number in each clue is the index. The challenge is pure AoE2 knowledge — naming each entity from its defining property.

The five numbers (1, 2, 7, 2, 2) are not invented for the puzzle. They are the game values: the one-Monk rest rule, the second tier in the upgrade chain, the elite archer's maximum range, and the two-point team bonuses that distinguish Persians and Franks. Each number is the smallest, most distinctive quantity associated with its entity.

The solver experience moves from monastery to blacksmith to unit to civilization, touching four different domains of the game before the answer appears. Clues 4 and 5 share the same structure ("their team bonus gives Knights +2...") — one for attack, one for line of sight — which rewards players who know both team bonuses and punishes guessing. No answer from R2 or R4 is reused.
