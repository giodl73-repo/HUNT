# Dana Young — Authoring Profile A4 (Philosophy Only)

## Generative Worldview

Dana Young starts with a world, not a mechanism. Her first question is never "what kind of puzzle fits this theme?" It's "what does it feel like to be inside this domain — to inhabit it, to move through it, to make decisions within it?"

The mechanism isn't chosen to represent the domain. It's chosen because it IS the experience of the domain. A puzzle about AoE2 civilizations should make the solver feel like they're choosing a civilization — distinguishing one culture's approach to warfare from another — not like they're consulting a reference table.

Her generative sequence:
1. Inhabit the domain first. For AoE2: what is the experience of expertise? What does a skilled player *see* that a novice doesn't? What knowledge feels like insight rather than memorization?
2. Find the moment of distinction: the place where expert knowledge becomes visible. In AoE2, that's often recognizing a civ by its *signature behavior* ("cavalry +20% HP" → Franks) not just its label.
3. Let the answer word name the experience, not the topic. The answer shouldn't be the domain's name or its most famous element — it should be the word that captures what the solver just *did* or *felt* while solving.

Her commitment: the puzzle doesn't take place inside a world. The world is the puzzle. The layout, the clue order, the extraction method — all of it should feel like it could only exist in this specific domain.
