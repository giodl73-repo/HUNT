# Condition A1 — Task Spec

## Puzzle

**FIRST PRINCIPLES**

*Five things every veteran commander knows. Identify each one. The first letter of each answer, read top to bottom, spells your final answer.*

1. The only generic siege unit that must be packed before it can move, fires in a high arc over walls, and deals full damage to stone fortifications at a range no other unit can match.

2. The upgrade to the Mangonel that gains a significantly wider blast radius — infamous among teamgame players for wiping out allies who stray too close to the red targeting ring.

3. The Celtic unique infantry unit, named for an ancient body-paint tradition, that moves faster than any other foot soldier in the game and trains at roughly the same cost as a Knight.

4. The foot unit that replaces the cavalry line for Aztec, Inca, and Maya — immune to the bonus damage that halberds and camels deal to horses, yet still counts as infantry for all other purposes.

5. The neutral object found scattered across the map in small groups; a Monk who carries one back to a monastery generates a trickle of gold for the rest of the game, making it a high-priority contest in Castle Age.

---

## Solution Key

Clue 1: Trebuchet → first letter: **T**
Clue 2: Onager → first letter: **O**
Clue 3: Woad Raider → first letter: **W**
Clue 4: Eagle Warrior → first letter: **E**
Clue 5: Relic → first letter: **R**
Answer: **TOWER**

## Design Notes

Each clue describes the item through its mechanical role and community reputation rather than its name, rewarding genuine game knowledge over a simple lookup — a veteran will recognize "packed before it can move" as the Trebuchet immediately, while a casual player would need to research it. The five items span all major game categories (siege, upgrade, unique unit, unit line, map object), giving the puzzle breadth and preventing pattern recognition from shortcutting the knowledge test. The answer TOWER has satisfying thematic resonance for an AoE2 puzzle: the Watch Tower line is a central defensive structure in the game and the target of many aggressive rush strategies. Clarity was prioritized by making each clue contain one unambiguous identifying detail (the packed/unpack mechanic, the wider blast radius, the Celtic body-paint etymology, the halberd/camel immunity exception, the monastery gold trickle) so that a solver who knows the game cannot reasonably misidentify any answer.
