# Condition A2 — Domain Expertise

## Puzzle

**Stone and Steel**

Five questions. Each answer is a specific unit, upgrade, or technology from Age of Empires II.
Take the first letter of each answer, in order, to spell a two-word defensive structure that
every civ can build.

---

1. The Franks' unique unit is classified as infantry — yet it attacks from range, hurling its
   namesake weapon over walls and past obstacles. What is this unit called?

2. A Castle Age siege weapon devastating against grouped archers and infantry, upgraded from the
   Mangonel in the Imperial Age, capable of cutting down trees on Black Forest. Name the upgrade.

3. The Persians field a cavalry unit with the highest hit points of any unit in the game — 600
   in its elite form — and deals trample damage, though it moves extremely slowly. Name this unit.

4. An upgraded archer-line unit available from the Castle Age that hard-counters other archers
   and costs food and wood (no gold). It is the natural answer to an enemy Crossbowman mass.
   Name this unit.

5. A Monastery technology costing 475 gold that extends the Monk's conversion ability to include
   buildings and siege weapons — not just living units. Name this technology.

---

## Solution Key

Clue 1: Throwing Axeman → first letter: T (verified in units.md)
Clue 2: Onager → first letter: O (verified in units.md)
Clue 3: War Elephant → first letter: W (verified in units.md and civs.md)
Clue 4: Elite Skirmisher → first letter: E (verified in units.md)
Clue 5: Redemption → first letter: R (verified in techs.md)
Answer: TOWER

## Design Notes

Each clue isolates a single verifiable data point — a unit's combat classification, an upgrade
tier, an HP value, a resource cost, a tech effect — so that an AoE2 player with genuine game
knowledge can arrive at exactly one answer per clue without guessing. The clues are ordered to
feel like a natural survey of the game's major systems (unique units, siege, cavalry, archers,
Monks), which mirrors the thematic breadth of the "Wololo" hunt without telegraphing the
acrostic structure. The answer TOWER is doubly appropriate: it is both a literal in-game
building (Watch Tower / Guard Tower / Keep) and the defensive shape the puzzle traces across
AoE2's five major unit and technology categories.
