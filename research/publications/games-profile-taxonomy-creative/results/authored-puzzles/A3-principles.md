# Condition A3 — Design Principles

## Puzzle

**CHAIN OF COMMAND**

*Five questions. Five answers. Read the first letter of each.*

1. The only Imperial Age siege unit constructed at the Castle building itself — not the Siege Workshop. It fires in an arcing trajectory, deals bonus damage to static defenses, and is hard-countered by fast cavalry.

2. The Imperial Age upgrade of the Mangonel. Unlike any other siege unit in the game, it can be ordered to target and destroy trees, opening new attack corridors through otherwise impassable forest.

3. The fastest infantry unit in the game, with a movement speed of 1.03. Its civilization's team bonus reduces the training time at the same workshop that produces the unit named in clue 2. Name the unit, not the civilization.

4. The Castle Age upgrade of the Skirmisher. It is one of three "trash unit" lines — costing no gold to train — and it hard-counters the unit class that it itself evolved from.

5. The Monastery technology that extends a Monk's conversion power to buildings and siege weapons. Before researching this, Monks can only convert living units.

---

## Solution Key

Clue 1: **Trebuchet** → first letter: T
Clue 2: **Onager** → first letter: O
Clue 3: **Woad Raider** → first letter: W
Clue 4: **Elite Skirmisher** → first letter: E
Clue 5: **Redemption** → first letter: R

Answer: **TOWER**

## Principle Compliance Check

1. **No Deliberate Errors**: Every fact is drawn directly from the domain data files — the Trebuchet's production building (Castle), the Onager's tree-targeting ability, the Woad Raider's speed of 1.03, the Elite Skirmisher's trash-unit status and counter relationship, and Redemption's effect on conversion targets are all verifiable in units.md, techs.md, and civs.md.

2. **Blame the Player**: Each clue describes a genuine AoE2 mechanic in plain terms; once a solver sees TOWER, the path is clean and retrospectively obvious — no misdirection, no trick phrasing to blame the designer for.

3. **Solving = Proving Understanding**: A solver who correctly identifies all five items has demonstrated knowledge of production buildings, siege mechanics, unit speed values, economic unit classifications, and Monastery tech trees — a meaningful cross-section of AoE2 systems.

4. **No Over-Scaffolding**: The instruction "read the first letter of each" appears once at the top and is not repeated; solvers who know first-letter extraction do not need it spelled out, and those who do not have a fair chance to discover it.

5. **Surprise the Answer**: The five clues span siege, forest mechanics, infantry speed, economic units, and Monk tech — there is no surface-level theme pointing toward defensive structures, so TOWER arrives as genuinely surprising yet inevitable.

6. **Reading Reward >= 4**: Each clue requires recalling a specific AoE2 mechanic (Trebuchet's production building, Onager's tree-cutting, Woad Raider's exact speed, Elite Skirmisher's trash-unit status, Redemption's specific effect); a non-player Googling "AoE2 fastest infantry" or "AoE2 Monastery tech buildings" would find answers but would not know which result was the target without deep familiarity.

7. **One Aha, Not Zero, Not Three**: The single aha moment is recognizing that the first letters of five disparate AoE2 items spell a word — the mechanism is unified and fires once, not in stages.

8. **The Dinner Party Test**: Clue 2 is the standout — "siege units that can destroy trees" is a specific, surprising, and verifiable AoE2 fact that makes non-players say "wait, a catapult can chop down forests?"; it passes the test.

9. **No Computation Without Deduction**: Every clue requires recognizing the correct AoE2 item from its properties; no clue asks the solver to calculate a value or apply a formula — the answer is always a named game entity.

10. **Verify the Last Mile**: T is the first letter of Trebuchet; O is the first letter of Onager; W is the first letter of Woad Raider; E is the first letter of Elite Skirmisher; R is the first letter of Redemption — each extraction step is a direct initial, verified individually.

11. **Interlock, Not Independence**: Clue 3 explicitly references "the workshop that produces the unit named in clue 2," creating a hard dependency: a solver cannot confirm the Woad Raider's civilization team bonus without first knowing that clue 2's answer (Onager) trains at the Siege Workshop — the two clues constrain each other.

---

## Design Notes

The puzzle is built around a deliberate collision of game systems — siege production, environmental destruction, unit speed, economic classification, and religious conversion — so the five items feel unrelated until the extraction reveals TOWER. The interlock between clues 2 and 3 is the structural backbone: because clue 3 names the Siege Workshop indirectly through the team bonus, a solver who gets the Onager early can use that as confirmation when identifying the Woad Raider's civilization, and vice versa. The title CHAIN OF COMMAND does mild misdirection work, suggesting military hierarchy rather than a letter chain, without being dishonest. Clue 4's phrasing ("hard-counters the unit class it evolved from") rewards solvers who understand the Skirmisher's own upgrade lineage — archers beget Elite Skirmishers which destroy archers — a small recursive surprise inside the larger extraction.
