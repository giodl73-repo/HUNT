# Condition A4 — Authoring Philosophy (Snyder)

## Puzzle

**BREACH**

*Five entries. Five defenses broken. Take the first letter of each.*

1. The only siege unit in the game that must unpack before it can fire, and packs up to retreat — incapable of attacking while moving.
2. The Imperial-Age upgrade that, unlike any other siege engine, can destroy trees to cut new paths through a forest.
3. The Celts' unique unit: the fastest infantry in the game, with a movement speed of 1.03.
4. The Archery Range unit that is specifically designed to hard-counter other archers — its entire purpose is suppression of the archer line.
5. The Monastery technology that extends conversion to buildings and siege weapons, not just military units.

---

## Solution Key

Clue 1: TREBUCHET (must unpack to fire; cannot attack while mobile) → first letter: **T**
Clue 2: ONAGER (Imperial upgrade of Mangonel; uniquely destroys trees) → first letter: **O**
Clue 3: WOAD RAIDER (Celts unique infantry; speed 1.03 — fastest infantry in game) → first letter: **W**
Clue 4: ELITE SKIRMISHER (counter-archer unit of the Archery Range) → first letter: **E**
Clue 5: REDEMPTION (Monastery tech; enables conversion of buildings and siege) → first letter: **R**

Answer: **TOWER**

---

## Philosophy Application

The Snyder approach demanded identifying a single deduction — "the first letter of each item spells TOWER" — before any clue was written, then building each constraint to make that identification necessary and sufficient. Every clue describes exactly one item in the AoE2 database: removing any phrase ("must unpack before it can fire," "unlike any other siege engine," "speed of 1.03") either makes the answer ambiguous or leaves the first-letter sequence broken. The mechanism is the domain's own logic: the puzzle teaches something structural about AoE2 (how the Trebuchet's pack/unpack mechanic differs from all other siege; how the Onager's tree-cutting is unique; what the Skirmisher line is actually for) rather than using AoE2 trivia as decoration. Without this philosophy, the puzzle would likely have used broader clues — "a siege weapon that beats buildings" could yield three answers — and the aha moment would be weakened or lost.

---

## Design Notes

The five items were chosen to span all major knowledge domains in AoE2 (siege, unique units, generic military, technology) so that solving the puzzle is also a structured tour of the game's system. The title BREACH is a double signal: it names the thematic spine (each item is a tool for breaking through a defense) and invites the solver to suspect that the answer itself relates to defensive structures — which TOWER confirms.
