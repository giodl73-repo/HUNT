# Condition A5-Katz — Full Katz Authoring Profile

## Puzzle

**Five clues. Each clue identifies a specific Age of Empires II unit, technology, structure, or mechanic. Take the first letter of each answer, in order, to spell the solution.**

---

**1.**
The Franks' unique unit is technically infantry — trained at the Castle, attacks on foot — but it never closes to melee. Its damage comes from the weapon it releases.

**2.**
Upgraded from the Mangonel in the Imperial Age, this siege engine is the only military unit in the game that can destroy trees — opening new corridors through Black Forest when walls and choke points aren't enough.

**3.**
Every civilization has one, architecturally distinct and massively expensive. Complete construction and survive the countdown and you win — no enemies killed required.

**4.**
Aztec and Mayan armies have no horses at all. This unit fills the cavalry role instead: fast-moving, costs no gold at its final upgrade tier, and unusually effective against Monks.

**5.**
Once a Monk researches this Monastery technology, an enemy Ram or Trebuchet is as convertible as any soldier. The opponent's own siege line can walk into your camp.

---

E: **Eagle Warrior** — verified: AoE2 DE game knowledge (Aztec/Maya/Inca unique unit replacing Scout Cavalry line; costs no gold at Eagle Warrior / Elite Eagle Warrior tier; strong vs. Monks and cavalry-counters; not listed in local units.md which covers 8-civ scope only)
R: **Redemption** — verified: techs.md (Monastery technology; cost 475G; "Monks can convert buildings and siege weapons")

Answer: TOWER

## Construction Log

**Answer word test (Katz step 1).**
TOWER is given. Test: is TOWER surprising for an AoE2 puzzle? Marginally — the Watch Tower / Guard Tower / Keep chain means "tower" lives in the game's vocabulary, and the building called a Tower is a common defensive structure. Risk: the answer is not invisible. Mitigation: the puzzle's mechanism (first letters of five disparate items) distances the word from the Tower building. None of the five clues are about towers or tower-related content. The word arrives as a first-letter pattern, not as a topical answer. That structural gap is enough.

**Short-circuit check (Katz step 4).**
After clues 1–2 (T, O): no short-circuit — TO__ has too many completions. After clue 3 (W): TOW_ is strongly suggestive of TOWER, especially for an AoE2 solver who knows the building. This is the point where the solver suspects the answer. Mitigation: clue 4 (Eagle Warrior) is the least expected item — an E-starting cavalry-replacement unit from Mesoamerican civs. A solver who jumped to TOWER at W will spend time on clue 4 confirming the E, which earns the confirmation rather than handing it. Clue 5 (Redemption) is then the "delayed confirmation" per Katz's signature: clean, satisfying, makes the solver think "of course."

**Variety across clue types (Katz step 5).**
Clue 1: unique unit (Franks) — knowledge of civ-specific paradox (ranged infantry).
Clue 2: siege unit — knowledge of upgrade chain and map interaction.
Clue 3: building / alternate win condition — knowledge of game rules, not combat units.
Clue 4: unique unit (Mesoamerican civs) — knowledge of civ design philosophy (no horses).
Clue 5: technology — knowledge of Monastery research tree.
Consecutive check: unit → siege → building/mechanic → unit → technology. No two consecutive clues draw on the same category. The unit-to-unit gap (clues 1 and 4) is separated by two intervening categories, making the repetition non-consecutive.
