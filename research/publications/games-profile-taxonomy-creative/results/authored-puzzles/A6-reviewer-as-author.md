# Condition A6 — Reviewer Profile as Author (Snyder C8)

## Puzzle

**ESCALATING FORCE**

*Five facts about Age of Empires II. Identify the specific unit, upgrade, or technology described by each clue. The first letter of each answer, read top to bottom, spells a five-letter word.*

1. This Imperial Age siege engine cannot fire while moving — it must be unpacked into a stationary firing position first. It costs 200 wood and 200 gold to train, and deals bonus damage against buildings and static defenses.

2. This Imperial Age upgrade to the Mangonel gains a capability its predecessor lacks: the ability to destroy trees, cutting new corridors through forests like Black Forest.

3. The Celtic unique unit holds the highest movement speed of any infantry in the game — 1.03 tiles per second — making it the fastest foot soldier in Age of Empires II.

4. This trash unit — the Castle Age upgrade of the Skirmisher — counters archers with a direct attack bonus and costs no gold to produce, unlike its target.

5. This Monastery technology costs 475 gold and extends a Monk's conversion ability beyond enemy soldiers to include buildings and siege weapons.

---

## Solution Key

Clue 1: **Trebuchet** → first letter: T
Clue 2: **Onager** → first letter: O
Clue 3: **Woad Raider** → first letter: W
Clue 4: **Elite Skirmisher** → first letter: E
Clue 5: **Redemption** → first letter: R
Answer: **TOWER**

---

## Reviewer-as-Author Application

The most consequential review question was "Is each element load-bearing?" — applied during authoring, it became a constraint to add: every factual detail in a clue must eliminate at least one plausible wrong answer. Clue 1's pack/unpack mechanic alone identifies the Trebuchet, so the cost (200W/200G) was nearly cut; it survived because it gives the solver an independent verification path rather than forcing trust in a single remembered detail, satisfying the companion question "Can the solver confirm each step independently?" Clue 4 required the most iteration: "Skirmisher" and "Elite Skirmisher" are genuinely different units, so the phrase "Castle Age upgrade of the Skirmisher" was required to force the Elite version — without that anchor, the clue admits two answers, which Snyder's "uniqueness problem at the structural level" lens flags as a construction failure. The review question that felt most awkward transposed to authoring was "Does the theme shape the structure, or sit on top of it?" — for an acrostic extraction puzzle, the answer word (TOWER) is structurally inert relative to the five clues; the theme cannot shape which items are chosen without artificially constraining the letter search. The honest answer is that this format has a structural limit: the extraction mechanism and the domain content are loosely coupled by design, and Snyder's coherence standard exposes that gap rather than resolving it.

## Design Notes

All five identified items appear in the domain data files (units.md, techs.md) with exact figures, so every clue can be verified against documented sources rather than community-wiki memory. The title "Escalating Force" is minimal and referential — the five items move from a siege weapon (Trebuchet) through siege-adjacent units (Onager, Woad Raider) into economy-adjacent mechanics (Elite Skirmisher as trash unit, Redemption as gold-only tech) — but this arc is thematic texture, not structural dependency, which is the honest limit of the format.
