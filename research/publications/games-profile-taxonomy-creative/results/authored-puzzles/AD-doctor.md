## Solution Key

### Answers

| # | Clue subject | Answer | Shaded letter |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Siege engine that packs/unpacks, long range, destroys Castles | **TREBUCHET** | **T** |
| 2 | Siege Workshop upgrade to Mangonel; area-of-effect boulders | **ONAGER** | **O** |
| 3 | Celtic unique Castle unit; name references blue battle-paint dye | **WOAD RAIDER** | **W** |
| 4 | Mesoamerican cavalry replacement; infantry that beats cavalry | **EAGLE WARRIOR** | **E** |
| 5 | Chinese Imperial Age unique tech; adds projectiles to Chu Ko Nu | **ROCKETRY** | **R** |

### Extraction

Taking the **first letter** of each answer in order:

> **T** (Trebuchet) + **O** (Onager) + **W** (Woad Raider) + **E** (Eagle Warrior) + **R** (Rocketry)

= **TOWER**

### Thematic Confirmation

The answer TOWER is deeply embedded in *Age of Empires II* gameplay. Towers are a central defensive structure — the Watch Tower upgrades through Guard Tower to Keep — and are a defining feature of the castle-building, siege-and-defend loop that defines the game. The puzzle title "Siege and Stone" signals both the siege-engine clues and the defensive architecture of the answer.

### Fact-Check Notes

- **TREBUCHET**: Available from Castle Age onward; must be packed/unpacked to move; primary building-demolition tool at range.
- **ONAGER**: Upgraded from Mangonel at the Siege Workshop (Castle Age); further upgrades to Siege Onager; famous for friendly-fire incidents.
- **WOAD RAIDER**: Celtic unique unit; woad (*Isatis tinctoria*) is a real plant used as blue dye; the unit is unusually fast for infantry.
- **EAGLE WARRIOR**: Available to Aztecs and Mayans in the base game (Incas added in *The Forgotten* DLC); infantry with anti-cavalry bonus; no stable access for these civs.
- **ROCKETRY**: Chinese Imperial Age unique technology; grants +2 projectiles to Chu Ko Nu and +1 projectile to Scorpion and Hand Cannoneer per volley.
