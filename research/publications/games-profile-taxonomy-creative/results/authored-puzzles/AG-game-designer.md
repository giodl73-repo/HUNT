# Condition AG — Game Designer

## Puzzle

**IMPERIAL ROSTER**

Each clue below describes a unit or structure from *Age of Empires II*. Identify all five, then read the first letter of each answer in order to find the puzzle's answer.

---

**1.**
This Imperial Age siege weapon must be unpacked before it can fire. It launches stones at extreme range, making it the premier tool for dismantling enemy Castles and Town Centers. Unlike most siege units, it deals very little damage against units — it is built to crack fortifications, not armies. It is trained at the Siege Workshop and requires a Castle to be constructed first.

`_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _`

---

**2.**
This siege unit is the area-of-effect specialist of the Siege Workshop. Its projectile explodes on impact, hitting every unit within a radius — making it devastating against dense formations of archers or infantry. It is particularly effective against Skirmishers and other units that tend to clump together. Hussars are its primary counter because they can charge into minimum range before it fires.

`_ _ _ _ _ _`

---

**3.**
This massive cavalry unit is exclusive to the Persian civilization (and a handful of Dynasties of India civilizations in the Definitive Edition). It boasts one of the highest hit point totals of any unit in the game, but moves slowly. It deals trample damage to all units nearby when it attacks. Its principal weakness is the Halberdier, which receives a large attack bonus against it.

`_ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _`

---

**4.**
This infantry unit is unique to Native American civilizations in the game — Aztecs, Mayans, and Incas. It fills the cavalry role for those civilizations, which cannot train the Knight-line. It moves faster than a standard infantry unit and receives an attack bonus against cavalry. It is one of the few infantry units that can effectively chase down and kill mounted units.

`_ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _ _`

---

**5.**
This Castle Age siege unit gains additional speed and attack power when infantry are garrisoned inside it. It has a massive hit point pool and cannot be harmed by arrows due to its Siege armor class. It deals a bonus against buildings. It is the affordable, mobile alternative to the Trebuchet for knocking down walls and towers in a direct assault.

`_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _`

---

Read the **first letter** of each answer (1–5) to spell the puzzle's answer.

---

## Solution Key

**Answers and extraction:**

| # | Answer | First Letter | Verification |
|---|--------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | TREBUCHET | **T** | Imperial Age siege; must unpack before firing; long range vs buildings; Siege Workshop + Castle required |
| 2 | ONAGER | **O** | Area-of-effect siege; upgraded from Mangonel; counters massed archers and infantry; minimum range exploited by Hussars |
| 3 | WAR ELEPHANT | **W** | Persian cavalry unit; among highest HP in the game; trample damage; countered by Halberdiers who receive +32 bonus attack vs it |
| 4 | EAGLE WARRIOR | **E** | Native American civilizations only (Aztecs, Mayans, Incas); replaces Knight-line; fast infantry with bonus vs cavalry |
| 5 | BATTERING RAM | **R** | Castle Age siege; infantry garrison to boost speed and attack; immune to arrows (siege armor class); building demolition specialist |

**First letters: T — O — W — E — R**

**Answer: TOWER**

---

**Thematic note:** The answer TOWER is the signature defensive structure of AoE2 — Watch Tower, Guard Tower, Keep — and the thing that Battering Rams and Trebuchets are specifically deployed to destroy. The puzzle traces a complete picture of the game's siege warfare ecosystem, from the long-range Trebuchet to the ram that breaks down the very structures the answer names.
