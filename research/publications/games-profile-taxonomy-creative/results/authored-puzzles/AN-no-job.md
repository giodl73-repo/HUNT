**Clue accuracy notes**

- **TWO-HANDED SWORDSMAN**: The standard infantry upgrade chain in AoE2 is Militia (Dark Age) → Man-at-Arms (Feudal) → Long Swordsman (Castle) → Two-Handed Swordsman (Imperial) → Champion (Imperial). Two-Handed Swordsman is the fourth of five tiers. Most civilizations have access to it; a small number are cut off before Champion.
- **MANGONEL**: Trained at the Siege Workshop beginning in the Castle Age. The Mangonel line (Mangonel → Onager → Siege Onager) is distinct from the Ballista/Scorpion line; both are available from the Siege Workshop but serve different counter roles.
- **TOWN CENTER**: In every standard AoE2 game mode, each player begins with one Town Center already placed. It cannot be built in the Dark Age; additional Town Centers can be constructed from the Feudal Age onward. Losing the last Town Center is not an automatic defeat condition (unlike some other RTS titles), but it cripples economy and Age advancement.
- **TREBUCHET**: The Trebuchet is the only AoE2 military unit with a pack/unpack toggle mechanic. Packed, it moves like a slow civilian unit. Unpacked, it fires at range 16 (or 17 with Warwolf, Britons bonus) and deals full damage to stone fortifications. It is available in the Imperial Age and built directly at a Castle.
- **SKIRMISHER**: The Skirmisher (and Elite Skirmisher) is the primary counter to Crossbowmen and Arbalests. Its javelin deals +3 bonus damage against archers (Elite: +4). Cost: 25F 35W — no gold requirement, making it sustainable in long games.
