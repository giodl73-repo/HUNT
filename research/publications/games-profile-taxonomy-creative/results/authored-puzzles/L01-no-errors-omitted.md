# Condition L01 — Leave Out: No Deliberate Errors

## Puzzle

**FIELD ORDERS**

Five units were deployed. Each left a record. Read the records. Find the pattern. Report what they spell.

---

1. The Celts field this unit at speed 1.03 — faster than any infantry in the game. It charges straight into the enemy's archers before they can react.

2. Upgrading from the Mangonel, this machine no longer just shreds grouped archers. Its projectile is heavy enough to topple trees and cut new corridors through Black Forest walls.

3. A unique ability unlocked at the Monastery: after researching this technology, your Monks can convert not just soldiers but the enemy's siege equipment and fortifications.

4. The counter to archers. Cheap to train, costs no gold, throws a javelin that outranges the Archer's own shot. Its Elite upgrade makes it viable all the way into Imperial.

5. This Imperial Age engine fires from outside castle range, dismantles stone walls tile by tile, and cannot be rushed — only outranged by another of its kind.

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet (siege unit — Imperial Age, destroys buildings from outside castle range)
O: Onager (siege unit — Mangonel upgrade, projectile heavy enough to cut trees)
W: Woad Raider (Celts unique unit — speed 1.03, fastest infantry in the game)
E: Elite Skirmisher (archer-line unit — gold-free counter to archers, javelin range beats Archer)
R: Redemption (Monastery technology — lets Monks convert buildings and siege weapons)

Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Omitting "No Deliberate Errors" created no material design change: all five items (Trebuchet, Onager, Woad Raider, Elite Skirmisher, Redemption) are core AoE2 facts well within reliable recall, and every detail in the clues — Woad Raider speed 1.03, Onager tree-cutting, Redemption enabling siege conversion, Skirmisher gold cost — is standard knowledge unlikely to be wrong. Had verification been required, the clue details would have been identical; the only risk would have been catching a subtle stat error that did not in fact occur.
