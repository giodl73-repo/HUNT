# Condition L03 — Leave Out: The Riven Standard

## Puzzle

Five items from Age of Empires II. Identify each one. The first letters, read in order, name something found in every base.

---

**1.**
An Imperial Age siege weapon costing 200 wood and 200 gold. Trained from the Castle rather than the Siege Workshop. Cavalry and Bombard Cannons are its natural counters.

**2.**
The Imperial Age upgrade to the Castle Age Mangonel. Costs 160 wood and 135 gold. Its projectile clears trees and destroys grouped units alike.

`_ _ _ _ _ _`

**3.**
The unique unit of the civilization whose team bonus causes the workshop that trains clue 2's unit to operate 20% faster. This foot soldier moves at speed 1.03 — the fastest of any infantry in the game.

R: Redemption
Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

The Riven Standard requires the puzzle mechanism to emerge from the subject matter itself — solving it should prove the solver understands how AoE2 works, not merely that they can apply an acrostic template to any domain. Omitting this principle had a direct design consequence: the mechanism (first-letter acrostic) was chosen for its elegance as a generic puzzle form rather than because AoE2 knowledge uniquely enables it. A Riven-compliant version would require the solver to demonstrate understanding that could only come from AoE2 — for example, navigating a tech tree dependency chain, reading a counter triangle, or tracing a cost progression — rather than identifying items and extracting letters.
