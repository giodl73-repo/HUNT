## Solution Key

Answer: TOWER

**Extraction trace (letter by letter):**

- Clue 1 → **T**rebuchet → T
- Clue 2 → **O**nager → O
- Clue 3 → **W**oad Raider → W
- Clue 4 → **E**lite Skirmisher → E
- Clue 5 → **R**edemption → R
- Reading top to bottom: T-O-W-E-R

**Interlock verification:**

Clue 2 identifies the Onager and names Celts as the civilization that fires siege 25% faster. Clue 3 asks for the unique unit of the civilization named in Clue 2, requiring the solver to carry the Celts identification forward to reach Woad Raider. Solving either clue in isolation leaves ambiguity; solving both together eliminates it. This satisfies Interlock, Not Independence.

**Fact verification (No Deliberate Errors):**

- Trebuchet: 200W 200G, Imperial Age, cannot move and fire simultaneously, primary building-destroyer. Source: units.md.
- Onager: Imperial Age upgrade of Mangonel, 160W 135G, shreds grouped units and trees, weak vs. cavalry. Celts: siege weapons fire 25% faster. Source: units.md, civs.md.
- Woad Raider: Celts unique unit, infantry, speed 1.03 (fastest infantry), trained at Castle. Source: civs.md, units.md.
- Elite Skirmisher: Castle Age upgrade of Skirmisher (Feudal), costs 25F 35W, no gold, counters Archer/Crossbowman lines, hard-countered by Knights. Source: units.md.
- Redemption: 475G, no other cost, enables Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons, no prerequisite. Source: techs.md.

**Surprise the Answer check:**

The puzzle uses siege units, a fast infantry unit, and a religious technology. Nothing in the surface content points toward "TOWER" — a defensive building. The irony is that you have assembled a tower out of the weapons used to destroy towers. A solver expecting SIEGE, MONK, or CELTS will pause.

**Reading Reward assessment:**

A solver who has never played AoE2 cannot solve Clue 2 (Celts' specific siege bonus requires domain knowledge), Clue 3 (unique unit requires knowing civ-unit pairings), or Clue 5 (Redemption's exact gold cost and effect require Monastery tech knowledge). Ctrl+F on a name index fails because the clues describe function, cost, and relationship — not name. Reading Reward: 4.

## Omission Notes

The "No Over-Scaffolding" principle is deliberately omitted in this condition. The puzzle provides a full mechanism explanation, a worked example with a non-answer item, labeled answer blanks with letter-count guides, a separate letter-assembly grid, and an explicit hint identifying the interlock between Clues 2 and 3. A version applying the principle would strip the mechanism explanation, remove the worked example, collapse the blanks to a single answer line, and omit the hint — leaving the solver to discover the first-letter extraction independently.
