# Condition L06 — Leave Out: Surprise the Answer

## Puzzle

**Five units and technologies define a tower attack.**
**Each description identifies one AoE2 item — a civilization, unit, technology, or map.**
**The first letter of each answer, read top to bottom, gives you the strategy.**

---

1. It outranges a Keep. It cannot fire while moving. Wheel it back one tile when the enemy cavalry charges, or lose it.

2. Its projectile does not stop at the first target. On Black Forest, it cuts through trees to open corridors no axe can make.

3. The Celts built it for speed. No other infantry unit in the game crosses ground faster. It arrives before the gate closes.

4. One gold in its cost. It trains at the Archery Range. Send it at any archer line and the archers scatter or die.

5. Without this Monastery research, your Monks stand helpless in front of a Battering Ram. With it, the Ram becomes yours.

---

*Write the first letter of each answer in order.*

---

## Solution Key

T: **Trebuchet** — Imperial Age siege unit; outranges all towers and Castles (range 16 vs. Keep's 9); must unpack to fire; minimum range of 4 tiles; vulnerable to cavalry charges while deployed.

O: **Onager** — Imperial Age siege unit upgraded from Mangonel; projectile passes through multiple targets; the only siege unit whose blast can fell trees and clear new corridors through forest maps like Black Forest.

W: **Woad Raider** — Celts unique unit; speed 1.03, the fastest infantry unit in the game; trained at the Castle from Castle Age onward; the Celts' infantry-speed bonus compounds with the Woad Raider's base speed.

E: **Elite Skirmisher** — Castle Age upgrade of the Skirmisher; costs 25F 35W (no gold); trained at the Archery Range; hard counter to all archers including Crossbowmen and Arbalesters.

R: **Redemption** — Monastery technology; costs 475G; allows Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons (base Monks can only convert military units); turns enemy Battering Rams and siege into your own.

**Answer: TOWER**

---

## Omission Notes

The "Surprise the Answer" principle is deliberately omitted here. All five clues describe elements of a tower rush or tower siege (a Trebuchet attack on a fortified position, Onager corridor-cutting, Woad Raider flanking speed, Elite Skirmisher screens, Redemption converting enemy siege) — a solver familiar with AoE2 can plausibly anticipate TOWER before completing all five clues. This is the condition being tested: whether telegraphing the answer word degrades or enhances the puzzle experience.
