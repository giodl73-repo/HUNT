# Condition L07 — Leave Out: Reading Reward ≥ 4

## Puzzle

**KNOWN QUANTITIES**

Five clues. Each answer is an Age of Empires II item — a civilization, unit, technology, or map. Take the first letter of each answer in order.

---

1. Named after the famous counterweight catapult used in real medieval warfare, this Imperial Age siege unit fires from outside castle range and is used to demolish enemy fortifications.

2. Named after the wild donkey of the ancient Near East and the Roman-era stone-throwing war machine that shared its name, this Castle Age siege unit upgrades from the Mangonel.

3. The Persians' unique unit is a real animal that fought in ancient armies from India to Carthage. In the game it has the highest HP of any trainable unit and moves very slowly.

4. Mesoamerican civilizations — Aztecs, Mayans — cannot train Scout Cavalry. Instead, their fast early-game scout replacement is named after a real Aztec warrior class.

5. A technology researched at the Monastery. Its name is a theological term meaning deliverance from sin. It allows Monks to convert enemy siege weapons and buildings, not just soldiers.

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet
O: Onager
W: War Elephant
E: Eagle Warrior
R: Redemption
Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Omitting Reading Reward ≥ 4 produces clues that are solvable by surface-level reference lookup: each clue anchors to a real-world name origin, a civ label, or a unit class visible in any AoE2 wiki summary, so a non-player can confirm each answer by Googling the description alone. The strategic reasoning required in P07 — understanding which siege unit cuts trees, which unit's HP creates a Monk vulnerability, which Monastery tech specifically targets structures — is entirely absent here, reducing the puzzle to a trivia lookup rather than a demonstration of game understanding.
