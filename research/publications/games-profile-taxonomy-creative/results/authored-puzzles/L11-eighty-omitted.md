# Condition L11 — Leave Out: The 80% Rule

## Puzzle

**FIVE ITEMS**

Each clue identifies a specific item from Age of Empires II: a civilization, unit, technology,
or map. Name the item. Take its first letter. The five letters, in order, spell the answer.

---

1. A Monastery technology that changes group behavior, not individual ability. Before it,
   every Monk in a formation must rest after a conversion — including the ones who did nothing.
   After it, only the Monk who performed the conversion rests. The rest keep working.
   Cost: 200 food, 100 gold.

2. A siege unit available in the Imperial Age. It is an upgrade of the Mangonel. It costs
   the same as the Mangonel. Its projectile is heavy enough to destroy trees — which means
   a player can order it to cut new corridors through a map like Black Forest. Against
   grouped units it is devastating. Against cavalry it falls quickly.

3. The unique unit of a civilization whose Town Centers and Docks accelerate in
   efficiency with each Age advance — a bonus that rewards a player who commits to
   economy over aggression. The unit itself costs 200 food and 75 gold. In elite form
   it reaches 600 hit points — the highest of any trainable unit in the game. It
   deals trample damage to everything it passes through. It moves slowly enough that
   a Halberdier can close the gap and destroy it before it reaches the archers it was
   sent to kill. The irony: it is classified as cavalry, and Halberdiers exist
   precisely to destroy cavalry.

4. An archer-class unit that costs no gold at any tier. Its attack upgrades in the
   Blacksmith alongside archers of all kinds. Yet its design purpose is the opposite
   of the units it shares upgrade chains with: it exists to kill them. Research the
   final upgrade in the armor chain described in clue 5 and this unit becomes harder
   to remove — but it was already the answer to any massed-archer formation.
   Name the Castle Age upgrade, not the base unit.

5. The third and final upgrade in the Blacksmith's ranged armor chain. Available in the
   Imperial Age. It adds the same +1 melee and +1 pierce armor as the two tiers before
   it. Its cost is 250 food and 250 gold — the most expensive armor upgrade in the chain
   and the only one symmetric in food and gold. The unit hardest to remove with this
   upgrade researched is the one named in clue 4.

---

Take the first letter of each answer, clues 1 through 5.

**Answer:** _ _ _ _ _

---

## Solution Key

T: Theocracy (Monastery technology — 200F 100G; only the converting Monk rests, not the whole group)
O: Onager (siege unit — Imperial Age Mangonel upgrade, same cost as Mangonel, projectile fells trees)
W: War Elephant (Persian unique unit — 200F 75G, 600 HP elite, trample damage, classified cavalry, countered by Halberdiers)
E: Elite Skirmisher (archer-line unit — Castle Age upgrade, no gold cost, hard counter to archers, shares Blacksmith upgrade chain)
R: Ring Archer Armor (Blacksmith technology — Imperial Age, +1/+1 armor, 250F 250G, final tier of ranged armor chain)

Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Without The 80% Rule, the five clues are designed to defeat pattern-completion: TOWER is not recoverable from any four letters because the answer space for T_WER, TO_ER, TOW_R, and TOWE_ each contains multiple plausible five-letter words, and the items (Theocracy, Onager, War Elephant, Elite Skirmisher, Ring Archer Armor) are individually obscure enough that no single clue telegraphs its initial letter to a casual player. The puzzle also adds cross-clue interlock — clue 4 names the unit from clue 5 and vice versa — so that the two hardest identifications (E and R) must be solved together rather than independently, directly exploiting the absence of the 80% safeguard.
