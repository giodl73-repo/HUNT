# Condition L12 — Leave Out: Discovered, Never Explained

## Puzzle

Five Age of Empires II items are described below. Identify each one. The first letter of each answer, read top to bottom, spells the five-letter answer word.

---

1. The Franks' unique unit. Ranged infantry trained at the Castle — a rarity in a game where ranged units are almost always archers on foot or mounted. It throws its weapon, which means it can hit targets behind walls that would block a melee soldier's path entirely.

   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _

2. The Imperial Age upgrade researched at the Siege Workshop. It inherits its predecessor's exceptional ability: no other unit in the game can cut down trees, and in Black Forest this makes it a map-rewriting weapon. It costs only wood and gold — no food.

   _ _ _ _ _ _

3. The Persian unique unit. It holds the record for most hit points of any non-building unit in the game — 600 in its elite form. It moves at walking pace, and every step deals collateral damage to anything standing adjacent to its target.

   _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

4. The cheapest answer to an enemy archer mass. Costs only food and wood. Available after Castle Age research at the Archery Range. It is purpose-built to trade favorably against ranged units while spending no gold at all.

   _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

5. The third and final tier of Blacksmith armor for the unit class that clue 2 specializes in destroying. Researched in Imperial Age. It costs both food and gold and gives the protected units their last increment of pierce and melee defense.

   _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _ _   _ _ _ _ _

---

## Solution Key

T: Throwing Axeman
O: Onager
W: War Elephant
E: Elite Skirmisher
R: Ring Archer Armor

Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Without the upfront instruction to read first letters, the solver's single aha — writing down all five answers and watching TOWER emerge — is replaced by a slower, more deliberate confirmation process. Stating the mechanism converts discovery into verification, which lowers engagement but removes the possibility of a solver finishing all five clues and missing the extraction entirely.
