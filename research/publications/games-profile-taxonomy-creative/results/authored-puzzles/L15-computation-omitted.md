# Condition L15 — Leave Out: No Computation Without Deduction

## Puzzle

**FIVE ANSWERS**

Each clue identifies exactly one Age of Empires II item — a civilization, unit, technology, or map. Identify each item. Take the first letter of each answer in order. Read downward.

---

1. An Imperial Age siege unit. Its wood cost is 200. No other siege unit in the game costs exactly 200 wood to build. To attack, it must first stop moving and deploy. A deployed one cannot be moved without packing up again.

2. An Imperial Age siege unit. Its wood cost matches the Mangonel exactly. Its Imperial upgrade cost to train is identical to its Castle Age predecessor's training cost — same food, same wood, same gold, same numbers. It is the only siege unit able to fell trees and convert forest into open terrain.

3. A unique unit. The food required to train it exceeds the food required to train a Jaguar Warrior by exactly five. The civilization that produces it gives its infantry a movement bonus and its lumberjacks a work rate bonus — both bonuses share the same percentage value. It is the fastest infantry unit in the game. Its gold training cost equals the food training cost of the answer to clue 4.

4. An archer-class unit first available in Castle Age. It costs no gold to train. It hard-counters other foot archers of its own class. Its food training cost equals the gold training cost of the answer to clue 3.

5. A Monastery technology. It costs gold only — no food, no wood. It is the only Monastery technology that grants Monks the ability to convert buildings. It also grants Monks the ability to convert siege weapons.

---

Take the first letter of each answer in order.

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet
O: Onager
W: Woad Raider
E: Elite Skirmisher
R: Redemption
Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

With No Computation Without Deduction omitted, arithmetic serves as the primary identification tool throughout: clue 1 relies on an exact wood-cost figure (200W, unique among siege), clue 2 uses cost-matching between the answer and its upgrade predecessor, clues 3 and 4 are explicitly interlocked through a shared numerical value (Woad Raider's gold cost of 25G equals Elite Skirmisher's food cost of 25F), and clue 5 uses a cost-type constraint (gold only) without requiring the solver to deduce a missing value or discover a hidden structure. All five clues are solvable by computation against the world data alone, with no deduction layer required.
