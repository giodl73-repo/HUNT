# Condition L16 — Leave Out: Interlock, Not Independence

## Puzzle

Five AoE2 items are described below. Identify each one. The first letter of each answer, read in order, spells your answer.

---

**1.** The only military unit trained directly from the Castle building — not the Castle Age building, the structure itself. It requires Imperial Age, costs the same amount of wood as gold, and its primary targets are buildings and static defenses. A fully upgraded version can hit from outside a Castle's own attack range.

**2.** The Imperial Age upgrade to the Mangonel. On Black Forest, it earns a role no other siege unit can fill: it can clear a path through the forest itself.

**3.** The Persian unique unit. It holds more hit points than any other unit in the game, but moves so slowly that a Monk can outpace it. Its attack damages every unit nearby, not just the one it strikes.

**4.** The Castle Age upgrade to the Skirmisher. It costs no gold — only food and wood. Its defining purpose is countering the Archer line, and it fills that role better than any cavalry or infantry can. It is the primary reason a massed archer army is never a safe answer to everything.

**5.** A Monastery technology costing 475 gold. It extends a Monk's conversion ability to two categories of targets that Monks ordinarily cannot convert at all: siege weapons and buildings.

---

_The first letter of each answer, in the order given, spells a five-letter word._

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet
O: Onager
W: War Elephant
E: Elite Skirmisher
R: Redemption

Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Without the Interlock principle, each clue stands fully alone — no answer feeds information into any other, and the five items share no structural constraint beyond their initial letters. The puzzle remains a valid acrostic extraction, but a solver can work through clues 1–5 in any order with no cross-referencing advantage; the mechanism is clean, but there is no web of mutual confirmation that would elevate it from a sequence of identifications into a single integrated deduction.
