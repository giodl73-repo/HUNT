# Condition L18 — Leave Out: Verify the Last Mile

## Puzzle

**Five Things a Commander Must Know**

A Frankish castle produces a ranged infantryman. He does not fire arrows. He hurls his weapon past walls without triggering minimum range. No other infantry class performs this trick. Name him.

A Siege Workshop graduates its most powerful stone-thrower in the Imperial Age. It does not merely launch rocks at soldiers. A single volley deletes grouped units and can fell trees to open new corridors through forest. It costs 160 wood and 135 gold to train. Name it.

The Celts field the fastest foot soldier in the game. He charges at speed 1.03. No other infantry unit matches him for movement. He originates from a Castle. A player facing archers still fears him closing the gap before they can retreat. Name him.

This archer-class unit costs no gold. It is the designated hard counter to other archers. It fires a javelin, not an arrow. Players research it at the Archery Range as early as the Castle Age. An opponent massing Crossbowmen should be answered with this unit. Name it.

A Monastery technology unlocks a conversion the base Monk cannot perform. Without it, a Monk stands helpless before a Ram or a Castle. With it, the Monk can pray over buildings and siege weapons and pull them across the line. It costs 475 gold. Name it.

---

Take the first letter of each answer, in the order given. That sequence is your answer.

---

## Solution Key

T: Throwing Axeman (Frank unique unit — ranged infantry trained at the Castle; hurls axes, no minimum range)
O: Onager (Imperial Age siege unit — upgraded from Mangonel; 160W 135G; area damage; can destroy trees)
W: Woad Raider (Celt unique unit — fastest infantry in the game at speed 1.03; trained at the Castle)
E: Elite Skirmisher (archer-class counter unit — costs no gold; javelin; hard counter to archers; available Castle Age)
R: Redemption (Monastery technology — 475G; allows Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons)

Answer: TOWER

## Omission Notes

Skipping character-by-character extraction verification introduced one latent risk: the "Elite Skirmisher" identification relies on the solver knowing that the base Skirmisher upgrades at the Castle Age (not Feudal), and the clue specifies "Castle Age" to distinguish the Elite from the base unit — but both begin with E. If a solver names the base "Skirmisher" instead of "Elite Skirmisher," the first letter is still E and the answer is still TOWER, so in this case the omission causes no error. However, the clue intended "Elite Skirmisher" specifically; had the answer letter depended on distinguishing the two, the omission would have masked that ambiguity.
