# Condition L20 — Leave Out: The Voice Rules

## Puzzle

**FIVE ANSWERS — ONE WORD**

Each clue below describes a specific item from Age of Empires II. Identify each item, then take its **first letter**. Read the five first letters in order to spell the answer.

---

1. This Imperial Age siege weapon was the only unit that could attack the enemy's Castle from outside the Castle's own fire range. It was the answer to walls, towers, and fortresses — but useless against a cavalry charge.

2. What separated this Imperial siege engine from its Castle Age predecessor? It could chop down trees. Send it into the Black Forest and watch the choke points disappear.

3. The Celts called this infantry unit their pride. Historians might call it the fastest foot soldier on any battlefield — faster even than a Scout Cavalry unit can move on foot. It trades raw attack for the ability to close ground before the enemy can react.

4. Every archer player feared this upgrade. The Castle Age version countered archers so efficiently that a mass of them could shred the most expensive archer ball in the game, making archers functionally useless against players who had prepared for them.

5. Without this Monastery technology, Monks were powerless against Trebuchets, Rams, and Castles. Researching it expanded the Monk's conversion reach to include buildings and siege weapons — the same targets that the first item on this list was designed to destroy.

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet
O: Onager
W: Woad Raider
E: Elite Skirmisher
R: Redemption

Answer: TOWER

---

## Omission Notes

Without the Voice Rules the clues shift into a descriptive, essay-style register — longer sentences, past tense narration, and rhetorical framing that would violate the present-tense, short-sentence, no-question constraints. Clue 5 also carries explicit meta-commentary ("the same targets that the first item on this list was designed to destroy"), linking two answers directly, which is permissible here but would read as over-scaffolding in strict Voice mode.
