# Condition P07 — Reading Reward ≥ 4

## Puzzle

**FIELD KNOWLEDGE**

Five questions. Each answer is an Age of Empires II item — a civilization, unit, technology, or map. Take the first letter of each answer in order.

---

1. After your monks successfully convert an enemy unit, your entire group can convert again immediately — except for one monk who must rest. Which technology makes this possible?

2. You are sieging a fortified Black Forest base. Your opponent has walled the chokepoint with trees and a palisade. You have no rams available. Which siege unit do you train — not to breach walls directly, but to carve a new path through the forest itself?

3. A Persian player fields this unit in Castle Age. It has more base HP than any other trainable unit in the game. It deals trample damage to surrounding enemies. Yet a single Monk can often decide the battle by targeting it. Name the unit.

4. Your opponent is playing Aztecs or Mayans on Arena. You have noticed that their fast-moving infantry unit — the one that replaces Scout Cavalry for Mesoamerican civilizations — is collecting Relics outside the walls. Which unit is this?

5. Your Monks have been researching aggressively: Fervor, Sanctity, Block Printing. Now you want them to be able to convert the enemy's Trebuchets and Castles directly. Which single technology, researched at the Monastery, grants this ability?

---

## Solution Key

T: Theocracy
O: Onager
W: War Elephant
E: Eagle Warrior
R: Redemption

Answer: TOWER

## Principle Application

Each clue describes a gameplay situation that an experienced AoE2 player resolves through muscle memory and strategic knowledge — Theocracy's micro role in mass monk armies, the Onager's tree-cutting trick, the War Elephant's HP paradox against Monks, the Eagle Warrior's relic-collecting scouting role, and Redemption as the monk tech that targets buildings and siege. A non-player Googling these descriptions will find multiple plausible AoE2 items for most clues (clue 2 could lead to "Siege Onager" vs. "Onager"; clue 3 requires knowing unit HP values to confirm; clue 5 requires knowing which Monastery tech specifically targets siege and buildings rather than just converting units), making confident extraction of the first-letter sequence impossible without genuine game familiarity.
