# Condition P10 — The Dinner Party Test

## Puzzle

**FIVE CLUES. Each answer is an Age of Empires II item (civilization, unit, technology, or map).
Take the first letter of each answer in order to spell the final answer.**

---

1. The only Imperial Age siege engine that attacks from beyond the range of Castle arrows — it can demolish a Wonder without ever entering the fight.

2. Its area-of-effect fire hits hard enough to obliterate a dense formation — and on Black Forest, commanders use it to do something no other military unit can: **fell trees**, opening corridors through the forest that were never meant to be there.

3. The base-tier defensive tower, first available in Feudal Age, that begins a three-stage upgrade chain: this → Guard Tower → Keep.

4. Skirmisher's promoted form, available in Castle Age — the unit specifically designed to hard-counter other ranged units, turning archers' strength back against them.

5. The Monastery technology that extends a Monk's conversion ability to siege weapons and buildings — meaning a single holy man, given enough time, can steal an enemy's Trebuchet.

**What five-letter word do the initial letters spell?**

---

## Solution Key

T: **Trebuchet** — Imperial Age siege unit; attacks from outside Castle arrow range; destroys buildings and Wonders
O: **Onager** — Imperial Age siege; area-of-effect fire; uniquely able to destroy trees on maps like Black Forest
W: **Watch Tower** — Feudal Age defensive structure; base of the Watch Tower → Guard Tower → Keep upgrade chain
E: **Elite Skirmisher** — Castle Age archer-line unit; hard-counter to archers; available as an upgrade from Skirmisher
R: **Redemption** — Monastery technology; enables Monks to convert buildings and siege weapons

Answer: **TOWER**

---

## Principle Application

The Onager clue delivers the Dinner Party moment: a knowledgeable solver realizes mid-puzzle that a siege weapon serves as a lumberjack — the Onager can destroy trees, and competitive players on Black Forest use this deliberately to breach forest corridors that define the entire map's strategic identity. The answer TOWER also pays off the theme: you have just used five instruments of warfare (a siege catapult, a forest-clearing cannon, a defensive turret, an anti-archer skirmisher, and a faith-powered conversion technology) to spell the name of the structure they all exist to attack or defend.
