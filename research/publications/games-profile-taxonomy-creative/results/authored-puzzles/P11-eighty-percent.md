# Condition P11 — The 80% Rule

## Puzzle

Each clue below describes a specific item from Age of Empires II (a civilization, unit,
technology, or map). Identify each item, then take its first letter. The five first letters,
read in order, spell the answer.

1. This Imperial Age siege weapon costs 200 wood and 200 gold, fires in a high arc, and is
   the primary tool for demolishing castles and towers from a safe distance. Unlike other
   siege, it cannot move and fire at the same time — it must be packed and unpacked.

2. This upgrade to the Mangonel arrives in the Imperial Age and can do something no other
   military unit can: cut down trees, opening new paths through forests like Black Forest.
   It devastates grouped units but falls quickly to cavalry.

3. This unique unit of the Celts holds a record no other infantry can claim: the highest
   movement speed in its class at 1.03 tiles per second. It is trained at the Castle and
   costs 65 food and 25 gold.

4. This Castle Age upgrade to the base skirmisher is the hard counter to archers of every
   kind, including Longbowmen and Cavalry Archers. Its name signals both its elevated
   status and its role as the specialist anti-archer unit.

5. This Monastery technology, costing 475 gold, extends a Monk's conversion ability to
   targets that normally cannot be converted: siege weapons and buildings. Without it,
   Monks are helpless against a ram at the gate.

Take the first letter of each answer in order (1 through 5).

**Answer:** _ _ _ _ _

---

## Solution Key

T: Trebuchet (siege unit — Imperial Age, 200W 200G, packs/unpacks, primary castle-breaker)
O: Onager (siege unit — Imperial Age Mangonel upgrade, can fell trees, weak to cavalry)
W: Woad Raider (Celts unique unit — fastest infantry, speed 1.03, 65F 25G, trained at Castle)
E: Elite Skirmisher (archer-class unit — Castle Age upgrade, hard-counters all archers)
R: Redemption (Monastery technology — 475G, allows Monks to convert siege and buildings)

Answer: TOWER

## Principle Application

Each clue is written with a single unambiguous correct answer, but the five answers together
spell a common English word that a solver can recognize and confirm with only four letters
resolved — for instance, solving T_WER, TO_ER, TOW_R, or TOWE_ leaves only one plausible
five-letter word. The 80% Rule is satisfied because the answer space at four-of-five is
effectively size one: any single unsolved clue can be filled in by pattern-matching the
near-complete word TOWER against the known letters.
