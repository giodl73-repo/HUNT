# Condition P16 — Interlock, Not Independence

## Puzzle

Five AoE2 items are described below. Identify each one. The first letter of each answer, read in order, spells a structure found on every battlefield.

---

**1.** This Imperial Age siege weapon packs up and unpacks to fire, and is the only unit that can outrange a Castle. It demolishes stone walls and towers in seconds but cannot fire while moving.

**2.** In the same workshop as #1, this siege weapon began life as a Castle Age catapult lobbing boulders at packed infantry. Researching a single Imperial Age upgrade transforms it into something far more destructive — capable of leveling trees to cut new paths through Black Forest.

**3.** Of all the ranged counters to #2's primary target (grouped infantry), this one has no gold cost. Its upgrade from the base version costs only food and wood, and it hard-counters the archers that threaten the unit in clue #4.

**4.** The Celts' unique Castle-trained infantry is the fastest of its kind in the game. It tears through buildings and trash units but falls quickly to the archers that clue #3 was designed to neutralize.

**5.** This Monastery technology costs only gold, and its sole purpose is to allow Monks to do to the units in clues #1 and #2 what Monks normally can only do to living soldiers.

---

## Solution Key

T: **Trebuchet** (clue 1 — Imperial siege, outranges Castle, cannot fire while moving)
O: **Onager** (clue 2 — upgraded from Mangonel in Siege Workshop, can cut trees on Black Forest)
W: **Woad Raider** (clue 4 — Celts unique infantry, fastest infantry in the game)
E: **Elite Skirmisher** (clue 3 — no-gold archer-line unit, hard-counters archers that threaten Woad Raiders)
R: **Redemption** (clue 5 — Monastery tech allowing Monks to convert siege weapons, i.e., Trebuchet and Onager)

Answer: TOWER

## Principle Application

Clues 1 and 2 share a workshop (Siege Workshop), so identifying either one as Imperial siege narrows the category for the other; clue 5 (Redemption) explicitly points back to the answers of clues 1 and 2 as its targets, binding three of the five clues into a single siege-and-conversion chain. Clues 3 and 4 are mutually dependent: clue 4 names Woad Raiders as the infantry threatened by archers, and clue 3 is defined entirely as the counter to those archers, so neither clue resolves cleanly without the other.
