## P1 Response Assessment

**P1.1 (CC-theoretic paragraph added — Boden combinational/exploratory, Jordanous SPECS):** Addressed. The rubric validity paragraph in Section 3.5 now explicitly connects the double-weighted dimensions to CC criteria: Elegance maps to Boden's combinational creativity, Reading Reward maps to SPECS' domain-competent criterion, and Riven Standard maps to Boden's exploratory creativity criterion. This is precisely the connection the paper needed between its measurement instrument and the ICCC theoretical conversation. The framing ("the rubric is a proxy for creative quality, not a direct measure") is appropriately modest.

**P1.2 (Rubric validity / knowing-that knowing-how operationalization):** Partially addressed. The knowing-that/knowing-how distinction from Section 2.3 is now echoed in Section 5.2: the paper states that "perceptual lenses are terminal and format-dependent" in the discussion, and the classification procedure in Section 5.2 distinguishes questions "framed as named operations with defined inputs and outputs" (knowing-how character) from "perceptual or aesthetic assessments requiring finished material" (knowing-that character). However, the connection is implicit rather than explicit — the paper does not use the Polanyi terminology in Section 5.2 to close the loop it opened in Section 2.3. The knowing-how / knowing-that framework appears in the related work as theoretical motivation but is not explicitly invoked in the lens taxonomy's classification procedure. My Round 1 P1.1 concern is partially met.

**P1.3 (Lens taxonomy — reframed as proposed, classification procedure added):** Addressed. Section 5.2 now opens with the explicit statement that the taxonomy "cannot be empirically validated from the present data" and is "offered as a theoretically motivated classification scheme requiring replication." The classification procedure (count proportion of questions by type from profile content alone) provides a path to pre-observational classification that was absent in Round 1. This substantially addresses the post-hoc circularity concern.

**P1.4 (Companion paper dependency — presence/absence table added inline):** Addressed. Table 4 is now present in Section 4.3 with C0 and C6 counts and net gains for all six frameworks. The C0 counts are documented as zero "by construction," and the caption explains that C0 presence is independently verifiable (none surface in unprompted reviews). This makes the key empirical claim — that the six frameworks are emergent from enriched profiles — readable without accessing the companion paper.

**P1.5 (CC positioning — new subsection added connecting to Colton/Wiggins, Boden, Stokes):** Addressed. Section 5.4 is a genuine contribution to the CC positioning. The three connections are well-chosen: the symmetric gradient connected to the evaluation problem, the A4-vs-A5 result connected to exploratory creativity, and the Young A6 result connected to Stokes on creative constraints. The section is appropriately brief but substantive.

## Remaining Concerns

**Knowing-how / knowing-that loop not explicitly closed.** Section 5.2 now classifies lens types by operational procedure, which implicitly maps to the Polanyi distinction, but the paper does not say so explicitly. One sentence connecting "operational lenses carry procedural content (knowing-how) that inverts directly to authoring constraints; perceptual lenses are purely declarative (knowing-that) and cannot be applied before the artifact exists" would complete the theoretical arc from Section 2.3. This was P3.1 in Round 1 and remains partially open.

**Young A6 forced substitution — cognitive mechanism still underdeveloped.** Section 5.2 attributes the Young result to "blocking a designer's default approach" and cites Stokes, but does not explain the cognitive mechanism at the model level (why does blocking one route activate another?) or connect it to Weisberg's reformulation account, which was specifically requested in the synthesis. The Stokes citation appears in Section 5.4 but the mechanistic interpretation in Section 5.2 is not extended by it.

**P2.4 (starting orientation and lens type connection).** Section 3.2 still describes the three designers' different starting orientations (Snyder: target deduction; Katz: answer word; Young: the world) without connecting this to lens type prediction. If starting orientation predicts lens type, that connection would provide additional evidence for the classification procedure described in Section 5.2. This is a P2 gap from Round 1 that was not addressed.

**P3.4 (profile statement classification note).** The methodological note on procedural-versus-declarative profile content (tagging profile statements as prescriptive vs. evaluative) was not added. The classification procedure described in Section 5.2 relies on the reader's own judgment about question framing without providing guidance. This is a P3 item but given the classification procedure's importance to the taxonomy argument, more specification would strengthen the contribution.

Accept with Minor Revisions
