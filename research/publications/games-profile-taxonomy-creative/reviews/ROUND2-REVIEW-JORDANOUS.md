## P1 Response Assessment

**P1.1 (Score reliability — range column, small gaps hedged):** Addressed. The revised Table 1 now includes a Range column (min--max across the three reviewers) and the table caption explicitly states that "differences within this spread should be interpreted with caution given the small panel size." The results narrative now qualifies the A1-vs-A2 gap as "a directional signal rather than a precise measurement" and notes the A4-vs-A5 difference "should be read as a weak trend rather than a sharp boundary." This is a meaningful and appropriate response to the psychometric concern, though it stops short of ICC or Krippendorff's alpha, which I would still prefer.

**P1.2 (Rubric validity — CC-theoretic paragraph added):** Addressed. The revised Section 3.5 rubric description now includes a validity paragraph connecting Elegance to Boden's combinational creativity criterion, Reading Reward to SPECS' domain-competent criterion, and Riven Standard to Boden's exploratory creativity criterion. This is exactly the grounding I asked for. The face-validity claim is also now backed by the Paper I calibration reference — the rubric separated tier populations with no overlapping distributions — which is genuine external validation.

**P1.3 (Lens taxonomy reframed as "proposed," classification procedure added):** Addressed. Section 3.3 now opens with explicit framing: "this taxonomy cannot be empirically validated from the present data; it is offered as a theoretically motivated classification scheme requiring replication with a broader designer sample." Section 5.2 uses "proposed three-category taxonomy" throughout. A classification procedure is described in Section 5.2: "A profile's lens type can be tentatively classified from its review lens section alone, without observing authoring behavior: count the proportion of questions framed as named operations with defined inputs and outputs (operational), as uniqueness or load-bearing tests (construction), and as perceptual or aesthetic assessments requiring finished material (perceptual)." This directly addresses the circularity concern.

**P1.4 (Companion paper dependency — presence/absence table added inline):** Addressed. Table 4 (framework-presence table) is now in Section 4.3, reporting C0 counts (all zero) and C6 counts by framework, with the net gain column. The caption explicitly states that C0 counts are "zero by construction: none of the six frameworks surface in unprompted reviews at any scoring level." The inline evidence now makes the "none appear in C0--C5" claim independently readable without requiring access to the companion paper, which was the core request.

**P1.5 (CC positioning — new subsection added):** Addressed. Section 5.4 ("Implications for Computational Creativity") is a new subsection that connects the symmetric-gradient finding to Colton and Wiggins' evaluation problem in CC, the A4-vs-A5 result to Boden's exploratory creativity account, and the Young A6 forced-substitution result to Stokes on creative constraints. The connections are substantive rather than superficial — each is argued, not merely cited.

## Remaining Concerns

**P2.1 (same-panel bias) — unaddressed.** The limitations section does not acknowledge that Young, Katz, and Snyder both authored puzzles under A5/A6 and evaluated all 13 puzzles. This is the one P2 item I consider most methodologically serious. Young scoring her own A5 and A6 puzzles on Reading Reward is a directional bias threat that should at least be named as a limitation with a note on whether it is expected to inflate or deflate scores.

**P2.2 (pass threshold motivation) — not fully addressed.** The threshold is described in Section 3.5 but its derivation is still not explained. The paper says it is a "panel average of 33/45 (73% of maximum)" without explaining whether this figure comes from Paper I calibration data, practitioner convention, or this study's own construction. One sentence stating the origin would close this.

**Residual gap in P1.3.** While the taxonomy is now appropriately hedged and a classification procedure is given, the classification procedure is still framed as a counting heuristic (proportion of questions by type) without specifying a threshold for assignment. A designer whose profile is 50% operational and 50% perceptual would be unclassifiable by the procedure as written. This is a minor gap that should be acknowledged.

**No response to P2.6 (statistical framing absent throughout).** While the range column addresses the most glaring gap, there is still no bootstrap confidence interval or effect-size measure on any of the reported differences. I understand the study was not designed for formal inference, but a methodological note on what the small-N panel design implies for precision of reported differences — recommended in the synthesis — does not appear anywhere beyond the table caption.
