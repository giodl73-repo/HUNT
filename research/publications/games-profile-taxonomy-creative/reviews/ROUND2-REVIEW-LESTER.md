## P1 Response Assessment

**P1.1 (Score reliability — range column added, small gaps hedged):** Addressed. The Range column in Table 1 is a practical and reader-friendly response to the reliability concern: seeing that the A1-vs-A2 gap (0.6 points) falls within the reviewer range of both conditions (27--28 and 28--29 respectively) immediately communicates that the gap is within inter-reviewer variability. The results prose now explicitly hedges the A1-vs-A2 difference as "a directional signal rather than a precise measurement" and notes the A4-vs-A5 difference "should be read as a weak trend rather than a sharp boundary." This is the correct level of epistemic caution given the panel size.

**P1.2 (Rubric validity — CC-theoretic paragraph):** Addressed. The rubric validity paragraph in Section 3.5 grounds the double-weighted dimensions in CC criteria (Elegance-to-Boden, Reading Reward-to-SPECS, Riven Standard-to-exploratory creativity) and adds the face-validity claim from Paper I calibration. This is appropriate and sufficient for the venue. From a game design research perspective, I note that the connections are primarily to computational creativity criteria rather than to game design quality frameworks — but this is an ICCC paper, so the CC framing is correct.

**P1.3 (Lens taxonomy — proposed framing, classification procedure):** Addressed. Section 3.3 now frames the lens taxonomy explicitly as a "theoretically motivated classification scheme requiring replication" and Section 5.2 provides a classification procedure that operates on profile content rather than on observed transfer outcomes. The "proposed" qualifier appears in the discussion as requested. My Round 1 P1.3 concern that the lens taxonomy could not generalize to other domains is partially addressed: a content-based classification procedure allows a reader to attempt classification of profiles from other design domains, even though validation would require additional studies.

**P1.4 (Companion paper dependency — presence/absence table added):** Addressed. This was the correct fix: the essential claim is now independently documented.

**P1.5 (CC positioning — new subsection):** Addressed. Section 5.4 adds substantive CC connections. The connection to Boden's exploratory creativity via the A4-vs-A5 result is particularly well-argued: a designer who has internalized a generative worldview "explores the same conceptual space as one equipped with a full operational checklist, because the worldview implicitly encodes the boundaries of that space" is a Boden argument, not just a citation.

## Remaining Concerns

**P2.1 (same-panel bias) — not addressed.** Katz, Snyder, and Young authored puzzles under A5/A6 and then evaluated all 13 puzzles, including their own. The limitations section does not mention this as a threat to internal validity. This is the most important unaddressed methodological concern. A single paragraph acknowledging that designer-evaluators scoring their own outputs may introduce directional bias — and noting whether any ordering or blinding controls were applied — would address this.

**P2.2 (artist persona not described) — still inadequate.** Section 5.3 references the artist persona result as evidence for the format ceiling argument and adds the detail ("damaged inscriptions with one missing letter per item") that I requested. However, the passage attributes this to the companion paper citation without reproducing the rubric score the persona achieved on this study's rubric. The claim that "a bare artist persona produced a higher Riven score than any A-series puzzle" currently lacks the evidence needed to evaluate it independently: what was the actual total score? On which dimensions did it outperform the A-series? At minimum, the Riven Standard score for the artist persona should be stated inline.

**P2.3 (pass threshold motivation) — not addressed.** The pass threshold of 33/45 still has no stated derivation. This is a P2 item but is directly load-bearing for the format ceiling argument: the claim that "no A-series condition passes" is meaningful only if the threshold has a principled derivation independent of this study's results.

**P3.2 (worked example of perceptual profile conversion).** The practical guidance for converting perceptual reviewing profiles into generative authoring profiles remains brief and is the paper's most immediately practitioner-useful contribution, particularly for game design. One worked example using Young's narrative-grammar substitution as the template (original check → generative intent → format-specific instruction) would substantially increase usability for the game content generation audience. This P3 item from Round 1 was not addressed in the revision.
