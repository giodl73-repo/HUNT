# Round 2 Review: Profile Depth and the Reviewing-to-Authoring Transfer Problem
**Reviewer:** Tony Veale
**Round:** 2

## P1 Response Assessment

**P1.1 (CC positioning / rubric quality as creativity quality):** Addressed. The new Section 5.4 engages substantively with the creativity-theoretic framing I requested. The authors connect the symmetric gradient to Colton/Wiggins' evaluation problem, the A4-vs-A5 finding to Boden's exploratory creativity, and the Young A6 result to Stokes on creative constraints. The rubric validity paragraph in Section 3.5 now links Elegance to combinational creativity, Reading Reward to SPECS' domain-competent criterion, and Riven Standard to Boden's exploratory creativity criterion. This is a meaningful reframing — the paper now makes a defensible argument that its quality dimensions are proxies for creativity-relevant properties. I am not fully satisfied (the paper still does not ask whether any output is creative in an output-level sense), but the ICCC positioning is substantially improved.

**P1.2 (Symmetric gradient differentiation from simpler prompting-literature story):** Partially addressed. Section 5.4 adds the evaluation-problem connection to Colton/Wiggins, which partially differentiates the finding from generic "more information = better prompts" accounts: the argument that a single competence profile architecture governs both generation and evaluation quality is a structural claim that the prompting literature does not make. However, Section 5.1's discussion of the symmetric gradient still does not explicitly distinguish the paper's claims from the standard diminishing-returns prompting result. The paper asserts the symmetry is theoretically significant but does not clearly state what the symmetry tells us that the generic prompting account does not. This is my primary remaining reservation.

**P1.3 (Lens taxonomy — classification procedure added):** Addressed. Section 3.3 now explicitly frames the taxonomy as a "theoretically motivated classification scheme requiring replication with a broader designer sample," and Section 5.2 adds a profile-content classification procedure based on the proportion of questions by type (operational, construction, perceptual). This addresses my concern that the taxonomy could only be applied retrospectively — a reader can now attempt to classify a new profile from its content prior to observing transfer outcomes.

## Remaining Concerns

**P1.2 partial gap — symmetric gradient still under-differentiated from prompting literature.** The paper now connects the symmetric gradient to CC theory (Section 5.4), but Section 5.1 does not explicitly address the simpler story: "more informative prompts produce better outputs, diminishing returns, as documented elsewhere." The CC-theoretic framing helps but does not fully answer the question of what the symmetry specifically tells us beyond what generic prompting studies already show. A sentence or two directly addressing this would close the issue.

**P2.1 (Young A6 "forced substitution" — needs more theoretical depth).** Section 5.2 now cites Stokes on creative constraints and notes the connection to constraint-induced creative pressure, but it does so briefly (one sentence). The forced-substitution mechanism — why does blocking one approach activate another, and what does this tell us about the AI system's generative architecture? — deserves the cognitive creativity treatment I requested in Round 1 (Ward on path-of-least-resistance, Boden on transformational creativity). The paper cites Stokes but does not discuss the mechanism.

**P2.2 (Six frameworks and CC theory).** Table 4 and Section 4.3 are improved by the presence/absence data, but the frameworks still have no connections to established CC constructs in this paper. Perceptual-shift connects naturally to Wiggins' exploratory creativity; a-ha economy connects to Csikszentmihalyi's flow; the paper would benefit from one or two sentences making these connections. This was a P2 item in Round 1 and remains unaddressed.

**Abstract positioning.** The abstract still leads with "practical guidance for constructing authoring profiles" as the primary contribution statement. For ICCC, foregrounding the structural property claim ("profile depth is a structural property of how constrained generation systems engage complex tasks") would better serve the paper's revised positioning. This is a P3 suggestion from Round 1 that was not addressed.
