## Remaining Issues (P2/P3 only)

The following issues were not addressed in the revision but do not block acceptance. They are consolidated from individual reviewer reports.

### P2 Items (important; should be addressed before camera-ready)

**P2-A: Same-panel evaluation bias (Jordanous, Lester; originally P2.1).**
Katz, Snyder, and Young served as both the authoring personas (A5/A6) and the C8 evaluation panel. This introduces a directional bias risk — most acute for Young scoring her own A5/A6 puzzles on Reading Reward and Elegance — that is not acknowledged anywhere in the current revision. The limitations section should add one paragraph naming this as a threat to internal validity and discussing its likely direction (over- or underestimation) and any ordering or blinding controls that were applied.

**P2-B: Artist persona result underspecified (Lester; originally P2.2).**
Section 5.3 now mentions that the artist persona produced "damaged inscriptions with one missing letter per item" but still does not report the actual total rubric score or Riven Standard score the artist persona achieved. The format ceiling argument depends on the claim that this persona "outperformed" all A-series conditions, but the comparison is currently qualitative rather than quantitative. Adding the score (even as an approximation) would allow readers to assess the claim.

**P2-C: Pass threshold motivation still absent (Jordanous, Lester; originally P2.3 / P2.4).**
The 33/45 pass threshold is stated but its derivation is never explained. If it comes from Paper I calibration data, one sentence saying so would suffice. If it is a constructed threshold, the construction logic should be stated. This is a minor but recurring complaint across two reviewers.

**P2-D: Mechanistic account of framework-vs-content gap absent (Riedl; originally P1.1 in R1, downgraded to P2 in R2).**
The paper's central finding — that framework contributes more than content — is explained at the cognitive level but not at the computational level. Riedl has downgraded this from P1 blocking to P2 given the CC positioning in Section 5.4, but the question of what the model computationally does differently with a framework-enriched versus content-only profile remains open. A sentence positioning this as an explicit open mechanistic question would suffice.

**P2-E: Alternative explanations for lens-type gaps not analyzed (Riedl; originally P1.2 in R1, downgraded to P2 in R2).**
The attribution of Katz's +1.6, Snyder's -0.7, and Young's +3.7 gaps to lens type is not tested against alternative explanations (verbal specificity, accidental misreading of evaluative questions as generative). A brief paragraph in Section 5.2 acknowledging the most plausible alternatives and why they are less parsimonious than the lens-type account would strengthen the causal claim.

### P3 Items (suggestions; nice to have)

**P3-A: Knowing-how/knowing-that loop not explicitly closed in Section 5.2 (Gervás).**
The Polanyi distinction from Section 2.3 is implicitly reflected in the classification procedure of Section 5.2 but is not named. One sentence explicitly mapping lens types to knowing-that/knowing-how would complete the theoretical arc.

**P3-B: A2-vs-A3 exemplar comparison not added (Riedl).**
A clue-level comparison of Stone and Steel (A2) against the A3 puzzle would ground the "database rows vs. constructions" distinction empirically. This was a P3 suggestion in Round 1 and remains unaddressed.

**P3-C: Worked example of perceptual profile conversion not added (Lester).**
The practical guidance for converting perceptual reviewing profiles into generative authoring profiles would benefit from one worked example using Young's narrative-grammar substitution. This was a P3 item in Round 1 and remains unaddressed.

**P3-D: Six frameworks not mapped to CC theory in Section 4.3 (Veale).**
Perceptual-shift connects to Wiggins' exploratory creativity; a-ha economy connects to Csikszentmihalyi's flow; social fabric connects to collaborative creativity models. Brief notes in Section 4.3 would increase the CC relevance of what is already the paper's most original qualitative contribution.

**P3-E: Abstract positioning not updated (Veale).**
The abstract still leads with "practical guidance for constructing authoring profiles" rather than the structural CC claim. For ICCC, foregrounding the structural property finding would better align the abstract with the paper's revised positioning.

## Panel Recommendation

**Accept with Minor Revisions**

All P1 items are resolved and the gate is cleared with a panel average of 3.15/4. The revision demonstrates a well-targeted response to the most important concerns: the rubric validity paragraph, the range column with explicit hedging, the taxonomy's provisional reframing, the inline framework-presence table, and the new CC positioning section together represent a substantive and coherent set of improvements. The paper's empirical core — the symmetric gradient, the A2 inversion, the lens-type transfer result, and the format ceiling — was strong in Round 1 and remains strong.

The remaining issues are all P2 or P3: they are individually addressable without new data collection or structural changes, and none is blocking. The most important for camera-ready are P2-A (same-panel bias acknowledgment) and P2-C (pass threshold motivation), both requiring fewer than 100 words each. P2-B (artist persona score) requires one numeric value. P2-D and P2-E require one brief paragraph each.
