**Source:** Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition (AoE2 DE)
**Scope:** 8 standard map types used in competitive and casual play.

## Map Descriptions

### Arabia
The most-played competitive map. Open terrain with scattered woodlines, berries near the Town Center, two boars, eight sheep, and deer. Gold and stone on the flanks. No natural walls — aggression starts in Feudal Age. Favors aggressive civs (Franks, Mongols, Aztecs). The "default" AoE2 experience.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 2 o'clock, medium — forward gold, toward the enemy
- **Gold 2:** 8 o'clock, medium — back gold, away from the enemy
- **Stone 1:** 10 o'clock, medium — typically on opposite flank from front gold
- **Stone 2:** 4 o'clock, medium — mirrored stone on opposite flank
- **Berries:** 11 o'clock, close — within 6-8 tiles of TC, slightly back
- **Boar 1:** 1 o'clock, medium — within luring range (~8-10 tiles from TC)
- **Boar 2:** 7 o'clock, medium — roughly opposite side from Boar 1
- **Deer:** 12-1 o'clock, medium-far — cluster of 3-4, pushed forward from TC [VERIFY: sometimes 3, sometimes 4 deer]
- **Sheep:** scattered close, 4 near TC + 4 further out in pairs
- **Woodlines:** multiple scattered woodlines at various angles, typically 2-3 main lines within close-medium range
- **Notes:** Resource positions have significant variance on Arabia. Gold/stone "flank" positions are the most consistent feature. The forward gold is the most contested resource. Woodlines are randomly generated — no fixed clock position.

### Black Forest
Dense forest covers the map. Each player spawns in a pocket cleared among the trees. Narrow corridors connect bases — usually one or two choke points. Players wall the chokes and boom to Imperial Age. Onagers and Siege Onagers can cut through trees to create new paths. Favors late-game civilizations with strong siege or unique units. Games tend to be long.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 12 o'clock, close-medium — inside or at the edge of your forest pocket
- **Gold 2:** 6 o'clock, close-medium — opposite side of pocket
- **Gold 3:** extra gold patches often spawn in the corridors or neutral zones [VERIFY]
- **Stone 1:** 3 o'clock, close-medium — inside the pocket
- **Stone 2:** 9 o'clock, close-medium — inside the pocket, opposite flank
- **Berries:** 12 o'clock, close — near TC within the cleared pocket
- **Boar 1:** within the pocket, close-medium — exact position varies with clearing shape
- **Boar 2:** within the pocket, close-medium — opposite side
- **Deer:** 12 o'clock, medium — near the edge of the pocket, sometimes partially in forest [VERIFY]
- **Sheep:** scattered within the pocket clearing, close
- **Woodlines:** the entire perimeter is forest — wood is unlimited and everywhere
- **Notes:** Resource layout is compressed into the forest clearing. All resources tend to be closer to TC than on Arabia. The pocket shape varies, so clock positions are less reliable. Gold and stone are always inside or near the walled pocket.

### Islands
Each player spawns on a separate island surrounded by water. Fish provide the primary early food source. Transport Ships are required to land armies. Naval control (Galleys, Fire Ships, Cannon Galleons) is essential. Favors naval civs (Japanese, Byzantines, Persians). Deep fish and shore fish are abundant.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 3 o'clock, close-medium — on-island, toward one coast
- **Gold 2:** 9 o'clock, close-medium — on-island, opposite coast
- **Stone 1:** 1 o'clock, close-medium — on-island
- **Stone 2:** 7 o'clock, close-medium — on-island, opposite side
- **Berries:** 12 o'clock, close — near TC, on-island
- **Boar 1:** on-island, close-medium — position varies with island shape
- **Boar 2:** on-island, close-medium — opposite side [VERIFY: may only have 1 boar on small islands]
- **Deer:** on-island, medium — may not always spawn depending on island size [VERIFY]
- **Shore Fish:** multiple clusters around the island perimeter, all directions
- **Deep Fish:** scattered in open water between islands, medium-far
- **Woodlines:** on-island, limited — 1-2 woodlines, positions vary with island shape
- **Notes:** Island size varies between generations. Small islands may have reduced land resources (fewer deer, possibly 1 boar). Shore fish ring the island and are the primary early food alongside berries. The island footprint constrains all land resource positions to a tight area.

### Mediterranean
A large central sea divides the map, with land on all sides. Players typically start on the coast with access to both land and water resources. Docks go up early. Hybrid strategy required — naval control of the center plus land armies for the flanks. Moderate amounts of fish. Transport Ships enable cross-water raids.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 2 o'clock, medium — forward, toward the enemy's side
- **Gold 2:** 8 o'clock, medium — back, inland away from enemy
- **Stone 1:** 10 o'clock, medium — inland, on a flank
- **Stone 2:** 4 o'clock, medium — inland, opposite flank
- **Berries:** 11 o'clock, close — near TC, slightly inland from the coast
- **Boar 1:** 1 o'clock, medium — between TC and coastline
- **Boar 2:** 7 o'clock, medium — inland side
- **Deer:** 12-1 o'clock, medium — pushed forward [VERIFY: deer count varies]
- **Shore Fish:** along the central sea shoreline, close to docking areas
- **Deep Fish:** in the central sea, medium-far from shore
- **Woodlines:** scattered inland, close-medium
- **Notes:** The central sea runs roughly north-south (or east-west depending on spawn). Land resources mirror Arabia distribution but are compressed toward the inland side because the coast eats into the map. The sea-side of each base has shore fish. Forward gold is often vulnerable to naval raids.

### Arena
Each player starts fully enclosed by stone walls. Safe booming to Castle Age is standard. The game opens when someone breaks through (Rams, Trebuchets) or goes Castle-drop. Relics spawn outside the walls — Monks must venture out early. Favors Monk rushes (Aztecs) and fast Imperial civs. No early aggression unless you commit siege.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 2 o'clock, close — inside the walls, forward side
- **Gold 2:** 8 o'clock, close — inside the walls, back side
- **Gold 3:** outside the walls, medium-far — extra gold in no-man's land [VERIFY]
- **Stone 1:** 10 o'clock, close — inside the walls
- **Stone 2:** 4 o'clock, close — inside the walls
- **Berries:** 11-12 o'clock, close — inside the walls, near TC
- **Boar 1:** inside the walls, close — within the walled enclosure
- **Boar 2:** inside the walls, close — opposite side of enclosure
- **Deer:** 12 o'clock, close-medium — inside the walls, near the edge of the enclosure [VERIFY: may be partially outside walls]
- **Sheep:** inside the walls, close — scattered near TC
- **Relics:** outside the walls, medium-far — 3-5 relics in the neutral zone between bases
- **Woodlines:** inside the walls, close-medium — enough wood for early game inside the enclosure
- **Notes:** The defining feature is that most resources start INSIDE the stone wall enclosure. Gold and stone are safe until walls break. Extra gold patches outside the walls become contested mid-game. Relics are always outside, forcing early Monk ventures through gates.

### Nomad
No starting Town Center, no starting Scout. Each player begins with 3 Villagers wandering the map. First task: find a good spot and build a TC. Resources are scattered randomly. Water may or may not be present (depends on map generation). Total chaos in the first few minutes. Favors civs with early economic bonuses (Persians, Chinese).

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold:** randomly scattered across the map — no fixed positions relative to TC (TC doesn't exist at start)
- **Stone:** randomly scattered — no fixed positions
- **Berries:** multiple berry patches scattered randomly — players choose TC location based partly on berry proximity
- **Boar:** scattered randomly — may or may not be near where you build your TC
- **Deer:** scattered randomly in groups of 3-4
- **Shore Fish:** if water is present, shore fish appear along coastlines
- **Deep Fish:** if open water exists, deep fish spawn in it
- **Woodlines:** large forests scattered across the map
- **Notes:** Nomad has NO standard resource geometry. Everything is random. There is no TC at game start, so "clock position from TC" is meaningless until the player places their TC. The player's first strategic decision is WHERE to build the TC — ideally near wood, berries, and at least one gold or stone pile. This map is unsuitable for Puzzle IV-B (Resource Map) because there is no predictable pattern to trace.

### Gold Rush
Open terrain similar to Arabia, but with a massive gold deposit in the center of the map. Standard gold patches near each player are smaller than usual, forcing players to contest the center. Mid-game fights over the central gold define the map. Favors aggressive military civs that can hold the center. Wood and food are normal.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Central Gold:** 12 o'clock (toward center of map), far — massive gold pile (7-8 tiles of gold) at map center
- **Gold 1 (near):** 2 o'clock, close — small patch near TC, runs out quickly
- **Gold 2 (near):** 8 o'clock, close — small patch near TC, runs out quickly [VERIFY: some gens only have 1 near gold]
- **Stone 1:** 10 o'clock, medium — standard positioning
- **Stone 2:** 4 o'clock, medium — standard positioning
- **Berries:** 11 o'clock, close — near TC, similar to Arabia
- **Boar 1:** 1 o'clock, medium — standard luring distance
- **Boar 2:** 7 o'clock, medium
- **Deer:** 12-1 o'clock, medium — forward, between TC and the center gold
- **Woodlines:** scattered, close-medium — similar to Arabia
- **Notes:** The signature feature is the central gold. Near-TC gold patches are deliberately small (2-3 tiles instead of Arabia's 3-4). Players are forced to move villagers to the center gold by mid-Castle Age. The central gold pile is enormous — often 7-8 individual gold tiles clustered together. Distance from TC to center gold is roughly half the map.

### Baltic
Coastal map inspired by the Baltic Sea. Water runs along one or more edges of the map. Players start on land with access to the coast. Fish are available but not as central as on Islands. A blend of land and naval combat, with the water being important but not dominant. Stone and gold patches are on land. Favors hybrid civs comfortable on both land and water.

#### Resource Geometry (approximate, 1v1)
- **Gold 1:** 2 o'clock, medium — inland, between TC and coast
- **Gold 2:** 8 o'clock, medium — inland, opposite side
- **Stone 1:** 10 o'clock, medium — inland
- **Stone 2:** 4 o'clock, medium — inland
- **Berries:** 11-12 o'clock, close — near TC, inland side
- **Boar 1:** 1 o'clock, medium — between TC and coastline
- **Boar 2:** 7 o'clock, medium
- **Deer:** 12 o'clock, medium — forward, may be near the coast [VERIFY]
- **Shore Fish:** along the coastal edge, accessible from docks
- **Deep Fish:** in the sea body, medium-far from shore
- **Woodlines:** scattered inland, close-medium — wood is standard
- **Notes:** Players spawn on the land portion with the sea along one or two edges. Resource layout on land resembles Arabia but is compressed away from the waterline. The coast provides fishing income to supplement land food. Gold and stone are always on land, never in water. The water edge acts as a natural wall on one side, reducing the angles of attack.

## Map Features Summary

| Map | Early Game | Mid Game | Late Game | Resource Quirk |
|-----|-----------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Arabia | Rush / pressure | Knights or Crossbows | Trash wars | Standard distribution |
| Black Forest | Safe boom | Wall + mass army | Siege wars, tree cutting | Extra wood everywhere |
| Islands | Fish boom, Galleys | Naval control | Transport landings | Fish is primary food |
| Mediterranean | Fish + Feudal pressure | Naval + land armies | Combined arms | Fish + standard land |
| Arena | Safe boom | Castle break-in | Imperial army fights | Relics outside walls |
| Nomad | TC race, survival | Recover + boom | Varies wildly | Random resource scatter |
| Gold Rush | Standard open | Fight for center gold | Gold advantage wins | Central gold pile |
| Baltic | Fish + land economy | Coastal fights | Land + naval late game | Moderate fish access |

## Notes for Puzzle Authors

- Map names are proper nouns in AoE2 (always capitalized).
- Arabia, Arena, and Black Forest are the three most commonly played maps in ranked.
- Each map naturally favors different civ types — this creates puzzle material (match civ to map).
- Nomad is the most unusual: no starting TC breaks every standard build order.
- The water/no-water distinction is the primary map classifier for strategy.
