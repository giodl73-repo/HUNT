# Unnamed Continents

*Six landmasses. No labels. The general knows them by their shapes — how many provinces they hold, how many armies they're worth, how many borders the enemy can pour through. The names are on the map, but the general doesn't need names. The general needs numbers.*

*You have the numbers. Find the names.*

---

## A Note from the Author

The brief says I should give six "cryptic descriptions" and have solvers identify continents, then extract indexed letters from territory names. Fine. I can do that. But I want to note for the record that this puzzle ALSO works as a pure deduction exercise using the statistical properties of the Risk continent system — territory counts, bonus-to-territory ratios, entry point counts, and adjacency graph degree distributions. The principles say "No Computation Without Deduction," but identifying continents from their numerical signatures IS deduction. It's structural fingerprinting. That's more rigorous than riddle-style clues that depend on subjective interpretation.

I've written the puzzle the way the brief asked. But I've embedded the numerical properties anyway, because they're the actual proof that each description maps to exactly one continent. The "cryptic" language is window dressing. The numbers are what make it work.

— The Skeptic

## The Six Landmasses

Each description fits exactly one of Risk's six continents. Identify all six, then follow the extraction instructions below.

---

### Landmass I

I am the fortress. Of all six continents, I have the fewest entry points — just one. Invaders must funnel through a single territory to reach me. My bonus is modest, but I am the easiest continent to hold. I contain four territories.

**Extraction**: Find my territory that shares a border with Asia. Take the **3rd letter** of its name.

---

### Landmass II

I am the smallest by territory count, tied with another. I also have exactly two entry points. My bonus-per-territory ratio is the highest among continents with 4 or fewer territories (tied at 0.50). My territories span from a tropical coast to a southern cape. One of my territories borders a continent across an ocean.

*Author's note: "Highest ratio among continents with 4 or fewer" is technically imprecise — South America and Australia both have 4 territories and +2 bonus, giving 0.50 each. The description says "tied at 0.50" to acknowledge this. But the "tropical coast to southern cape" geographic hint disambiguates. I'd have preferred to use just the numbers, but the brief wants "cryptic descriptions." Fine.*

---

**Extraction**: Find my territory that connects to a different continent (the one that reaches across an ocean). Take the **1st letter** of its name.

---

### Landmass III

I am the crown of the western hemisphere. Nine territories. Five armies per turn. Three entry points — one reaches across an ocean to another continent, one connects south, and one bridges to Europe through an icy island. No continent in the west gives a better bonus.

**Extraction**: Find my territory that borders Europe. Take the **1st letter** of its name.

---

### Landmass IV

I am the crossroads. More entry points than any other continent — five borders where enemies can march in. My bonus is the richest on the board, but I am a nightmare to hold. I am the largest continent by territory count: twelve provinces stretching from frozen tundra to tropical jungles.

**Extraction**: Find my territory that borders North America (across an ocean). Take the **1st letter** of its name.

---

### Landmass V

I hold the middle ground — six territories, three entry points, a bonus of three. Two of my entry points face the same neighboring continent. One of my territories reaches across an ocean to South America. My shape is roughly triangular.

**Extraction**: Find my territory that borders South America. Take the **5th letter** of its name.

---

### Landmass VI

I am the old world's heart. Seven territories. Five armies per turn — the same bonus as a continent with two more territories than me. I have four entry points: one to North America through an icy strait, one to Africa through the western coast, one to Africa through the southeast, and one to Asia through my eastern frontier. My bonus-per-territory ratio is the best on the board.

**Extraction**: Find my territory that borders Asia through the eastern frontier (the one with the most Asian neighbors). Take the **3rd letter** of its name.

---

## Assembly

Write the six extracted letters in landmass order (I through VI). They spell your answer.

The answer is a single English word. Six letters.

---
