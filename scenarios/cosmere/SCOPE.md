# THE SIXTEENTH SHARD — Scope

## Identity

**Name:** THE SIXTEENTH SHARD
**Domain:** Real-world — Brandon Sanderson's Cosmere shared universe (published novels + Coppermind wiki)
**Tone:** Reverent but playful. The Cosmere is dense and the hunt respects that. But Hoid is watching, and he is amused. There is wit under the lore.
**Scale:** 36 puzzles + 3 round metas + 1 super-meta (40 total solve objects)
**Structure:** 3 rounds x 12 puzzles, 6 modules of 6 puzzles each
**Audience:** Cosmere readers who have read at least Mistborn Era 1 and The Stormlight Archive; puzzle experience varies
**Format:** Website + print companion
**Duration:** 10-16 hours (full team), designed for weekend-length engagement

---

## The Frame

Hoid has hidden something in the Cosmere's history. A message left across three worlds for anyone with the pattern recognition to find it. You are Worldhoppers. You move through Scadrial, Roshar, and the wider Cosmere, piecing together the secret Hoid encoded in the fabric of each world's Investiture.

The answer to the final puzzle is the nature of what Hoid has been looking for all along. It does not exist in the published books. But the puzzle logic creates it.

---

## Content Library

Real-world domain. All puzzle facts drawn from published Brandon Sanderson works:

- **Mistborn Era 1:** *The Final Empire*, *The Well of Ascension*, *The Hero of Ages*
- **Mistborn Era 2:** *The Alloy of Law*, *Shadows of Self*, *The Bands of Mourning*, *The Lost Metal*
- **The Stormlight Archive:** *The Way of Kings*, *Words of Radiance*, *Edgedancer*, *Oathbringer*, *Dawnshard*, *Rhythm of War*, *Wind and Truth*
- **Standalone:** *Warbreaker*, *Elantris*, *The Emperor's Soul*, *Tress of the Emerald Sea*, *Yumi and the Nightmare Painter*, *The Sunlit Man*
- **Arcanum Unbounded:** novellas + cosmere essays (*Mistborn: Secret History*, *Shadows for Silence in the Forests of Hell*, *Sixth of the Dusk*, *The Hope of Elantris*, *Allomancer Jak and the Pits of Eltania*)
- **Coppermind wiki** — community-verified canonical reference

**Exclusions:** No Words of Brandon (WoBs) unless confirmed in published text. No unpublished drafts. No fan theories presented as fact.

**Verification rule:** Every puzzle fact must be traceable to a specific published book or Coppermind page. Anything uncertain is flagged with [VERIFY].

## Narrator Voice: Hoid/Wit

Hoid narrates. Short, dry observations. Present tense. He knows more than he is letting on.

**Rules:**
1. No exclamation marks.
2. Present tense default. Past tense only for Shattering-era events.
3. Short sentences. Rarely more than 15 words.
4. No direct questions to the solver. Hoid does not ask. He implies.
5. No meta-commentary. The puzzles exist in-world.
6. The solver is addressed obliquely — "one notices" or "a Worldhopper might observe" — never "you."
7. Dry humor. The kind that makes you re-read to confirm it was a joke.

**Example:**
> The metals have names. The names have letters. The letters have positions. One suspects the Lord Ruler noticed this too, though he had other concerns at the time.

## Meta Architecture

**Round Meta I (THE WELL):**
The 12 Scadrial puzzle answers combine via the Allomancy table. The table IS the meta extraction mechanism — metal positions, properties, or grid coordinates index into answer letters.

**Round Meta II (THE OATHS):**
The 12 Roshar puzzle answers encode the 5 Oaths of a specific Radiant order. The Oaths themselves are the extraction mechanism.

**Round Meta III (ADONALSIUM):**
The 12 Cosmere puzzle answers reference 12 of the 16 Shards. The 4 missing Shards spell the round meta answer.

**Super-meta (THE SEVENTEENTH SHARD):**
The 3 round meta answers, combined with the Investiture logic of all three rounds, reveal the nature of the 17th Shard — a Shard that does not exist in the books but is created by the puzzle logic. The answer is a word representing Hoid's true nature: the seeker, the questioner. Something like CURIOSITY or INQUIRY.

## Key Puzzle Affordances

**Allomancy table (Module A):**
- 16 metals in a structured grid: 4 categories (Physical, Mental, Temporal, Enhancement) x 2 axes (Internal/External, Push/Pull)
- Each metal: name, allomantic power, feruchemical power, hemalurgic use
- Massive encoding potential: grid position, first letters, property matching, paired metals

**Knights Radiant (Module C):**
- 10 orders, each with 2 Surges, 1 Herald, 1 Gemstone, 1 Spren type
- The Oaths (5 per order, progressive)
- Matching, sequencing, and extraction puzzles

**The 16 Shards (Module E):**
- 16 Shards: name (Intent), original Vessel, current status, planet, Investiture type
- 16 = 4x4 grid encoding potential
- Single-word Intent names: Preservation, Ruin, Honor, Cultivation, Odium, etc.

**Hoid appearances (Module F):**
- Hoid in every Cosmere book under different aliases
- Timeline-ordered appearances encode information
- Identification and deduction puzzles

## Delivery Format

**Website:**
- Primary delivery. Themed as a Worldhopper's journal or Hoid's collection.
- Three round pages (Scadrial, Roshar, Cosmere), each revealing 12 puzzles.
- Answer submission per puzzle. Round meta unlocks after all 12 feeders are solved.
- Super-meta unlocks after all 3 round metas solved.

**Print companion:**
- Optional physical supplement. Reference sheets for the Allomancy table, Knight Radiant orders, Shard list.
- Print-ready puzzle pages for offline solving.
- Hoid's notebook aesthetic: handwritten marginalia, sketches, torn pages.

## Review Gate

This scope defines a hunt that:
- Uses real-world canon from a beloved fantasy series (Riven Standard — the puzzle IS the domain knowledge)
- Has interlocking data systems that reward cross-referencing (allomancy + feruchemy + hemalurgy on the same metals)
- Frames the meta target in-universe (the 17th Shard is a diegetic mystery Hoid would pursue)
- Requires Cosmere familiarity but rewards deep knowledge proportionally
- Provides all necessary reference data in world/ files so solvers can verify without leaving the hunt
- Scales to 36 puzzles across 6 parallel author teams without coordination bottlenecks
