# P05 — Three Arts, One Spike

*Module A (Allomancy) | Pewter (#4) | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

Three arts share the sixteen metals. Allomancy burns. Feruchemy stores. Hemalurgy steals. The same metal serves each art differently, and a scholar of the Metallic Arts knows which service belongs where.

Eight metals from the Physical and Mental quadrants are presented below. For each, three descriptions appear — one labeled Allomancy, one Feruchemy, one Hemalurgy. Two descriptions correctly belong to the metal in question. One has been taken from a neighboring metal in the same quadrant and placed in the wrong slot.

Name each metal. Find each lie.

---

## The Eight Triples

---

### Triple I

*Another set of records, another discrepancy. The Terris keepers grow careless.*

- **Allomancy:** Riots emotions in others, inflaming what they feel.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores warmth — colder while storing, warmer while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Allomantic Emotional powers.

---

### Triple II

*Dampened fury and stored heat — this metal knows both arts, yet one entry here is borrowed.*

- **Allomancy:** Soothes emotions in others, dampening what they feel.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores mental speed — thinking slows while storing, quickens while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Feruchemical Cognitive powers.

---

### Triple III

*In silence and in seeking, the Mental metals guard their secrets. One guard has been reassigned.*

- **Allomancy:** Detects nearby Allomantic pulses — a seeker's gift.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores memories — forgetting while storing, recalling perfectly while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Allomantic Mental powers.

---

### Triple IV

*Alloy and base share a quadrant but not a destiny. The spike tells the difference.*

- **Allomancy:** Pulls on nearby metals, attracting them toward the Allomancer.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores physical weight — lighter while storing, heavier while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Feruchemical Physical powers.

---

### Triple V

*Reap what Allomancy sows, store what Feruchemy saves, steal what Hemalurgy takes. One of these lies.*

- **Allomancy:** Heightens all five senses dramatically.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores physical strength — weaker while storing, stronger while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Allomantic Mental powers.

---

### Triple VI

*The alloy's record looks right at first glance. A second glance reveals the theft.*

- **Allomancy:** Pushes on nearby metals, repelling them from the Allomancer.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores physical speed — slower while storing, faster while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Allomantic Physical powers.

---

### Triple VII

*In the Physical quadrant, strength and senses keep close company. One has crossed the fence.*

- **Allomancy:** Enhances physical strength, speed, balance, and endurance.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores one specific sense — diminished while storing, enhanced while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Feruchemical Mental powers.

---

### Triple VIII

*Seekers and smokers share more than a quadrant. Here, they have shared a gift not theirs to give.*

- **Allomancy:** Creates a coppercloud — hides Allomantic pulses from seekers.
- **Feruchemy:** Stores wakefulness — drowsy while storing, alert while tapping.
- **Hemalurgy:** Steals Allomantic Mental powers.

---

## Finding the Lie

Two descriptions in each triple correctly belong to the same metal. Together they identify it. The third has been taken from a neighboring metal — one that shares the same quadrant.

A useful pattern: in each quadrant, the base metal's Hemalurgic spike steals Allomantic powers of that quadrant's type, while the alloy's spike steals Feruchemical powers. The Physical base steals Allomantic Physical, the Physical alloy steals Feruchemical Physical, and so on. Knowing this pattern helps separate truth from theft.

---

## The Extraction

Eight triples, eight metals. Each triple has an introductory sentence in italics.

Identify all eight metals. Place them in table order, first through eighth. Read the first letter of each triple's italicized introduction in that order. The phrase that forms contains the answer. Five letters.

---

*Three arts, one table, eight lies. The symmetry would be beautiful if it were not so dangerous.*
