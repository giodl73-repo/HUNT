# P07 — The Lord Ruler's Thousand Years

**Electrum (#10)** · Module B (Scadrial History) · Round 1

---

> A millennium is a long time to sit on a throne. Events pile up. Memory blurs. The trick is getting them in the right order — the same trick Rashek himself could never quite manage, what with all that rewriting of history. Ten moments define his rise and fall. They arrive here scrambled, as history so often does.

---

## The Puzzle

Ten events from the history of Scadrial are listed below. They are not in chronological order. Arrange them from earliest to latest.

Once ordered, read the first letter of each event description from top to bottom. A word hides in the resulting string.

---

**A.** Having journeyed to the Well of Ascension as a Terris guide, a packman murders his companion and seizes the divine power held within.

**B.** Years of torment in the geode caves shatter something inside a prisoner — and ignite something far more dangerous. A Mistborn claws free.

**C.** Every river, every mountain, even the planet's orbit bends to a new god's will. Ash begins to fall from freshly raised volcanoes.

**D.** Rashek's spear finds the Survivor's heart in the streets of Luthadel. A rebellion loses its leader — though death proves a flexible concept.

**E.** A street urchin's fingers close around a pair of metal bracers and pull. A thousand years of compounded age arrive all at once.

**F.** The man who will one day be called the Survivor is cast into the Pits of Hathsin, sentenced to harvest atium until his body fails.

**G.** Standing where two gods fell, a quiet Terris scholar takes up both Shards and remakes the entire world in an afternoon.

**H.** Toward the dawn of all things, a single entity shatters. Sixteen Shards scatter across the cosmos. Two of them choose to build a world together.

**I.** No fewer than three armies descend on the capital — Venture's forces from one direction, Cett's from another, koloss from a third.

**J.** The power pooled at the Well pulses once more after a thousand years. It is released. An ancient prisoner walks free at last.
