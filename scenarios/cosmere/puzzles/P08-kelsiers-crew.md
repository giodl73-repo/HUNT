# P08 — Kelsier's Crew

**Gold (#9)** · Module B (Scadrial History) · Round 1

---

> Every great heist needs a crew. This one had eight. Some burned metals. Some did not. All of them mattered — though the ones who burn a single metal carry a street name, and street names carry letters. A number hides in every portrait below. The street knows what to do with it.

---

## The Puzzle

Eight members of Kelsier's crew are described below. Each portrait contains a number. Identify every crew member. For those who burn a single metal — the Mistings — find their street name from the reference below. The number in the portrait tells which letter of that street name to take. Mistborn and those who burn no metal contribute nothing. Read the collected letters top to bottom.

---

**I.** This one treats philosophy and violence as two faces of the same coin. Pewter hums through his veins. He asks questions nobody else considers, then settles every argument with his fists.

**II.** Born in ash-choked alleys, this one trusted no soul until a dead man's crew offered a reason. Every metal in the table burns at her command. She also ended a god.

**III.** A woodworker by trade. His coppercloud shields the crew from any Seeker within range. He keeps a shop with five apprentices and speaks fewer words than all of them combined.

**IV.** The man grins even at death. He burned every metal there is. He shattered the Pits of Hathsin. He shattered the Lord Ruler's grip on the skaa. Then he died, and that barely slowed him down.

**V.** The youngest by far. He speaks in two tongues — the Eastern street slang only his uncle half-understands, and the common speech he learns later. One of those tongues gave him his name. His single metal sharpens every sense he has.

**VI.** One brother chose open rebellion. This one chose the Steel Ministry, walking straight into the Inquisitors' domain as a spy. His metal let him Seek Allomantic pulses — until the day the Inquisitors made him one of their own.

**VII.** No metal burns inside this man. He does not need it. He is the ledger-keeper, the one who turns Mistborn bravado into a workable plan. The leader's oldest friend.

**VIII.** He calls himself a nobleman, which is only half a lie. His metal lets him dampen emotions — fear, anger, doubt, sorrow — four flavors of feeling he considers beneath the well-dressed.
