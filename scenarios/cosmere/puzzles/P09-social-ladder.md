# P09 — The Social Ladder

**Cadmium (#11)** · Module B (Scadrial History) · Round 1

---

> Rashek built his empire in layers. Every soul in a slot. Every slot on a rung. The rungs were never meant to move. Six tiers. A thousand years. One ladder. The descriptions below arrive out of order — as the Lord Ruler's subjects often did, when they tried to climb.

---

## The Puzzle

Six tiers of the Final Empire's social order are described below, each in a single paragraph. They are not arranged by rank. Identify which class each paragraph describes. Then sort the six tiers from the lowest rung to the highest. Read the first letter of each paragraph in that order.

---

**A.** Attendance at every contract, every wedding, every seal pressed into wax. Their eye-markings cannot be hidden. They watch and they record. The empire moves because they permit it to move. The Steel Ministry is their domain, and within that domain, even lords bend.

**B.** Their homeland lies in the north, a dominance shaped and reshaped by the Lord Ruler's hand. A thousand years of controlled breeding kept their talents useful and their ambitions small. Their scholars carry memories in metal, hiding them from those who would burn every last volume.

**C.** Imitation is a strange form of immortality. They were wraiths once, mindless and crawling, until a pair of metal blessings granted them thought. Now they consume the dead and wear their forms. Their Contracts bind tighter than any chain the Lord Ruler forged.

**D.** Linchpin. That is the word that matters. A single spike, seated just so, and the whole terrible mechanism falls apart. Before that moment, nothing short of decapitation slows one of them down. They see through spikes driven through their own eyes. Every stolen ability pulses in the metal.

**E.** Rusted, ash-covered, and beneath notice — except when someone needs a mine worked or a field tilled. Their blood is meant to be clean of Allomancy. It is not. The Lord Ruler's oldest lie concerns what runs in their veins and what does not.

**F.** Upheld by bloodline and by tradition. Their ancestors knelt before Rashek a millennium ago, and that single genuflection echoes through every generation since. Some of them burn metals. All of them attend balls. Their houses rise and fall like the ash itself.
