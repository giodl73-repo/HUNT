# P12 — The Roughs Justice

**Duralumin (#14)** · Module B (Scadrial History) · Round 1

---

> The Roughs have laws. Not many, and not always enforced, but they exist. What the Roughs do not have is a shortage of Twinborn criminals — individuals who burn one Allomantic metal and tap one Feruchemical metal, a combination that makes them unpredictable and dangerous. Six case files from the constable's desk. Each crime bears the signature of a specific pair of metallic arts. Identify both metals. The rest is arithmetic.

---

## The Puzzle

Six crimes are described below. Each was committed by a Twinborn: a person with one Allomantic ability (burning a metal) and one Feruchemical ability (storing/tapping a different metal). Identify both metals from the crime's description.

**Extraction rule:** For each crime, find the Allomantic metal's quadrant on the Allomancy table (Physical = 1, Mental = 2, Temporal = 3, Enhancement = 4). Count that many letters into the name of the Feruchemical metal. The letter at that position is the crime's contribution to the answer. Read the six letters in case-file order.

---

### Case File 1 — The Phantom Burglar

A vault on the fifth floor was emptied overnight. The thief scaled the outer wall with no rope and no ladder — witnesses on the street below said she weighed less than a feather. Once inside, she froze before the safe, staring at empty air for nearly a minute. The guards found her later, sitting on the floor, weeping. She told them she had seen herself as she might have been — a woman who chose a different path. Then she walked out carrying every coin in the vault, her footsteps heavier than any person's should be.

---

### Case File 2 — The Dialect Con

A stranger arrived in Weathering speaking the local patois as though born on its streets. Within an hour, a crowd gathered in the market square — calm at first, then blazing with fury that no one could explain. The stranger played the part of an outraged citizen, channeling the mob's inflamed emotions until it stormed the trading post. By dawn, the stranger had vanished, the post was cleaned out, and every witness recalled the rage but not the face of the man who stoked it.

---

### Case File 3 — The Impossible Shot

A lawman was struck dead at three hundred yards. No bullet was recovered — only a coin embedded in the adobe behind the body. An ordinary Coinshot cannot push a coin with that kind of accuracy at range. But this one could hear a man's breathing from across a dry lake bed and spot a tin badge glinting in moonlight half a mile off. The Roughs had never seen a marksman like this, and they hope not to again.

---

### Case File 4 — The Card Sharp

The woman at the Copper Kettle never lost. Three nights running. Perfect hands. The other players said she always knew what was coming — not what they held, but what she herself was about to draw. She saw her own next move a breath before she made it. When the table finally turned and the losing men drew knives, she took cuts that should have killed any ordinary person. By the time the constable arrived, her wounds had closed to scars. She walked out smiling, pockets full, blood drying on a shirt that no longer had holes.

---

### Case File 5 — The Ghost Heist

The Allomantic detectors at the bank entrance registered nothing — no pulses, no sign of any metallic art in use. Yet the vault was breached by a Lurcher's pull, dragging the iron lock clean out of its housing from across the room. How does a Lurcher pass a detector without a trace? The answer was in what the thief had given up. She had been storing her Identity — her very sense of self — in a metalmind for weeks before the job. She walked into the bank as a hollow shell, no spiritual signature, no self to detect. She Pulled the lock with iron. The detectors saw nothing because, in every way that mattered, no one was there.

---

### Case File 6 — The Distillery Heist

The thieves moved inside a shimmering bubble. Time inside the bubble raced forward — minutes inside for every second outside. The guards saw nothing but a flicker of heat-haze, and when it passed, every barrel was gone. Inside the bubble, the thieves had all the time in the world to load a wagon. The ringleader, it was later said, never tired. She could carry a barrel on each shoulder, strong beyond any natural frame, because strength was something she kept stored in metal and tapped when the job demanded it.
