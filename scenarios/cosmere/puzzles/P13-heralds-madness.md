# P13 — Herald's Madness

*Module C (Knights Radiant) | Dustbringers (#3) | Difficulty: ★★*

---

Four and a half thousand years is a long time to remain sane. Some managed better than others. Six accounts survive from the last days — each describing a Herald's condition. The accounts are filed by ward number.

The Heralds held Honorblades. Honorblades belonged to Orders. Orders claimed gemstones. Gemstones have letters. The ward number marks which letter to take.

---

## The Six Wards

**Ward 3.** She was the third to break, they say. She guarded the way between, and now guards nothing. The one called Chana — the Guard — lives among those she once protected, disguised, diminished. The fires she could have called remain unlit.

**Ward 7.** The King of Heralds is dead. Not broken — killed. The one who bore the Blade of Adhesion and Gravitation met an end no Oathpact could prevent. A knife, a traitor, and an era concluded.

**Ward 2.** The Soldier endured alone. Four and a half millennia of torment on Braize while nine others walked free. He returned at the end of an age, clutching nothing, speaking in fragments. The one who never abandoned the pact carried its full weight.

**Ward 4.** He calls himself a judge. The Herald of Justice still walks, still leads, still kills those who would bind Surges without permission. Alone among the ten, he kept his purpose — though the purpose may have kept him from seeing how far the world has turned.

**Ward 2.** The founder of the Radiants sits on a throne in Tukar and claims divinity. Two words suffice for his condition: utterly mad. The one once called Priest now demands worship, and the Surges of Tension and Adhesion bend to a shattered mind.

**Ward 4.** She destroys paintings. Statues. Murals. Anything bearing her own likeness. The daughter of the King of Heralds, she whose Blade once shaped Illumination and Transformation, cannot bear to see her own face rendered in stone or pigment.
