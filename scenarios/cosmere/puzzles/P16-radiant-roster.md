# P16 — Radiant Roster

*Module C (Knights Radiant) | Skybreakers (#2) | Difficulty: ★★★*

---

A roster of twelve names hangs in the Tower. Some names glow. Some do not. The glow meant a bond — a spren, an oath, a burden. The ones without glow carried burdens of a different kind.

Each entry below describes one figure from the war for Roshar. No names are given. Each description contains one word that matters more than the rest. It is marked.

Only the bonded contribute. Their marked words, read in the order their Orders are numbered, carry the message.

---

## The Twelve

**1.** A highprince's son. He fights with a dead Blade named for a woman he may yet save. He wears Plate he did not earn through oaths but through lineage and **dueling**. No spren speaks in his mind.

**2.** A king who united the highprinces through conquest and **feasting**. His ambition reached beyond the throne — into secret meetings with strange lights and stranger visitors. He was assassinated on the night of a treaty celebration. No oath. No bond. Just a legacy of war.

**3.** This one came from the bridgecrews. A **surgeon's** son who became a slave who became a soldier. The spren that chose him has the shape of a ribbon of light and the temperament of a drill sergeant. He swears oaths about protection.

**4.** Once a friend. Now something else. He killed a king and blinded himself to guilt. He carries a title the Fused gave him — a name that means **"He Who Quiets."** No oath binds him. No spren guides him. Only hatred moves.

**5.** He wrote a diagram on his most brilliant day and spent every other day trying to follow it. A king who bargained with something ancient for **intellect** on the good days and compassion on the bad. He ended up holding something far greater than a throne.

**6.** The Assassin in **White**. Truthless. Cast out by his people for speaking truth about the coming of the Desolation. He wielded an Honorblade first, then found something sharper. He swore himself to a code of justice under a Herald's direct tutelage.

**7.** She remembers being hungry. She remembers being **overlooked**. Now she steals food from the Azish emperor's kitchens and turns it into Stormlight. A cultivationspren follows her, taking the form of a vine, complaining endlessly.

**8.** He was the highprince who played the game better than anyone — alliances, betrayals, wars of **ambition** arranged like pieces on a board. He died for it. No bond. No oath. Just a knife from someone he underestimated.

**9.** The younger son. The one they worried about. His spren is corrupted — touched by something ancient and dark — and his visions work differently from what his Order would expect. He sees the future in **refracted** light, and the future terrifies him.

**10.** She spent decades studying fabrials. She documented their mechanisms, theorized about spren capture, and eventually bonded a being older than the Tower itself. Her discovery of a **new** form of Light changed the nature of the war.

**11.** A king who saw shapes in the mirrors and heard whispers he could not name. He was close — so close — to speaking the words that would seal a bond. A Cryptic circled him for months. Then a spear found his chest, and the **words** died with him.

**12.** He carried a bow when everyone else carried a spear. He cooked for his crew — the best stew on the Shattered Plains — and laughed louder than anyone in the barracks. He called himself a third son, and that meant something among his **native** people. He saw spren that others could not, but no spren chose him.
