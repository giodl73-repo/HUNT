# P25 — Shattered and Whole

*Module E (The 16 Shards) | Honor | Difficulty: ★★*

---

Sixteen pieces of a god. Some endure. Some were broken. Some chose stranger fates. One was there when it happened and took nothing — but that is a different story.

Below are sixteen fragments, each described by what became of it. The Intent has been excised. Six descriptions carry a number in brackets. That number marks a position within the Intent's name. The marked letters, read first through sixth, have something to say.

---

## The Sixteen Fragments

**A.** Oaths and bonds. The wind and the storm. This Shard's Vessel died long ago, its power Splintered by the god of hatred on a world of stone and light. A remnant persists in the storm itself.

**B.** Growth and patience. This Shard abides on the same world as the broken one above, its original Vessel still intact after all these millennia. The Nightwatcher serves it.

**C.** Hatred given form. Its first Vessel was killed — not by an enemy, but by an ambitious mortal who seized the power for himself. A new Vessel holds it now, trapped in a system of three worlds.

**D.** `[4]` This Shard believed in stasis. Its Vessel sacrificed himself to imprison his counterpart. Together with that counterpart, it created a world of ash and metal. The end-positive art draws from its power.

**E.** The union of two opposites. A Terrisman who once memorized the world's religions now holds both creation and destruction. The tension between the two Intents may prove unsustainable.

**F.** `[3]` Self-governance. Isolation. This Shard's Vessel sends avatars to many worlds but permits no one to approach its primary domain of white sand and dark side. Extremely territorial.

**G.** `[2]` Courage. Little else is confirmed. Its Vessel and home world remain unverified in published records. The name speaks for itself.

**H.** `[5]` Broken on the same world as a companion Shard. Both were Splintered by the god of hatred. Their combined power sank into the land and became the Dor — location-dependent, fueling strange scripts and bone-monks. This one was the love between them.

**I.** Authority and control. Splintered alongside the Shard described above. Same world. Same killer. Same fate — dissolved into the Dor. This one was the rule between them.

**J.** Generosity. Breath and color. Every person born on this Shard's world carries one divine gift. Some accumulate thousands. The Returned are its most visible agents.

**K.** `[2]` Entropy and decay. Its Vessel was slain, and the Shard was taken up alongside its opposite by a scholar. The end-negative art — the one that steals through metal spikes — draws from its power.

**L.** Drive and reach. First to be hunted by the god of hatred. Splintered near a world of forests and shades. The attack was not on its home world — it was caught while traveling.

**M.** Compassion. Little else is known. Its Vessel, world, and magic system remain undocumented. One of the quietest of the sixteen.

**N.** Caprice. Spontaneity. Another Shard about which almost nothing is known. The Intent was confirmed. The details were not.

**O.** The maker of new things. Name confirmed. Status uncertain. No known Vessel, world, or magic system in published texts.

**P.** `[7]` This Shard chose to break itself. No enemy forced the Splintering — it was voluntary. Among the sixteen, this fate is unique.
