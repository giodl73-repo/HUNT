# P26 — Vessel Roll Call

*Module E (The 16 Shards) | Preservation | Difficulty: ★★★*

---

Someone once knew all sixteen names. One still does. But that someone is not telling.

Eleven of the original Vessels are documented. Their names survive even where their persons did not. Below, each is described by temperament and fate — but the Shard they carried has been excised. Match the keeper to the kept. Six entries carry a number in brackets. That number indexes a letter in the Shard's Intent. The marked letters, read first through sixth, describe what every Vessel was before the Shattering changed them.

---

## The Eleven Known Keepers

**1.** He was cold. Calculating. He hated with precision. For millennia he hunted other Shards, Splintering three before being trapped by the remnants of his last victim. An ambitious mortal eventually took the power from him.

**2.** `[4]` He was a scholar. Not one of the original sixteen — he came later, after the dust settled, and picked up what two others had dropped. A Terrisman. He carries two opposing forces inside himself and calls the result something almost peaceful.

**3.** `[2]` A god of oaths. He held his Shard for millennia on a world of highstorms, standing as a pillar of structure among gods. The god of hatred broke him. His death was slow — ages of Splintering — and his remnant lingers in the storm.

**4.** He ruled. Alongside a companion Shard of love on the same world, he was Splintered by the god of hatred. His power merged with his companion's into the Dor. Short name. Four letters.

**5.** `[2]` He was, by all accounts, a decent person. The world he helped create was meant to keep itself intact. He gave everything to imprison his partner. When his power faded, he died — and in dying, bought time for a mortal girl to finish what he started.

**6.** `[5]` She loved. That is what the records say. She and a companion settled the same world — a place of scripts and glowing cities. The god of hatred found them both. Her power was shattered and dissolved into the land itself.

**7.** She gives freely. Every person born under her influence arrives with one Breath — a gift of life and color. She dwells on a world of vivid hues, and her Returned walk among the people as divine envoys.

**8.** `[1]` Her nature is drive, aspiration, the hunger to reach further. She was the first Shard to be actively hunted. Caught traveling between worlds, she was Splintered near Threnody. The shades in the Forests of Hell are her legacy.

**9.** She permits no intrusion. Her primary domain is a world of white sand and brutal sun, but she sends aspects of herself to other worlds — avatars, each with a measure of independence. She does not trust visitors.

**10.** He was called the god of decay. His Intent was entropy. He was slain, and a mortal girl carrying the power of his opposite ended him. His condensed body was a metal that bore his name.

**11.** `[3]` She is patient. She plans in centuries. She remains on the same storm-wracked world as the broken god of oaths, tending growth where he left ruin. Her original Vessel endures. The Nightwatcher is her creation.
