# P27 — Odium's Kill List

*Module E (The 16 Shards) | Odium | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

Four confirmed kills. One self-inflicted casualty. The god of hatred kept no journal, but the evidence speaks plainly enough.

Below are five reports — one for each Splintered Shard. They are filed in no particular order. Each report has two parts: an account of the Shard, and a bolded phrase describing the manner of its Splintering. One must determine which Shard is which, then arrange the five by the order in which they fell. The four killed by Odium came first. The one that broke itself came last — it had its own reasons.

The bolded manner-phrases, read in chronological order of Splintering, have something to say. First letters only. Where two fell in the same campaign, alphabetical order of Intent settles the tie.

---

## The Five Splinterings

**Report I.**

This Shard settled a world of highstorms with a companion. It represented binding oaths, structure, the keeping of promises. Its Vessel fought for ages but could not resist forever. The Splintering was gradual — a slow erosion across millennia rather than a single blow. A remnant persists in the great storm that circles the world.

Manner of Splintering: **Torn apart over ages of grinding attrition**

---

**Report II.**

This Shard never meant to be where it died. It was traveling between worlds when the hunter found it. Its Intent was drive, aspiration, the desire to reach ever further. The attack occurred near a world it had not chosen — a place of forests and dangerous shades. Those shades are the residue of the violence done here.

Manner of Splintering: **Waylaid between worlds before reaching safe harbor**

---

**Report III.**

This Shard settled a world of glowing cities alongside a companion whose Intent was love. Together their powers fused into something location-dependent — usable only in specific regions. This one represented control, authority, the imposition of will. Same attacker. Same day. Same fate as its companion.

Manner of Splintering: **Annihilated alongside a companion in a single campaign**

---

**Report IV.**

This Shard was not killed. It chose its own end. Among the sixteen original pieces of Adonalsium, it alone elected to Splinter itself — voluntarily, for reasons that remain unclear. No attacker. No campaign. A deliberate act of self-dissolution.

Manner of Splintering: **Her own Intent turned inward and unmade itself**

---

**Report V.**

Love, loyalty, the bonds between people. This Shard settled the same world as the Shard of authority described in another report. Both were Splintered by the god of hatred. Their combined Investiture sank into the planet, becoming a force called the Dor — tied to geography, powering scripts drawn in light.

Manner of Splintering: **Rent asunder on Sel, its power dissolving into the Dor**
