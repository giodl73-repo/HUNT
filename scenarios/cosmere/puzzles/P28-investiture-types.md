# P28 — Investiture Types

*Module E (The 16 Shards) | Endowment | Difficulty: ★★★*

---

Every Shard that settles a world leaves a mark. The mark is Investiture. The Investiture takes a form. The form becomes a magic system. One who has sampled several of these can confirm: they are all branches of the same tree, and the tree was shattered long ago.

Below are twelve descriptions of Cosmere magic systems. The system's name has been excised from each. Six carry a number in brackets. For those six, write the system name as one unbroken word — no spaces — and take the letter at the marked position. The marked letters, read first through sixth, describe what every person on Nalthis is from the moment of birth.

---

## The Twelve Systems

**I.** Metal is burned in the stomach. Power flows out — always more than went in. Sixteen metals, sixteen abilities. End-positive. Powered by the Shard of stasis on a world of ash.

**II.** `[9]` Color drains from the world. Each person born on this Shard's planet carries one Breath. Breaths accumulate. Enough of them animate objects on command. The Returned are its grandest product: dead made divine. The Shard endows freely.

**III.** Metal spikes driven through flesh. Attributes stolen from one person, transferred to another. Power is lost in the exchange. End-negative. Powered by the Shard of entropy. The cruelest of arts.

**IV.** `[7]` A bond with a spren. Two Surges per bond, ten Orders total. Fueled by light captured from storms. The oaths are the price. The Shard behind it valued binding promises above all else.

**V.** `[1]` Attributes stored in metal worn against the skin. Storing diminishes the user. Tapping enhances. End-neutral. Born not from one Shard but from the overlap of two opposing forces on the same world.

**VI.** Symbols drawn in the air — glowing lines that produce effects when completed correctly. Each symbol must match the local geography. Move too far from the right region, and the lines fail. Powered by a force called the Dor: the merged Investiture of two Shards Splintered on the same world.

**VII.** `[8]` White sand turns black when Investiture flows through it. The practitioners draw power from the sun on a world split between scorching daylight and frozen dark. The Shard behind this system guards its world with extreme prejudice, permitting no visitors.

**VIII.** Bone and flesh reshaped by chanting. Monks whose bodies become weapons. Like the symbol-drawing art in entry VI, this one draws from the Dor — the merged Investiture of two Splintered Shards. Same world, different discipline.

**IX.** `[5]` A stamp carved from soulstone is pressed to an object. The object's history is rewritten — not illusion, but genuine transformation, as if the past had played out differently. Temporary unless maintained. Also draws from the Dor, but operates without geographic restriction.

**X.** Boons and curses, granted by a spren in a valley. A petitioner asks for something, and the spren grants it alongside an unasked-for counterpart. Growth and pruning. The Shard behind it is patient.

**XI.** `[4]` The dark mirror of entry IV. Same world, different patron. Instead of spren bonds, ancient singers are reborn in new bodies. Instead of Stormlight, Voidlight. The Shard behind it is hatred, and its servants are called the Fused.

**XII.** A flowing martial art fueled by the same merged power as entries VI, VIII, and IX. Practiced on the same world. The body channels the Dor through motion. Graceful and lethal.
