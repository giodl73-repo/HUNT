# P29 — The 4x4 Shard Grid

*Module E (The 16 Shards) | Autonomy | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

Sixteen pieces. One suspects they were not scattered at random. There is a pattern — four categories along one axis, four along the other. The grid is not labeled. That is the first problem. The placements are the second.

Place all sixteen Shard Intents in a 4x4 grid. Discover what the rows and columns represent. Then read what the axes spell.

## Clues

**The axes:**

1. One axis groups Shards by what HAPPENED to them. The other groups them by the CHARACTER of their Intent. Each row and each column contains exactly four Shards.

2. The fate axis has four states: conquered, thriving, transformed, obscured. These are the rows. Row A contains the conquered Shards. Row B, the thriving.

3. The nature axis has four qualities, each named with a single word. The first letters of those words, columns 1 through 4, are A, S, P, E.

**Fate constraints:**

4. Honor and Dominion share a row. Both were Splintered by the same enemy.
5. Devotion shares a row with Honor. She was Splintered on a different world, by the same hand.
6. Ambition shares a row with Dominion. First to fall.
7. Cultivation and Autonomy share a row. Both are held by their original Vessels.
8. Odium and Endowment share the same row as Cultivation. All three still have active Vessels.
9. Preservation and Ruin share a row. Neither still exists in its original form.
10. Harmony shares a row with Preservation. It is what became of Preservation and Ruin combined.
11. Virtuosity shares a row with Harmony. It too changed form — it Splintered itself.
12. The remaining four Shards — Mercy, Valor, Whimsy, Invention — share a row. Almost nothing is known about any of them.

**Nature constraints:**

13. Devotion and Cultivation share a column. Both nurture.
14. Preservation shares a column with Devotion. It too sustained rather than destroyed.
15. Dominion and Odium share a column. Both impose by force.
16. Ruin shares a column with Dominion. All three in this column carry a destructive edge.
17. Honor and Autonomy share a column. Both concern themselves with principles.
18. Ambition and Endowment share a column. Both are about the individual — personal drive, personal gift.
19. Harmony shares a column with Honor — not with Preservation.
20. Virtuosity shares a column with Ambition — not with Preservation.
21. Each of the four unknown Shards (Mercy, Valor, Whimsy, Invention) belongs to a different column. Invention belongs in column 1. Valor belongs in column 2.
22. Mercy belongs in column 3. Whimsy belongs in column 4.
