# P31 — The Wanderer's Aliases

*Module F (Hoid & Worldhoppers) | Ruin | Difficulty: ★★★*

---

One has been many people. A storyteller on one world, a fool on another, a cabin boy on a third. Others wander too — soldiers, scholars, swordsmen — but they are not the one. The worlds carry many footprints. Only one set belongs to the person who matters.

Twelve encounters across the Cosmere are described below. Some feature the wanderer who was present at the Shattering. Some do not. The worlds remember who passed through them, even when the traveler wore a different face.

---

## The Twelve Encounters

**I.** On a world of twelve seas, each a different color of spore, a young woman sails to rescue the man she loves. Her cabin boy cannot speak — cursed to silence — but he narrates this story to us long after it ends. He has lived for a very long time.

**II.** In a grand city of color and breath, a man who once wielded the most dangerous sword ever created now lives in quiet exile. He has given up his thousands of divine gifts, keeping just enough to age slowly. He teaches swordsmanship to children and calls it honest work.

**III.** At a warcourt where lighteyes duel with words as sharp as Shardblades, the king employs a professional insulter. This insulter tells a bridgeman the story of a runner named Fleet. He plays a stringed instrument whose name no one recognizes.

**IV.** At a lake of warm water in a land of storms, three men wade together. One is broad-shouldered, with scars that speak of a decade fighting in pits. One is dark-skinned with a curious accent. The third is tall with oddly pale skin. They appear to be searching for something — or someone.

**V.** In a city of fallen gods and silver-skinned transformations, a beggar sits near the gates and offers cryptic directions to the one man who still believes the city can be healed. He tells a small story before vanishing.

**VI.** In a land of white sand and dark sky, a young woman studies the mechanics of Investiture with a scholarly intensity that borders on obsession. She travels with an armed companion who keeps detailed notebooks. She will one day author the definitive survey of the Cosmere's magic systems.

**VII.** On a world where an artist paints with light and a girl stacks stones to call spirits, the narrator interrupts his own story to editorialize. His companion — a spren who insists on literal truth — corrects him frequently. He has told this kind of story before, on other worlds, to other audiences.

**VIII.** In the warcamp of a highprince, a quiet man serves as a scout. He has no Shardblade, no obvious power, no noble blood. But he knows things about distant lands that no Rosharan should know. He came from somewhere with ash and mist.

**IX.** In a quiet palace where an empress has died and a forger labors under sentence of death, a man plays the role of a court fool. His appearance is brief. His foolishness is unconvincing.

**X.** A woman with a strange sword that does not match any known Shardblade design commands soldiers on the wall of a besieged city. She calls herself by a gemstone name, but her hair changes color in ways that have nothing to do with Stormlight.

**XI.** In a realm of shadow and thought, where a dead man stubbornly refuses to pass on, that dead man encounters a traveler walking with purpose toward a pool of liquid power. The traveler is amused. The dead man is not. They are not friends.

**XII.** A scarred man with a missing eye collects trophies from across the Cosmere. He keeps a vial of pale sand, a## the branch of a flowering tree, and other curiosities. He works for an organization that monitors movement between worlds — though "monitors" may be too gentle a word.
