# P32 — Cognitive Shadows

*Module F (Hoid & Worldhoppers) | Harmony | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

Death is not always the end. Across the Cosmere, certain individuals refuse to move on — or are refused permission to leave. A god's power holds them in place, a shadow of the mind persisting where the body has failed. The mechanism is always the same. The names change. The Shards responsible differ. But the stubbornness is universal.

Below are eight accounts of persistence beyond death. Not all of them describe the same phenomenon. Some are shadows of the mind, sustained by a Shard's Investiture. Others are something else entirely — constructs, splinters, or enchantments that merely resemble the dead. A careful reader knows the difference.

---

## The Eight Accounts

**A.** Ten warriors bound by an ancient pact. When one breaks under torture, all return to a world of storms to fight again. They have died and returned hundreds of times over thousands of years. Their sanity has not survived the repetition. All but one eventually walked away from the agreement. The one who stayed endured alone for four and a half millennia.

**B.** Ancient singers who served the god of hatred in a previous age. When they die, their souls do not pass on — they are reborn into the bodies of modern singers during each new conflict. Each one wields a single Surge. They remember every previous life. The rage has had millennia to ferment.

**C.** On a world of ash and mist, a man died publicly — impaled by the tyrant he defied. His body was buried. His mind refused to leave. He lingered in a realm of thought, unable to touch the physical world, watching his friends finish what he started. Centuries later, he is still there. He has found ways to remain relevant.

**D.** On a world of color and breath, certain individuals die and are sent back. They return with a divine gift — a single, powerful Breath that sustains them. They do not remember their previous life. They require one Breath per week to survive, tithed from the living. They are worshipped as gods, though most do not deserve the title.

**E.** A sentient blade, forged with a thousand divine gifts and a single Command: Destroy Evil. It hungers constantly. It speaks to whoever holds it. It is not a person who died — it is an object that was given a semblance of life. The distinction matters.

**F.** In forests where the rules are simple — do not kindle flame, do not shed blood, do not run at night — the dead wander as translucent figures. They were not chosen. No god sustains them deliberately. They are the aftermath of a violent cosmic event, a Shard torn apart far from its home. The dead are collateral.

**G.** Glowing constructs that orbit their masters on a distant world, each tied to a symbol from an ancient script. They carry messages, provide light, and serve as companions. They were not people. They are fragments of something divine, given form by the land's invested geography.

**H.** A spren in the Cognitive Realm, once bonded to a Knight Radiant. When the Knight broke their oath, the spren's mind was destroyed. It persists — eyes scratched out, shambling through the realm of thought — but its identity is gone. Recently, one such figure has begun to respond to the name it was given. Something stirs in Maya.

---

## The Matching

Six of the eight accounts describe true Cognitive Shadows — individuals whose minds persist after death, sustained by a Shard's Investiture. Two do not.

For each true Cognitive Shadow, identify which Shard's power holds them in place. Then find that Shadow's row and that Shard's column in the grid below. The letter at the intersection has been waiting.

The two accounts that are NOT Cognitive Shadows should be set aside. Their intersections lead nowhere.
