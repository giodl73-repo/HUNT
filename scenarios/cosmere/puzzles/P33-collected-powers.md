# P33 — Collected Powers

*Module F (Hoid & Worldhoppers) | Cultivation | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

One has been collecting for a very long time. A power from this world, an art from that one, a gift from a third. Most people master one magic system and consider themselves fortunate. One has never been most people.

Below are eight vignettes of the same individual using abilities acquired from across the Cosmere. Two of the eight are false — they describe powers this individual has never demonstrated in published record. The remaining six are genuine. For each genuine power, identify the specific name of the ability or investiture being used.

Patience. The names are precise.

---

## The Eight Vignettes

**I.** He stands before a crowd and the air shimmers. An image appears — not of Stormlight, not of Breath, but of something older. A craft from a world that existed before any god took a name. The illusions are beautiful and they owe nothing to any Shard.

**II.** He swallowed something precious. A bead of condensed divinity, meant for making gods, wasted instead on a wanderer who merely wished to burn metals. The bead was made from a god's own body — the god of stasis, the one who believed in preservation above all else.

**III.** He always knows where to go next. Not why, not what he will find, but where. The sense defies explanation. It is not Allomancy. It is not Surgebinding. It predates both. He follows it between worlds like a compass that points not north but toward necessity.

**IV.** He speaks a single word — a Command — and the rope in his hand twitches, then moves. Color drains from his shirt as investiture flows into the object. The rope ties itself into a knot. This is an art of words and color, learned on a world where every soul is born carrying one gift of this kind.

**V.** He walks among the plagued and the dying. He reaches out, and the sickness retreats from the skin. Wounds close. Bones knit. He has never been able to do this before. This is new, and it frightens him as much as it comforts the healed.

**VI.** Colors are brighter near him than they should be. His clothing is more vivid. His hair has a sheen. He carries something invisible — a reservoir of investiture acquired on a world of color. He did not earn it. He accumulated it. He holds more than one.

**VII.** He bonds something that insists on telling the truth. A strange companion, all angles and shifting patterns, who corrects his exaggerations and resents his embellishments. The bond is Rosharan in nature. It has a name — an old word for the connection between spren and human.

**VIII.** He touches a piece of metal and it reshapes under his fingers, flowing like clay. He does not burn it. He does not store anything in it. He reshapes matter through a form of Connection tied to symbols and geography, powered by a force that sank into the land when two Shards were broken on the same world.
