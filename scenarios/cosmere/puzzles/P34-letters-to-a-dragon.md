# P34 — Letters to a Dragon

*Module F (Hoid & Worldhoppers) | Devotion | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

One writes letters. One has always written letters. Across millennia and between worlds, words travel — carried by means that have nothing to do with spanreeds. The recipients and senders vary: a dragon who refuses to act, gods who refuse to listen, a wanderer who refuses to stop asking. The Stormlight Archive preserves fragments of these exchanges in its epigraphs.

Below are eight such letters, paraphrased, with the sender's name removed. The voice of each letter reflects the writer's nature. A god of stasis writes differently from a god of growth. A dragon writes differently from a wanderer.

Identify who wrote each letter. Write their name — a Shard's Intent for Shards, a personal name for mortals and immortals — in the blanks provided. Then attend to the marks.

---

## The Eight Letters

**A.** "This is not my concern. I made my choice long ago. I will not interfere. The cost of involvement is always higher than it appears, and I have lived long enough to know the difference between wisdom and cowardice. One of us chose to act. One of us chose to watch. I remain the one who watches. Do not write again."

**B.** "Growth requires pruning. The dead branches must fall so the living ones may reach the sun. I have my own plans for this world, and they account for the threat you describe. Your interference is neither required nor entirely unwelcome. Come if you wish. But do not presume to understand what I am tending. The garden is older than your memory."

**C.** "I hold two things that oppose each other, and the strain is considerable. You understand opposition better than most — you chose to hold nothing, which is its own kind of burden. The enemy you describe concerns me. I have seen what he did to the one who held bonds, and to the one who held stasis. I will watch. I will act when the time demands it. But do not mistake my patience for agreement with your methods."

**D.** "One has a favor to ask. It has been a very long time since one has asked anything of anyone, which should indicate the severity of the matter. The god of hatred moves between worlds, and what he leaves behind does not recover. One has tried subtlety. One has tried stories. Neither has been sufficient. What is needed now is help — not power, just presence. That is all."

**E.** "The request is denied. I have given my gifts freely to those born under my care, and I will give no more to wanderers who collect what does not belong to them. My people receive what I offer. Outsiders do not. The generosity you describe as reluctance is, in fact, precision. Take your quest elsewhere."

**F.** "I believed in stasis. In preservation. In holding the world exactly as it was, because change meant loss and loss meant entropy. I sacrificed myself to prevent the worst, and another took what I left behind. If you are reading this, the worst has happened anyway. The metals still sing. The mists still rise. That is all I could ensure."

**G.** "I held love. It was taken from me — not by the god of hatred himself, but by what he became when he arrived on our world. The land remembers what I was. The scripts remember. The Dor carries what I gave, mixed with what another gave, and the people still write their prayers in shapes that echo us both. I did not survive. But what I held does not require a living vessel to persist."

**H.** "Do not approach my worlds. Do not contact my agents. Do not speak my name to those I have chosen to protect. I operate independently and have done so since the beginning. My isolation is not weakness — it is policy. If you approach my shores again, I will respond with more than words."
