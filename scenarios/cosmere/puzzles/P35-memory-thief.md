# P35 — The Memory Thief

*Module F (Hoid & Worldhoppers) | Whimsy | Difficulty: ★★★★*

---

One has perfect memory. Or one did, until recently. In the final moments of a conversation with a new god — a god wearing the face of an old king — something was taken. Altered. The memories are still there, but some of them are wrong now, and the worst part is not knowing which ones.

Below are eight of the wanderer's memories. Each describes an event the wanderer witnessed across the Cosmere. Six of these memories have been tampered with — one detail in each has been changed. The alteration is subtle: a wrong name, a wrong metal, a wrong Shard, a wrong place. The kind of error that feels almost right.

Two memories remain untouched. They are accurate in every detail.

The wanderer needs help. Cross-reference each memory against what is known. Find the six errors. The wrong words carry the weight of what was done.

---

## The Eight Memories

**I.** "One remembers swallowing a bead of condensed **Stormlight** in the caverns beneath Kredik Shaw. It burned going down. The power expanded through the stomach like liquid fire, and afterward, every metal in the world sang with possibility. One has been a Mistborn ever since."

**II.** "One remembers Kelsier's crew. The Thug they called Ham — a man who loved philosophical arguments and burned **tin** with the subtlety of a charging koloss. He could arm-wrestle a Steel Inquisitor and almost win. Almost."

**III.** "One remembers Dalinar Kholin at the Battle of Thaylen Field, arms spread wide, refusing to yield to the god of hatred. He spoke the most important words a man can say. The Stormfather wept. One has seen many things, but that was among the finer ones."

**IV.** "One remembers the Heralds. Ten warriors bound by the **Oathgate**, returning each time a Desolation threatened, enduring torture on Braize between cycles. Nine of them eventually walked away from the agreement. The tenth — the Soldier — held on alone for four and a half thousand years."

**V.** "One remembers watching Vin confront the Lord Ruler. She pulled the **lerasium** bracers from his arms and the centuries caught up with him all at once. A thousand years of stolen youth, repaid in an instant. He aged, and died, and the longest tyranny in Scadrial's history ended with a girl's hands."

**VI.** "One remembers the Returned on Nalthis. Gods in human form who did not remember their mortal lives. **Elhokar**, who called himself a god of bravery, was the most entertaining. He spent his divine days philosophizing about the nature of purpose and occasionally judging art contests. He did not deserve worship, but then, most gods do not."

**VII.** "On Sel, in a city where the walls glowed and the fallen had skin like silver, one sat near the gates and offered directions to a man who still believed the city could be healed. His name was Raoden. One told him a story — short, pointless by most standards — and left before the ending. One tends to do that."

**VIII.** "One remembers the Shattered Plains. A wasteland of broken plateaus and chasms, where the Alethi waged war against the **Natan** for possession of gemhearts. Bridge crews ran across the chasms carrying portable bridges under arrow fire. One watched a particular bridgeman survive things that should have killed him. He was the sort of person who makes a wanderer's job easier."

---

## The Task

For each memory, determine whether it is **accurate** or **tampered**.

For each tampered memory, identify the specific word or phrase that has been altered — the element that does not match the known record. Write that wrong element in the error column.

The first letter of each wrong element, read in order from Memory I through Memory VIII, spells what was done to the wanderer.
