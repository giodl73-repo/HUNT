# The Camouflage Club

**★★★** | By Nadia Koresh

Five field biologists meet monthly to share findings on nature's greatest masters of disguise. Each studies a different animal, works in a different habitat, and relies on a different observation tool. Ten clues will guide you to the full solution — but one clue is false. It has camouflaged itself among the truthful statements. Identify the impostor, discard it, and solve the grid using the remaining eight.

---

## Members
Alice, Blake, Carmen, Devi, Elias

## Animals
Arctic fox, Chameleon, Cuttlefish, Octopus, Stick insect

## Habitats
Coral reef, Mangrove, Rainforest, Tundra, Woodland

## Tools
Binoculars, Camera trap, Diving mask, Magnifying glass, Thermal scope

---

## Clues

1. The person who studies the arctic fox works in the tundra.

2. Blake uses the diving mask and works at the coral reef.

3. Alice studies the stick insect.

4. Carmen does not study either the chameleon or the octopus.

5. Devi uses the magnifying glass.

6. The person with the magnifying glass studies the stick insect.

7. Elias studies the chameleon and works in the rainforest.

8. The cuttlefish researcher works in the mangrove.

9. The arctic fox researcher uses the thermal scope.

10. The researcher in the woodland uses a camera trap.

---

**One of the ten clues above is false. Which one?**

*Hint: Try assuming each clue is false in turn. Only one assumption leads to a complete, consistent solution.*
