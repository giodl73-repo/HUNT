# Plain Sight

**★★** | By Felix Ortega

A quiet afternoon at the Greenfield Public Library. Nothing out of the ordinary — unless you know where to look. Ten common objects have disguised themselves among the shelves, fixtures, and furniture. Find them all.

---

## The Illustration

*[Full spread: detailed line-art illustration of a library interior. The scene includes: tall bookshelves, a circulation desk, reading tables with lamps, an arched window, a chandelier, a globe on a pedestal, a card catalog, potted plants, a spiral staircase, wall sconces, a fireplace, armchairs, scattered open books, and patrons browsing. The illustration is rich in detail and rendered in a clean, crosshatch style suitable for B&W reproduction.]*

*[Art direction: Each of the 10 hidden objects is integrated into the architectural or decorative detail of the scene. The objects are not floating or obviously inserted — they are formed by the lines of the illustration itself. A CROWN is formed by the peaks of the chandelier. An ANCHOR is embedded in the ironwork of the spiral staircase railing. A MUSHROOM appears in the silhouette of a table lamp. And so on. The difficulty ranges from easy (the crown in the chandelier) to challenging (the umbrella formed by negative space between two bookshelves).]*
