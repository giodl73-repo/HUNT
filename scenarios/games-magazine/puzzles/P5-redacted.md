# Redacted

**★★★★** | By Margaret Huang

History is full of secrets kept in plain sight. Invisible ink on parchment. Microfilm in a hollow coin. Messages woven into tapestries. The essay below tells eight such stories — but in each case, a key word has been blacked out. Recover each redacted word from context. Then extract the letter indicated by the superscript number beside each blank. Read the eight extracted letters in order to reveal a final hidden word.

Among flowering plants, the orchid genus **[██████]**^6 (6) achieves pollination through pure deception. Its flowers mimic the appearance and pheromone scent of female wasps so convincingly that males attempt to mate with the petals, pollinating the flower unwittingly.

And in the sixteenth century, the French diplomat Blaise de **[████████]**^4 (8) devised a polyalphabetic cipher so resistant to analysis that it was known as "le chiffre indechiffrable" — the indecipherable cipher. For three hundred years his system defeated every known method of attack.
