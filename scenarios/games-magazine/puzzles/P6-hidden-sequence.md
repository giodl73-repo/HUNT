# The Hidden Sequence

**★★★★★** | By Nadia Koresh

A grid of numbers. Most are noise. But hidden within the grid are exactly six arithmetic sequences — runs of four consecutive cells where the values increase or decrease by a constant difference. The sequences may run horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. Find all six. Then look at what remains.

## Instructions

1. **Find all 6 arithmetic sequences.** Each sequence is exactly 4 cells long (in a line — horizontal, vertical, or diagonal). In an arithmetic sequence, the difference between consecutive terms is constant. Example: 10, 15, 20, 25 (difference = +5).

2. **Mark the cells.** Each cell that belongs to a sequence gets circled. Some cells may belong to more than one sequence — that is fine.

3. **Read the remainders.** After marking all sequence cells, some cells will be unmarked. Read the unmarked cells left to right, top to bottom. Pair the digits into two-digit numbers. Convert each pair using A=01, B=02, ... Z=26 to spell the answer.
