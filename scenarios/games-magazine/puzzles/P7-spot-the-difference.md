# Spot the Difference

**★★** | By Sam Whitfield

Two descriptions of the same scene. Nearly identical — but six words have each had a single letter changed, turning one real English word into a different real English word. Find all six changes. Note the letter in Version B that replaces the original. Read those six letters in order of appearance.

---

### Version A

The child was born in the old farmhouse by the river. A lime tree grew beside the gate, and morning light fell across the stone threshold. The long hallway smelled of woodsmoke and dried herbs. Every surface bore a faint tint of gold from years of candlelight. The farmer stacked each bale of hay with care before winter came.

### Version B

The child was burn in the old farmhouse by the river. A line tree grew beside the gate, and morning sight fell across the stone threshold. The lone hallway smelled of woodsmoke and dried herbs. Every surface bore a faint tine of gold from years of candlelight. The farmer stacked each bane of hay with care before winter came.
