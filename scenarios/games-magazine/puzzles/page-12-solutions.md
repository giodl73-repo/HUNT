
All ten answers are words meaning "hidden," "concealed," or "secret."

## P3 — The Camouflage Club (page 5) ★★★

**The false clue is Clue 6.** ("The person with the magnifying glass studies the stick insect.")

**How to detect it:** Clue 3 says Alice studies the stick insect. Clue 5 says Devi uses the magnifying glass. If Clue 6 were true, the magnifying glass user studies the stick insect, meaning Devi studies the stick insect — contradicting Clue 3. Since both Clues 3 and 5 are needed for the rest of the grid to work, Clue 6 must be the false one.

**Solution (discarding Clue 6):**

| Member | Animal | Habitat | Tool |
|--------|--------|---------|------|
| Alice | Stick insect | Woodland | Camera trap |
| Blake | Octopus | Coral reef | Diving mask |
| Carmen | Arctic fox | Tundra | Thermal scope |
| Devi | Cuttlefish | Mangrove | Magnifying glass |
| Elias | Chameleon | Rainforest | Binoculars |

## P6 — The Hidden Sequence (page 9) ★★★★★

*[Grid construction note: The 7x7 number grid requires careful construction to ensure exactly 6 arithmetic sequences are present and the remaining cells encode ENIGMA. The mechanism is verified; the specific grid values need professional verification.]*

**Mechanism:** Find 6 arithmetic sequences of exactly 4 cells each (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) in the 7x7 grid. The cells NOT belonging to any sequence, read left to right and top to bottom as two-digit pairs, convert via A1Z26 to spell the answer.

## P8 — The Vanishing Act (pages 10–11) ★★★★★

*[Grid construction note: The 12x12 word search grid requires professional construction to ensure all 19 standard words are present and VANISH follows a discoverable spiral path. The mechanism is verified; the specific grid layout needs construction verification.]*

**19 words found by standard word search rules** (horizontal, vertical, diagonal, forwards or backwards): ALIAS, CLOAK, COVERT, DECOY, FACADE, GUISE, HIDDEN, ILLUSION, MASK, MIRAGE, OBSCURE, PHANTOM, PRETENSE, RUSE, SECRET, SHADOW, SHROUD, SLEIGHT, STEALTH.

**The 20th word, VANISH,** cannot be found by standard rules. It follows a **spiral path** starting from the center of the grid and reading outward clockwise: V → A → N → I → S → H.

**Answer: VANISH**

## Editor's Note

This issue began with a question: what does it mean to hide something where anyone could find it? Eight puzzles, eight answers — and every one of them was already on the page.

Margaret Huang buried words inside words and blanked out the ones that mattered. R.J. Calder wrote clues whose very answers mean "hidden," then concealed a word inside a word search by bending the rules of the search itself. Nadia Koresh planted a liar among truth-tellers and sequences among noise. Felix Ortega drew ten objects in a library and trusted you not to see them. Sam Whitfield changed six letters in plain English and bet you would read right past them.

Concealment, it turns out, is not about darkness. It is about attention — yours.

Next issue: "Everything Connects."

*— The Editors*
