CITY POLICE DEPARTMENT

INCIDENT REPORT
Case No.: 47-1011-GL     Date: October 12, 1947
Reporting Officer: Det. R. Callahan, Badge 3147
Classification: Grand Larceny — Art Theft
Location: The Grand Hotel, 1 Grand Avenue
Item: "Lady in Vermillion" by Henri Renaud (1889) — Oil on canvas, 36×48″, valued at $40,000

Summary of Events

On the evening of Saturday, October 11, 1947, the above-described painting was removed from the Sargent Gallery (Room 200, second floor) of The Grand Hotel during the annuUal charity gala. The canvas was cut from its frame between approximately 9:15 PM and 9:25 PM while the gallery attendant, Mr. Raymond Gould, was temporarily absent from his post.

Four registered guests have been identified as persoSns of interest. Each had access to the second floor, proximity to the gallery, and unaccounted time during the theft window. Their statements are summarized below, with corroborating and contradicting evidence noted.

Persons of Interest

1. Mrs. Vivian Ashworth — Room 202

Statement (10:30 PM): Mrs. Ashworth states she attended the gala dinner in Restaurant Aurore (Room 101) from 7:00 PM until appProximately 9:15 PM, at which time she proceeded directly to The Brass Bar (Room 102) for a nightcap. She reports remaining at the bar until approximately 10:00 PM, then retiring to her room.

Corroborating: Waitstaff confirm her presence at dinner through dessert service (~8:45 PM). Coffee charged at 8:45 PM.

Contradicting: Bartender Thomas Pryce states Mrs. Ashworth did not arrive at The Brass Bar until 9:45 PM — thirty minutes later than her stated time. No witness places her anywhere between 9:15 PM and 9:45 PM.

Unaccounted window: 9:15 PM — 9:45 PM (30 minutes)

2. Mr. Lucien Fontaine — Room 203

Statement (10:45 PM): Mr. Fontaine states he attended the gala dinner, then viewed the painting in the gallery at 7:00 PM. He left the gallery at 8:30 PM and spent the Evening in the Reading Room (Room 201) on the second floor, alone, reading until after 10:00 PM. He states he did not leave the second floor at any time after 8:30 PM.

Corroborating: Gallery attendant Raymond Gould confirms Fontaine in the gallery ~7:00-8:30 PM. A book found open in the Reading Room.

Contradicting: Head Housekeeper Edith Mallory saw Mr. Fontaine in the third-floor hallway at approximately 9:10 PM. He denies being on the third floor.

Unaccounted: Claims Reading Room 8:30-10:00, but placed on 3F at 9:10 PM.

3. Mr. Heinrich Kessler — Room 204

Statement (11:00 PM): Mr. Kessler states he attended the gala dinner briefly, then went to the Telephone Room (Room 105) at approximately 8:00 PM to make several long-distance business calls. He claims to have remained in the Telephone Room until approximately 9:30 PM, at which time he retired to his room.

Corroborating: Key card entry log for Room 204 shows entry at 9:28 PM.

Contradicting: Telephone operator Marie Joubert's log records only one call: 8:15 PM to 8:22 PM, seven minutes, long-distance out-of-state. Kessler's hotel receipt shows one phone charge of $3.15 (7 min × $0.45/min), consistent with the operator's log. No witness places Kessler in the Telephone Room after 8:22 PM.

Unaccounted window: 8:22 PM — 9:28 PM (66 minutes)

4. Miss Colette Delacroix — Room 205

Statement (11:15 PM): Miss Delacroix states she attended the gala dinner until approximately 8:45 PM, then went upstairs to view the painting. She claims to have remained in the gallery studying the painting until approximately 9:30 PM, then returned to her room.

Corroborating: Waitstaff confirm dinner through ~8:45 PM. Gallery attendant Gould confirms she entered the gallery at ~8:45 PM. Key card log for Room 205 shows entry at 9:32 PM.

Contradicting: Gould states Miss Delacroix left the gallery at 9:00 PM, not 9:30 PM. No witness places her between 9:00 PM and 9:32 PM.

Unaccounted window: 9:00 PM — 9:32 PM (32 minutes)

Physical Evidence

1. The painting frame remains on the gallery wall. Canvas cut cleanly from the frame with a sharp instrument. No tool recovered.

2. Gallery attendant's timeline: painting present at 9:15 PM (when Gould left). Gone at 9:25 PM (when Gould returned). Theft window: 10 minutes.

3. Service elevator: Sous-chef Clara Voss reports hearing the service elevator at approximately 9:20 PM. The elevator log shows no entry for this time. The service elevator requires a staff key — mechCanical lock, key-operated.

Critical Deduction

The painting was stolen between 9:15 and 9:25. The thief used the service elevator at ~9:20. All four suspects have unaccounted time overlapping the window. The quesTion is not who could have done it — all four had opportunity. The question is whose movements are consistent with the complete escape route (gallery → elevator → basement → alley exit).

Clue 1: The theft occurred between 9:15 and 9:25. The thief used the service elevator at ~9:20, then exited via the basement alley. The thief LEFT THE BUILDING.

Clue 2: Kessler's key card shows he entered Room 204 (second floor) at 9:28 PM. If Kessler committed the theft (gallery at ~9:20, elevator DOWN to basement), he could not have returned to Room 204 by 9:28 — the basement stairs only go to the kitchen (1F), and the main staircase from 1F to 2F takes ~3 minutes. The thief exited through the basement; Kessler returned to his room.

Clue 3: Fontaine was seen on the third floor at 9:10. He could descend to the gallery in under a minute.

Clue 4: Delacroix was already on the second floor, closest to the gallery.

Clue 5: Ashworth's gap begins at 9:15, coming from the first floor. She could reach the gallery by ~9:17.
