# Dossier Cover Sheet -- Case Briefing

**Prop ID:** PR02
**Type:** document (printed page)
**Material:** Cardstock (65lb or heavier), letter size (8.5 x 11 in)
**Print:** Single-sided, black ink
**Position in dossier:** Top page (first thing the team sees)
**Print-ready file:** `delivery/print/puzzles/cover-sheet.html`

---

## Purpose

The cover sheet establishes the fiction and the assignment. It is written in the voice of Det. R. Callahan -- terse, official, slightly weary. It tells the team who they are (investigators), what happened (painting stolen), what they need to do (examine four exhibits, determine the method), and how to submit (website).

The cover sheet also serves as the orientation document: it lists all four exhibits by label (A through D) and by dimension (SUSPECT, LOCATION, TIME, OBJECT), giving the team a roadmap for the dossier.

---

## Content

```
                    CITY POLICE DEPARTMENT
                    INVESTIGATIVE DIVISION

                    CASE BRIEFING
                    -------------

Case No.:       47-1011-GL
Date:           October 12, 1947
Classification: Grand Larceny -- Art Theft
Location:       The Grand Hotel, 1 Grand Avenue


SUMMARY

A painting was stolen from the Sargent Gallery of The Grand Hotel
during last night's charity gala. The work -- "Lady in Vermillion"
by Henri Renaud, 1889 -- was cut from its frame between 9:15 PM
and 9:25 PM. Four suspects. Four pieces of evidence.


YOUR ASSIGNMENT

Examine all four exhibits. Determine:

  * The SUSPECT  (Exhibit A -- The Police Report)
  * The LOCATION (Exhibit B -- The Hotel Receipt)
  * The TIME     (Exhibit C -- The Floor Plan)
  * The OBJECT   (Exhibit D -- The Witness Statement)

When all four findings are complete, use the evidence log and
the calling card to determine THE METHOD -- how the painting
was taken.


EXHIBITS ENCLOSED

Exhibit A -- Incident Report (police report with suspect alibis)
Exhibit B -- Billing Statement (hotel receipts for all four suspects)
Exhibit C -- Architectural Survey (floor plans of The Grand Hotel)
Exhibit D -- Witness Statement (interview transcript, Miss Delacroix)
Evidence Log -- Case summary and correlation worksheet
Physical Evidence -- Hotel key card, calling card (loose items)

Submit your final answer at the answer submission site.

The trail is going cold.


                    -------------
                    Det. R. Callahan
                    Badge 3147
```

## Layout Notes

- **Header:** "CITY POLICE DEPARTMENT / INVESTIGATIVE DIVISION" centered in a double-ruled border box (Georgia, serif, 16pt / 13pt italic)
- **Body:** Courier New, 11pt, 1.5 line spacing
- **Field labels:** Bold, left-aligned, 140px width (Case No., Date, Classification, Location)
- **Assignment box:** Thin single rule border, 16px padding. Bullet items indented.
- **Signature:** Centered at bottom, horizontal rule above name and badge number
- **CONFIDENTIAL watermark:** Optional. Light grey (rgba 139,0,0,0.06), diagonal, 60pt Georgia. Adds atmosphere without impeding readability.
- **Margins:** 0.75in all sides

---

## Voice Calibration

Terse. Factual. Official. No exclamation marks. No questions except the implicit "can you solve it?" buried in the assignment. The final line -- "The trail is going cold." -- is the only concession to atmosphere. It lands because everything before it is dry.
