## SUMMARY OF EVENTS

On the evening of Saturday, October 11, 1947, the above-described painting was removed from the Sargent Gallery (Room 200, second floor) of The Grand Hotel during the annual charity gala. The canvas was cut from its frame between approximately 9:15 PM and 9:25 PM while the gallery attendant, Mr. Raymond Gould, was temporarily absent from his post.

Four registered guests have been identified as persons of interest. Each had access to the second floor, proximity to the gallery, and unaccounted time during the theft window. Their statements are summarized below, with corroborating and contradicting evidence noted.

---

## PERSONS OF INTEREST

### 1. Mrs. Vivian Ashworth — Room 202

**Statement given 10:30 PM, October 11:**
Mrs. Ashworth states she attended the gala dinner in Restaurant Aurore (Room 101) from 7:00 PM until approximately 9:15 PM, at which time she proceeded directly to The Brass Bar (Room 102) for a nightcap. She reports remaining at the bar until approximately 10:00 PM, then retiring to her room.

**Corroborating evidence:**
- Waitstaff confirm her presence at dinner through the dessert service (approximately 8:45 PM)
- Coffee was charged to her room at 8:45 PM (receipt on file)

**Contradicting evidence:**
- Bartender Thomas Pryce states Mrs. Ashworth did not arrive at The Brass Bar until 9:45 PM — thirty minutes later than her stated time
- No witness places her anywhere in the hotel between 9:15 PM and 9:45 PM

**Unaccounted window: 9:15 PM — 9:45 PM (30 minutes)**

---

### 2. Mr. Lucien Fontaine — Room 203

**Statement given 10:45 PM, October 11:**
Mr. Fontaine states he attended the gala dinner, then went to view the painting in the gallery at 7:00 PM. He left the gallery at 8:30 PM and spent the remainder of the evening in the Reading Room (Room 201) on the second floor, alone, reading until after 10:00 PM. He states he did not leave the second floor at any time after 8:30 PM.

**Corroborating evidence:**
- Gallery attendant Raymond Gould confirms Fontaine was in the gallery from approximately 7:00 PM to 8:30 PM
- A copy of "The Connoisseur's Guide to French Painting" was found open on the Reading Room table

**Contradicting evidence:**
- Head Housekeeper Edith Mallory states she saw Mr. Fontaine in the third-floor hallway at approximately 9:10 PM
- He denies being on the third floor at any time during the evening

**Unaccounted window: Claimed Reading Room 8:30-10:00, but placed on 3F at 9:10 PM**

---

### 3. Mr. Heinrich Kessler — Room 204

**Statement given 11:00 PM, October 11:**
Mr. Kessler states he attended the gala dinner briefly, then went to the Telephone Room (Room 105) at approximately 8:00 PM to make several long-distance business calls. He claims to have remained in the Telephone Room until approximately 9:30 PM, at which time he retired to his room (204).

**Corroborating evidence:**
- Key card entry log for Room 204 shows entry at 9:28 PM

**Contradicting evidence:**
- Telephone operator Marie Joubert's log records only one call from the Telephone Room that evening: 8:15 PM to 8:22 PM, seven minutes, long-distance out-of-state
- Kessler's hotel receipt shows one phone charge of $3.15 (7 minutes at $0.45/min), consistent with the operator's log
- No witness places Kessler in the Telephone Room after 8:22 PM

**Unaccounted window: 8:22 PM — 9:28 PM (66 minutes)**

---

### 4. Miss Colette Delacroix — Room 205

**Statement given 11:15 PM, October 11:**
Miss Delacroix states she attended the gala dinner until approximately 8:45 PM, then went upstairs to view "Lady in Vermillion" in the Sargent Gallery. She claims to have remained in the gallery studying the painting until approximately 9:30 PM, then returned to her room.

**Corroborating evidence:**
- Waitstaff confirm her presence at dinner through approximately 8:45 PM
- Gallery attendant Raymond Gould confirms she entered the gallery at approximately 8:45 PM
- Key card entry log for Room 205 shows entry at 9:32 PM

**Contradicting evidence:**
- Gallery attendant Gould states Miss Delacroix left the gallery at 9:00 PM, not 9:30 PM as she claims
- No witness places her between 9:00 PM and 9:32 PM (when her key card was used)

**Unaccounted window: 9:00 PM — 9:32 PM (32 minutes)**

## PHYSICAL EVIDENCE

1. **The painting frame** remains on the gallery wall. The canvas was cut cleanly from the frame with a sharp instrument, likely a razor or utility knife. No tool was recovered.

2. **Gallery attendant's timeline:** Mr. Gould confirms the painting was present at 9:15 PM when he left his post briefly. It was gone at 9:25 PM when he returned. Theft window: 10 minutes.

3. **Service elevator:** Sous-chef Clara Voss reports hearing the service elevator at approximately 9:20 PM. The elevator log shows no entry for this time. The service elevator requires a staff key to operate.

4. **Key card log (mechanical system):**

## CRITICAL DEDUCTION

Cross-reference the evidence:

**Clue 1:** The theft occurred between 9:15 and 9:25. The thief used the service elevator at ~9:20. The thief needed to be IN the gallery, cut the painting, and reach the service elevator — all within 10 minutes.

**Clue 2:** Kessler's key card shows he entered Room 204 at 9:28 PM. Room 204 is on the second floor. If Kessler committed the theft (gallery at 9:15-9:20, elevator at 9:20, basement by 9:22), he could not have returned to Room 204 by 9:28 PM via the main staircase from the basement — the basement stairs go to the kitchen (1F), not directly to 2F. He would need the service elevator again (but it was going DOWN at 9:20) or the main staircase (1F to 2F, 3-minute walk). Tight but possible.

**Clue 3:** Fontaine was seen on the THIRD floor at 9:10. The gallery is on the second floor. He could descend one flight in under a minute. He had time.

**Clue 4:** Delacroix was ON the second floor (left gallery at 9:00). She was closest to the gallery.

**Clue 5:** Ashworth's gap begins at 9:15 — exactly when the attendant left. She was coming from the FIRST floor (restaurant/bar area). The main staircase from 1F to 2F takes approximately 2 minutes. She could reach the gallery by 9:17 at earliest.

**The question is not who COULD have done it — all four had opportunity. The question is whose alibi, even when contradicted, is still consistent with being ELSEWHERE during 9:15-9:25.**

Consider Kessler: his alibi gap is 66 minutes (8:22-9:28). But his key card proves he was in Room 204 at 9:28. If he committed the theft at 9:20 and went DOWN via elevator to the basement, how did he get back to Room 204 at 9:28? He would have had to: exit the basement, go up to 1F via kitchen stairs, climb main staircase to 2F, enter room — in 8 minutes. Possible but it means he went DOWN (theft) then immediately UP (back to room). The thief exited via the basement alley. Kessler did NOT exit — he went to his room. **Kessler's movements are inconsistent with the thief's escape route.**

This means: the three suspects whose gaps are consistent with the FULL escape route (gallery → elevator → basement → alley) are Ashworth, Fontaine, and Delacroix. Kessler's return to his room at 9:28 eliminates him — the thief left the building.
