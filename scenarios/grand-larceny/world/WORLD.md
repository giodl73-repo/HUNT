# The Grand Hotel — World Overview

**Genre/Aesthetic:** Art Deco noir, 1947 period feel. Polished marble, brass fixtures, velvet curtains. A hotel that remembers a grander era.

**Hook:** A priceless painting was stolen during last night's gala. You are the investigative unit. The evidence is your puzzle.

**Core systems:**
1. **Hotel layout** — rooms, corridors, service passages, the gallery where the painting hung
2. **Staff & guests** — names, roles, alibis, relationships, motives
3. **Gala timeline** — minute-by-minute schedule of the evening's events
4. **Hotel services** — room charges, restaurant orders, phone calls, key card logs

**Meta target:** THE METHOD — how the painting was physically removed from the hotel

## Tone

Noir procedural. Documents are clinical. The police report is bureaucratic. The receipt is accounting. The floor plan is architectural. The witness statement is transcribed speech -- halting, uncertain, full of verbal tics. Nothing winks at the solver. The fiction is played straight.

## Canon Rules

- The Grand Hotel is at 1 Grand Avenue, in an unnamed city
- The gala took place on the evening of Saturday, October 11, 1947
- The stolen painting is "Lady in Vermillion" by Henri Renaud (1889), oil on canvas, 36x48 inches
- The painting hung in the Sargent Gallery on the second floor
- There are exactly four suspects, each with a documented alibi
- The hotel has exactly three floors plus a basement level
- All staff names, guest names, room numbers, and timeline events are canonical
- No fact in any puzzle document contradicts another puzzle document
- The thief is not identified by the puzzles — the METHOD is the meta answer
