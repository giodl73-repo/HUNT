# Characters — The Grand Hotel

## The Four Suspects

Each suspect attended the gala, had access to the second floor, and had a plausible motive. Their alibis overlap and contradict in ways that form the logic puzzle (P1).

### 1. Mrs. Vivian Ashworth — Room 202

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Age** | 58 |
| **Occupation** | Art collector, philanthropist |
| **Relation to painting** | Bid on "Lady in Vermillion" at auction in 1939; outbid by the hotel's original owner |
| **Alibi** | Claims she was in the restaurant (101) from 7:00 to 9:15, then the bar (102) until 10:00 |
| **Contradiction** | The bartender says she arrived at the bar at 9:45, not 9:15. 30 unaccounted minutes. |
| **Key trait** | Formal, precise, old money. Uses "one" instead of "I." |

### 2. Mr. Lucien Fontaine — Room 203

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Age** | 34 |
| **Occupation** | Art dealer, gallery owner |
| **Relation to painting** | Recently appraised the painting at $40,000 for the hotel's insurance. Had private access to examine it. |
| **Alibi** | Says he was in the reading room (201) from 8:30 to 10:00, reading. Alone. |
| **Contradiction** | The maid saw him on the third floor at 9:10 PM. He says he never went above the second floor. |
| **Key trait** | Smooth, confident, slightly oily. European affectation. |

### 3. Mr. Heinrich Kessler — Room 204

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Age** | 47 |
| **Occupation** | Import/export businessman |
| **Relation to painting** | No known connection to art. But his business partner owes $50,000 to a collector who specifically wants a Renaud. |
| **Alibi** | Claims he was in the telephone room (105) from 8:00 to 9:30 making long-distance calls, then went to his room. |
| **Contradiction** | The telephone operator logged only one call: 8:15 to 8:22. Seven minutes, not ninety. |
| **Key trait** | Brusque, impatient, accent. Sweats when questioned. |

### 4. Miss Colette Delacroix — Room 205

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Age** | 26 |
| **Occupation** | Painter, art student |
| **Relation to painting** | Claims to be studying Renaud's technique. Requested (and was denied) permission to set up an easel in the gallery. |
| **Alibi** | Says she was at the gala dinner (101) until 8:45, then went to view the painting in the gallery (200) until 9:30, then to her room. |
| **Contradiction** | The gallery attendant confirms she was there — but says she left at 9:00, not 9:30. Where was she for 30 minutes? |
| **Key trait** | Earnest, nervous, fidgets. Young and out of place at this hotel. |

## Alibi Matrix (for P1 logic puzzle)

| Time window | Ashworth | Fontaine | Kessler | Delacroix |
|-------------|----------|----------|---------|-----------|
| 7:00-8:00 | Restaurant (101) | Gallery (200) | Lobby (100) | Restaurant (101) |
| 8:00-8:30 | Restaurant (101) | Gallery (200) | Telephone (105) | Restaurant (101) |
| 8:30-9:00 | Restaurant (101) | Reading room (201) | Telephone (105)* | Gallery (200) |
| 9:00-9:15 | ? (gap) | Reading room (201)** | Room 204 | Gallery (200)*** |
| 9:15-9:30 | ? (gap) | Reading room (201)** | Room 204 | ? (gap) |
| 9:30-10:00 | Bar (102)*** | Reading room (201) | Room 204 | Room 205 |

- `*` Kessler claims telephone until 9:30, but phone log shows call ended 8:22. He has an 68-minute gap (8:22 to 9:30 arrival in room).
- `**` Fontaine claims reading room all evening, but maid saw him on 3F at 9:10.
- `***` Ashworth claims bar at 9:15, bartender says 9:45. 30-minute gap.
- `***` Delacroix claims gallery until 9:30, attendant says 9:00. 30-minute gap.

**Key deduction:** The painting was stolen between 9:15 (attendant leaves) and 9:25 (attendant returns). All four suspects have unaccounted time that overlaps this window. The logic puzzle determines which suspect's gap CANNOT overlap the theft window, eliminating three and identifying the one whose movements are consistent with the theft.

The answer to P1 is SUSPECT — but the encoded answer word is the name-derived extraction, not a suspect's name.
