ENVIRONMENTAL AUTOMATION — OPERATOR REFERENCE

  AUTOMATION ONLINE  (◉ ONLINE):
    Chemistry subsystems regulate atmosphere continuously.
    Any drift from setpoint triggers automatic correction within 90 seconds.
    Readings should not deviate from nominal by more than ±0.05% (gases)
    or ±0.5°C (temperature) during normal operations.

  AUTOMATION STANDBY (◌ STANDBY):
    Chemistry subsystems idle. Passive gas exchange continues.
    Setpoint regulation PAUSED — corrections do not occur.
    Atmosphere drifts freely toward equilibrium with any active sources.
    Instant wake on RESUME command. Previous setpoints retained.

  WHEN STANDBY CAUSES DRIFT:
    If a zone's atmosphere drifts in STANDBY, the drift reflects
    whatever was ACTUALLY OCCURRING in that zone without correction.
    Elevated O₂: additional oxygen source present (increased respiration,
      O₂-enrichment equipment, oxidative processes).
    Elevated CO₂: elevated metabolic output (exertion, chemical reaction,
      thermal decomposition).
    Elevated temperature: heat source present (biological, electrical,
      chemical).
