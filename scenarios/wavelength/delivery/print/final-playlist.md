## Design Notes for This Prop

### What solvers hold in their hands

This is the last playlist Vee ever wrote. She filled out this card in the WVLG broadcast booth before going on air for the final time. The songs are real. The intervals are real. The numbers are hers.

It is a working document. There are song titles, dotted leaders to artist names, numbers scrawled in the margins. It is not polished or designed -- it is written. The felt-tip pen pressed harder on some lines than others.

### Emotional weight

The closing quote -- "The signal was always there. You just had to listen." -- is Vee's goodbye. Solvers do not see this quote in context until they hold the card. It sits at the bottom, below the extraction blanks, waiting. When the solver fills in the diagonal and reads FADING, the quote becomes an answer to the answer: the signal was fading, yes, but it was always there.

This is the moment the hunt stops being a puzzle and becomes something else.

### What this card reveals (and does not reveal)

**Reveals:**
- Song titles and artists for Sets 1, 4, and 5 (these are also in the website puzzles)
- The interval descriptions for Set 2 (echoing the puzzle)
- The existence of 7 albums in Set 3 (but not their names)
- The existence of 6 bands in Set 6 (but not the definitions or the band names)
- The meta extraction mechanism (diagonal read, carets marking positions)

**Does NOT reveal:**
- Any puzzle answers (the blanks are empty)
- Which albums are described in Set 3
- Which bands are clued in Set 6
- The definitions or mechanisms for identifying the bands
- The hidden words in Set 5's song titles
- Vee's numbers are not labeled as A=1 Z=26

### Margin notes

Two margin notes appear in lighter brown, as if Vee scrawled them as afterthoughts:
- Next to Set 2: "read the notes" -- a subtle hint that the musical notes ARE letters
- Next to Set 6: "just words" -- echoing her puzzle intro, hinting at the double meaning

These are flavor. They are not necessary for solving but reward careful reading of the prop.
