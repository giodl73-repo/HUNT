# Set 1 — Side A

*"I played these songs in this order for a reason. I always did."*

---

Vee's playlist card for Set 1 lists six albums she played tonight. Each entry names the album and the track number she chose from it. But the album names have been smudged — only the track numbers survive.

Your job: identify each album from its description, find the indicated track on that album, and take the first letter of the track title.

---

## The Set List

**1. Track 12**
This album came out in autumn of 1992. A band from Athens, Georgia. The cover is a star against a cream-colored sky. The album's title references a local restaurant's slogan. Songs about Andy Kaufman, insomnia, and things that swim after dark. The band's lead singer rarely faced the audience in early shows.

**2. Track 8**
Released March 1973. One continuous suite of music — ten tracks flowing into each other. A prism on the cover. The band formed in London in 1965, originally led by a founding member who departed due to mental illness. This album spent over 900 weeks on the Billboard 200. Side two opens with the sound of cash registers.

**3. Track 10**
June 1983. The final studio album by a trio — bass, guitar, drums. Named after a concept from Carl Jung. The album's biggest single dominated the airwaves for eight consecutive weeks at the top of the Hot 100 that summer. Side two ends with a song about desert tea ceremonies.

**4. Track 1**
December 1976. A band that took its name from a place in the American West. Five musicians, three guitars. The title track is over six minutes long, opens with a twelve-string guitar arpeggio, and describes a mysterious lodging. The album cover shows the Beverly Hills Hotel at sunset.

**5. Track 10**
February 1977. Recorded during the disintegration of two marriages within the band. Eleven songs. "Dreams" reached number one. The bassist and drummer shared a surname. The cover photograph shows Mick Fleetwood holding crystal balls beside Stevie Nicks.

**6. Track 2**
May 1985. A British band led by a guitarist known for fingerpicking through a clean Stratocaster tone. The album was one of the first major releases recorded digitally. Its second track features an animated music video — a construction worker and a guitarist in a department store. That track reached number one in the US.
