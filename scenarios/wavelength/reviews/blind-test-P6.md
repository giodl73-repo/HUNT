## P6 — Name That Band (Lucas Pope)

### Thomas Snyder
**Solve attempt:**

Reading the six definitions:
1. Largest continent -> ASIA (4 letters). Letter 1 = A.
2. A mystery, Churchill quote -> ENIGMA (6 letters). Letter 2 = N.
3. Organ that pumps blood -> HEART (5 letters). Letter 3 = A.
4. Large birds of prey, bald variety -> EAGLES (6 letters). Letter 4 = L.
5. Port city in Massachusetts, tea, harbor -> BOSTON (6 letters). Letter 5 = O.
6. City on Lake Michigan, deep-dish pizza -> CHICAGO (7 letters). Letter 6 = G.

Wait — these are all band names. ASIA is a band. ENIGMA is a band (well, a music project). HEART, EAGLES, BOSTON, CHICAGO — all bands.

Extracted letters: A-N-A-L-O-G.

**Answer:** ANALOG

**Time:** ~8 minutes

### Wei-Hwa Huang
**Solve attempt:**

Definitions are straightforward. ASIA, ENIGMA, HEART, EAGLES, BOSTON, CHICAGO. All correct.

Noted the deduction potential: Definition 1 could be ASIA or EUROPE. Both are bands. ASIA(1)=A, EUROPE(1)=E. Testing both: A or E as first letter. The final answer ANALOG starts with A, so ASIA is correct. A solver who tried EUROPE first would get E-N-A-L-O-G = "ENALOG" — not a word. This forces a retry with ASIA.

**Answer:** ANALOG

**Issues:**
- Minor: The puzzle could benefit from one more ambiguous clue beyond ASIA/EUROPE. Definition 4 (large birds of prey) could theoretically be HAWKS, but hawks are not eagles. The "bald variety" clinches EAGLES. This is good clue design — just one ambiguity point, resolved by the extraction.
- Minor: ENIGMA is less famous as a band than ASIA, HEART, EAGLES, BOSTON, or CHICAGO. Some solvers might not know Enigma was a music project. But the puzzle doesn't require this knowledge — the band-name connection is a bonus aha, not a required step.
