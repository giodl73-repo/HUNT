**Dex (Veteran -- 6 minutes):**

Answer: FACADE.

**P2 sticking points:**
- Rae's slow counting is the intended difficulty. The chromatic scale chart makes it solvable.
- No solver was confused by the mechanism after reading the instructions
- The "Wait. Read those target notes as letters." line in the workspace is an effective nudge

**P2 verdict: PASS.** Beautiful aha moment. The double payoff (notes-are-letters AND the answer is spelled with notes) lands for all three.

### P3 -- Deep Cuts (Album Identification)

**Marcus (Music fan -- 15 minutes):**
Gets all 7 immediately. "Closer, Automatic for the People, Disintegration, Electric Ladyland, Nevermind, Crime of the Century, Eliminator." Takes first letters: C-A-D-E-N-C-E. "CADENCE. A musical term from a non-musical mechanism. Nice."

Uses music references to fill gaps. Gets the answer eventually.
Answer: CADENCE.

**Dex (Veteran -- 22 minutes):**
Identifies I (Joy Division, from "Manchester" + "founding member departed due to mental illness" + "remaining members formed a different band"), II (R.E.M.), V (Nirvana), VII (ZZ Top). Works III from "Crawley, England" + "#2 on Hot 100" + "single word suggesting dissolution" = Disintegration. **P3 verdict: PASS.** Good deduction puzzle. Description VI could use one more accessible clue (e.g., mention "Breakfast in America" as a later album by the same band) in a polish pass. Flag for Stage 11.

### P5 -- Between the Lines (Hidden Words in Song Titles)

**Marcus (Music fan -- 22 minutes):**
Reads the instructions. Understands hidden words from the example ("Every Breath You Take" -> VERY). Starts scanning:
1. "Another One Bites the Dust" -- takes a while. Tries HERO, then notices HERON spanning ANOTHER/ONE. H.
2. "Total Eclipse of the Heart" -- finds ALE in TOTAL/ECLIPSE quickly. A.

**Rae (Casual -- 28 minutes):**
Gets ALE quickly (entry 2). Gets ERAS (entry 4) after some scanning. Struggles with HERON (entry 1) -- finds "HERO" first but it doesn't cross a word boundary in the right way. **Dex (Veteran -- 15 minutes):**

**P5 verdict: PASS.** Strong puzzle. The apostrophe ambiguity is a minor polish item for Stage 11.
