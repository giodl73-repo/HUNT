# Artists Reference

Real-world artists used in WAVELENGTH puzzles. All data verifiable via AllMusic, Discogs, or Wikipedia.

## Core Artists (used in multiple puzzles)

### Fleetwood Mac
- **Active:** 1967-present (hiatus periods)
- **Genre:** Rock, soft rock, pop rock
- **Key members (classic lineup):** Mick Fleetwood (drums), John McVie (bass), Christine McVie (keyboards/vocals), Lindsey Buckingham (guitar/vocals), Stevie Nicks (vocals)
- **Key albums:** Fleetwood Mac (1975), Rumours (1977), Tusk (1979), Mirage (1982), Tango in the Night (1987)
- **Chart facts:** "Dreams" hit #1 on Billboard Hot 100 (1977) — their only #1 single. Rumours spent 31 weeks at #1 on Billboard 200.

### The Police
- **Active:** 1977-1986, 2007-2008 (reunion)
- **Genre:** New wave, post-punk, reggae rock
- **Members:** Sting (bass/vocals), Andy Summers (guitar), Stewart Copeland (drums)
- **Key albums:** Outlandos d'Amour (1978), Reggatta de Blanc (1979), Zenyatta Mondatta (1980), Ghost in the Machine (1981), Synchronicity (1983)
- **Chart facts:** "Every Breath You Take" — #1 for 8 weeks (1983). Synchronicity — #1 on Billboard 200 for 17 weeks.

### Eagles
- **Active:** 1971-1980, 1994-2016
- **Genre:** Rock, country rock, soft rock
- **Key members:** Glenn Frey, Don Henley, Joe Walsh, Timothy B. Schmit, Don Felder, Bernie Leadon, Randy Meisner
- **Key albums:** Eagles (1972), Desperado (1973), On the Border (1974), One of These Nights (1975), Hotel California (1976), The Long Run (1979)
- **Chart facts:** "Hotel California" peaked at #1 (1977). Their Greatest Hits (1971-1975) — first album certified platinum by RIAA, later certified 38x platinum.

### Pink Floyd
- **Active:** 1965-1995, 2005, 2012-2014
- **Genre:** Progressive rock, psychedelic rock, art rock
- **Key members:** Roger Waters (bass/vocals), David Gilmour (guitar/vocals), Nick Mason (drums), Richard Wright (keyboards), Syd Barrett (early era)
- **Key albums:** The Piper at the Gates of Dawn (1967), Meddle (1971), The Dark Side of the Moon (1973), Wish You Were Here (1975), Animals (1977), The Wall (1979)
- **Chart facts:** The Dark Side of the Moon — 937 weeks on Billboard 200. "Another Brick in the Wall (Part II)" — #1 (1980).

### Queen
- **Active:** 1970-present
- **Genre:** Rock, glam rock, progressive rock, arena rock
- **Members:** Freddie Mercury (vocals/piano), Brian May (guitar), Roger Taylor (drums), John Deacon (bass)
- **Key albums:** Queen (1973), Sheer Heart Attack (1974), A Night at the Opera (1975), News of the World (1977), The Game (1980), A Kind of Magic (1986)
- **Chart facts:** "Crazy Little Thing Called Love" — #1 (1980). "Another One Bites the Dust" — #1 (1980). "Bohemian Rhapsody" peaked at #9 (1976), re-entered at #2 (1992).

### Led Zeppelin
- **Active:** 1968-1980
- **Genre:** Hard rock, blues rock, folk rock
- **Members:** Robert Plant (vocals), Jimmy Page (guitar), John Paul Jones (bass/keyboards), John Bonham (drums)
- **Key albums:** Led Zeppelin (1969), Led Zeppelin II (1969), Led Zeppelin III (1970), Led Zeppelin IV (1971), Houses of the Holy (1973), Physical Graffiti (1975)
- **Chart facts:** Never released singles in the UK. "Stairway to Heaven" was never released as a single. Led Zeppelin IV has sold 23+ million copies in the US.

### Talking Heads
- **Active:** 1975-1991
- **Genre:** New wave, post-punk, art rock
- **Members:** David Byrne (vocals/guitar), Chris Frantz (drums), Tina Weymouth (bass), Jerry Harrison (keyboards/guitar)
- **Key albums:** Talking Heads: 77 (1977), More Songs About Buildings and Food (1978), Fear of Music (1979), Remain in Light (1980), Speaking in Tongues (1983), Little Creatures (1985)
- **Chart facts:** "Burning Down the House" — #9 (1983). "And She Was" — #54 (1985).

### R.E.M.
- **Active:** 1980-2011
- **Genre:** Alternative rock, jangle pop, college rock
- **Members:** Michael Stipe (vocals), Peter Buck (guitar), Mike Mills (bass), Bill Berry (drums, left 1997)
- **Key albums:** Murmur (1983), Reckoning (1984), Document (1987), Green (1988), Out of Time (1991), Automatic for the People (1992)
- **Chart facts:** "Losing My Religion" — #4 (1991). "Shiny Happy People" — #10 (1991). Out of Time — #1 on Billboard 200.

## Supporting Artists (used in 1-2 puzzles)

### The Clash
- **Active:** 1976-1986
- **Genre:** Punk rock, post-punk, new wave
- **Key album:** London Calling (1979) — 19 tracks across 2 LPs

### Dire Straits
- **Active:** 1977-1995
- **Genre:** Rock, roots rock
- **Key album:** Brothers in Arms (1985). "Money for Nothing" — #1 (1985).

### The Smiths
- **Active:** 1982-1987
- **Genre:** Indie rock, post-punk, jangle pop
- **Key album:** The Queen Is Dead (1986)

### U2
- **Active:** 1976-present
- **Genre:** Rock, post-punk, alternative rock
- **Key album:** The Joshua Tree (1987). "With or Without You" — #1 (1987).

### Blondie
- **Active:** 1974-1982, 1997-present
- **Genre:** New wave, punk rock, disco
- **Key facts:** "Heart of Glass" — #1 (1979). "Call Me" — #1 for 6 weeks (1980).

### The Cure
- **Active:** 1976-present
- **Genre:** Gothic rock, new wave, post-punk, alternative rock
- **Key album:** Disintegration (1989). "Lovesong" — #2 (1989).

### David Bowie
- **Active:** 1962-2016
- **Genre:** Glam rock, art rock, new wave
- **Key albums:** The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust (1972), Aladdin Sane (1973), "Heroes" (1977), Let's Dance (1983)
- **Chart facts:** "Let's Dance" — #1 (1983).

### Bruce Springsteen
- **Active:** 1964-present
- **Genre:** Rock, heartland rock
- **Key album:** Born in the U.S.A. (1984) — 7 singles in the top 10. "Dancing in the Dark" — #2 (1984).

### Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers
- **Active:** 1976-2017
- **Genre:** Rock, heartland rock
- **Key facts:** "Free Fallin'" — #7 (1989). "Don't Come Around Here No More" — #13 (1985).

### Depeche Mode
- **Active:** 1980-present
- **Genre:** Synth-pop, new wave, electronic
- **Key album:** Violator (1990). "Enjoy the Silence" — #8 (1990). "Personal Jesus" — #28 (1989).
