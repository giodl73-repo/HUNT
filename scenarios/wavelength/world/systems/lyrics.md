## Lyrics with Hidden Words

Songs where English words are hidden across word boundaries in the lyrics. These are the basis for P5 (the lyrics puzzle).

### "Hotel California" — Eagles (1976)
> "On a dark desert highway, cool wind in my hair"

Hidden: WIN (in "wind in")

### "Bohemian Rhapsody" — Queen (1975)
> "Is this the real life? Is this just fantasy?"

Hidden: HIS (in "this the"), ANT (in "fantasy")

### "Every Breath You Take" — The Police (1983)
> "Every breath you take, every move you make"

Hidden: OVER (in "every move" — read backwards: "evom yrevE")
Note: Forward hidden words are preferred for solver fairness.

### "Stairway to Heaven" — Led Zeppelin (1971)
> "There's a lady who's sure all that glitters is gold"

Hidden: LITTER (in "glitters")

### "Dreams" — Fleetwood Mac (1977)
> "Now here you go again, you say you want your freedom"

Hidden: HERE (in "Now here"), ANT (in "want")

### "Message in a Bottle" — The Police (1979)
> "Just a castaway, an island lost at sea"

Hidden: CAST (in "castaway"), SAND (embedded)

### "Don't Stop Believin'" — Journey (1981)
> "Just a small town girl, livin' in a lonely world"

Hidden: MALL (in "small"), OWN (in "town"), ONE (in "lonely")

### "Losing My Religion" — R.E.M. (1991)
> "That's me in the corner, that's me in the spotlight"

Hidden: THE (in "the corner"), POT (in "spotlight")

### "Under Pressure" — Queen & David Bowie (1981)
> "Pressing down on me, pressing down on you"

Hidden: SING (in "pressing")

### "Money" — Pink Floyd (1973)
> "Money, get away, get a good job with more pay and you're okay"

Hidden: OKRA (not found — strike)
Hidden: PAY (in "pay and")

### "Roxanne" — The Police (1978)
> "Roxanne, you don't have to put on the red light"

Hidden: ANNE (in "Roxanne"), RED (in "red light")

### "Wish You Were Here" — Pink Floyd (1975)
> "How I wish, how I wish you were here"

Hidden: WISH (in "wish you"), HER (in "here")

## Lyric Excerpt Selection Rules

For puzzle use:
1. Keep excerpts to 1-2 lines (fair use)
2. Cite song title and artist
3. Use only well-known, easily verifiable lyrics
4. Hidden words must be clearly present in the text — no ambiguous letter boundaries
5. The solver should be able to verify the lyric independently
