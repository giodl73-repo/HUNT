# WVLG — Station Lore

**Call sign:** WVLG (92.7 FM)
**Location:** Fictional small city, Pacific Northwest
**Format:** Album-oriented rock / eclectic (1983-2023)
**Founded:** 1983
**Final broadcast:** 2023

---

## The DJ — Vee

**Full name:** Never disclosed on air. Staff called her Vee.
**On air:** 1983-2023 (40 years, the station's entire life)
**Shift:** The midnight slot — "The Long Wave" (midnight to 4 AM)
**Voice:** Low, unhurried. Known for long pauses between tracks. Never raised her voice. Never used a jingle.
**Signature habit:** Handwritten playlists. She refused to use the digital logging system introduced in 2004. Every set list is a paper card filed in a milk crate behind the console.
**The secret:** Vee has been encoding messages in her playlists since the first broadcast. The messages are hidden in the music data itself — track numbers, chart positions, artist names, note values. Nobody noticed for 40 years. The final broadcast is the key.

## Station History

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1983 | WVLG signs on. First song: "Radio Ga Ga" by Queen (February 1984 single, played before official release as a statement of intent). |
| 1985 | Becomes the only local station to play full album sides during drive time. |
| 1990 | Survives format change pressure. Stays album-oriented while competitors switch to Top 40. |
| 1995 | Peak listenership. The midnight slot "The Long Wave" becomes a cult favorite. |
| 2004 | Digital logging system installed. Vee refuses to use it. |
| 2010 | Ownership changes. Budget cuts. Staff shrinks from 8 to 3. |
| 2018 | Streaming competition. Listenership drops below commercial viability. |
| 2023 | Final broadcast announced. Vee's last show is the station's last show. |

## The Final Broadcast

Vee plays 6 sets of songs, each set curated around a theme. Between sets, she speaks briefly — one sentence per break, no more. The 6 sets are the 6 puzzles. The final playlist card — the physical prop — lists all songs played that night.

After the last song, Vee says one final sentence and the transmitter goes dark.

## Station Aesthetic

- Felt-tip pen on index cards
- Milk crates of vinyl and CDs
- A single desk lamp in the booth
- The ON AIR sign, always slightly buzzing
- Coffee rings on every surface
- Handwritten notes taped to the console: "Side B, track 3. Trust me."
